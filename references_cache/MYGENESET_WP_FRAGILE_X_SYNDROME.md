## Description

WikiPathways Fragile X syndrome gene set (MSigDB C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS, 121 genes). Fragile X syndrome (FXS) is the most common inherited cause of intellectual disability and a leading monogenic cause of autism spectrum disorder, arising from CGG trinucleotide repeat expansion in FMR1 that silences FMRP (fragile X mental retardation protein). FMRP is a selective RNA-binding protein that represses local translation of hundreds of target mRNAs at synapses and regulates dendritic mRNA transport in response to metabotropic glutamate receptor activation. Loss of FMRP leads to excess and dysregulated synaptic protein synthesis, dendritic spine dysmorphology, and impaired activity-dependent synaptic plasticity. The gene set captures the major mechanistic arms: translational repression at synapses, chemical synaptic transmission, dendritic spine structure, mRNA transport to dendrites, and synaptic plasticity.
