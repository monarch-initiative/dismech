In the newborn, there exists a wide spectrum of pustular skin diseases. These 
range from transitory, benign adaptation disorders up to systemic, life 
threatening illnesses. In 30-60% of newborns pustules are observed in 
association with the relatively harmless Erythema toxicum, the origin of which 
is still unknown today. It is necessary to differentiate this from the pustular 
diseases which may be of infectious or non-infectious nature and which require 
therapy. Typical pathogens include Malassezia furfur, Staphylococci, 
Streptococci, Candida spp. and the herpes virus group.
