# Peutz-Jeghers syndrome: diagnostic and therapeutic approach

**Journal:** World J Gastroenterol (2009)
**DOI:** [10.3748/wjg.15.5397](https://doi.org/10.3748/wjg.15.5397)

## Content

Peutz-Jeghers syndrome (PJS) is an inherited, autosomal dominant disorder distinguished by hamartomatous polyps in the gastrointestinal tract and pigmented mucocutaneous lesions. Prevalence of PJS is estimated from 1 in 8300 to 1 in 280,000 individuals.

PJS predisposes sufferers to various malignancies (gastrointestinal, pancreatic, lung, breast, uterine, ovarian and testicular tumors). Bleeding, obstruction and intussusception are common complications in patients with PJS.
