Encephalocraniocutaneous lipomatosis (ECCL) is a rare neurocutaneous disorder 
caused by somatic FGFR1 and KRAS variants. It shares significant phenotypic 
overlap with several closely related disorders caused by mutations in the 
RAS-MAPK pathway (mosaic RASopathies). We report a diagnostically challenging 
case of ECCL in which next-generation sequencing of affected tissue identified a 
pathologic FGFR1 p.K656E variant, thereby establishing a molecular diagnosis. 
Patients with FGFR1-associated ECCL carry a risk of developing malignant brain 
tumors; thus, genetic testing of patients with suspected ECCL has important 
management implications.
