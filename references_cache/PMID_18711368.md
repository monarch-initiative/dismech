# tRNA splicing endonuclease mutations cause pontocerebellar hypoplasia.

Pontocerebellar hypoplasias (PCH) represent a group of neurodegenerative
autosomal recessive disorders with prenatal onset, atrophy or hypoplasia of the
cerebellum, hypoplasia of the ventral pons, microcephaly, variable neocortical
atrophy and severe mental and motor impairments. In two subtypes, PCH2 and PCH4,
we identified mutations in three of the four different subunits of the
tRNA-splicing endonuclease complex. Our findings point to RNA processing as a
new basic cellular impairment in neurological disorders.
