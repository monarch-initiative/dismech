# Natural history of acute HCV infection in hemodialysis patients.
**Authors:** Espinosa M, Martin-Malo A, Alvarez de Lara MA, Gonzalez R, Rodriguez M, Aljama P
**Journal:** Clin Nephrol (2002)
**DOI:** [10.5414/cnp58143](https://doi.org/10.5414/cnp58143)

## Content

1. Clin Nephrol. 2002 Aug;58(2):143-50. doi: 10.5414/cnp58143.

Natural history of acute HCV infection in hemodialysis patients.

Espinosa M(1), Martin-Malo A, Alvarez de Lara MA, Gonzalez R, Rodriguez M,
Aljama P.

Author information:
(1)Servicios de Nefrologia, Hospital Universitario Reina Sofia, Cordoba, Spain.
espinosahe@supercable.com

AIMS: Chronic liver disease develops in the majority of non-uremic patients with
hepatitis C virus (HCV) infection. The aim of this study was to analyze the
evolution towards chronic hepatopathy in 19 cases of acute hepatitis C observed
in hemodialysis patients from 1990 to 2001.
METHODS: A prospective follow-up study on HCV infection was conducted in 3 HD
units from April 1990 to June 2001 to study clinical outcomes after acute
hepatitis C. A total of 781 patients were tested monthly for alanine
aminotransferase and anti-HCV in serum. In this period, 19 patients suffered
from acute hepatitis C. Evolution to chronic liver disease in the follow-up was
evaluated by means of biochemical (increased ALT) and virological criteria
(HCV-RNA+). The transmission mechanism, the apparition of anti-HCV, clinical
manifestations and mortality were also investigated.
RESULTS: In 15 (78.9%) of the 19 patients, the viremia remained positive
(chronic viremia) and 11 patients (57.8%) evolved to chronic liver disease
(chronic viremia and high transaminase levels) with a median follow-up of 3
years (range 1 - 6). Five of them who underwent liver biopsies had histologic
signs of chronic active hepatitis. One of them (5.2%) evolved to liver cirrhosis
in the follow-up. In 4 out of 19 patients (21%) the HCV infection resolved.
Although 7 (36.8%) of them died in the follow-up, acute hepatitis C infection
was not a short-term independent risk factor of death.
CONCLUSIONS: Three years after acute hepatitis C, 87.5% of the hemodialysis
patients remained HCV-RNA positive and 56.2% evolved to chronic liver disease.
It is important to stress that HCV infection spontaneously cleared in 4 out of
19 patients (21%).
