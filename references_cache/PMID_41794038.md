Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors (pNETs) exhibit substantial clinical and 
molecular heterogeneity. Using bulk and single-nucleus RNA sequencing, we 
identify five molecular subtypes: Hedgehog-high, Alpha-like, Hypoxia-high, 
Gastrin-high, and Progenitor-like. The Gastrin-high and Progenitor-like subtypes 
associate with poor clinical outcomes. BEND2 gene fusions occur in 5% of pNETs, 
all belonging to the Gastrin-high subtype, which shows activation of the late 
endocrine progenitor FEV regulon. Functional studies in pNET cell models 
demonstrate that BEND2 fusions drive transcriptional reprogramming, promoting a 
shift from ASCL1+ endocrine states toward neurodevelopmental, mesenchymal, and 
immune-related gene programs. Single-nucleus analysis reveals complex 
multicellular ecosystems, with NOTCH3-mediated signaling between tumor cells and 
myofibroblasts emerging as a potential therapeutic vulnerability. Gastrin-high 
tumors exhibit CD8+ T cell infiltration alongside PD-1/PD-L1 upregulation, 
suggesting potential responsiveness to immune checkpoint blockade. These 
findings define a molecular taxonomy of pNETs and nominate tumor-intrinsic and 
microenvironmental programs as actionable targets.
