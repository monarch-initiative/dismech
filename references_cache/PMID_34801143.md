Hydrocephalus, the abnormal accumulation and impaired circulation/clearance of 
cerebrospinal fluid, occurs as a common phenotypic feature of a diverse group of 
genetic syndromes. In this review, we outline the genetic mutations, 
pathogenesis, and accompanying symptoms underlying syndromic hydrocephalus in 
the context of: L1 syndrome, syndromic craniosynostoses, achondroplasia, NF 1/2, 
Down's syndrome, tuberous sclerosis, Walker-Warburg syndrome, primary ciliary 
dyskinesia, and osteogenesis imperfecta. Further, we discuss emerging genetic 
variants associated with syndromic hydrocephalus.
