# GTPase-inhibiting mutations activate the alpha-chain of Gs in human tumours.
**Authors:** Vallar L
**Journal:** Biochem Soc Symp (1990)

## Content

1. Biochem Soc Symp. 1990;56:165-70.

GTPase-inhibiting mutations activate the alpha-chain of Gs in human tumours.

Vallar L(1).

Author information:
(1)Department of Pharmacology, C.N.R. Center of Cytopharmacology, Scientific
Institute San Raffaele, University of Milan, Italy.

Several trophic hormones transmit their signals by coupling to G-proteins. A
subset of human growth hormone (GH)-secreting pituitary tumours, characterized
by elevated GH secretion, cyclic AMP levels, and adenylyl cyclase activity,
carries mutations in the gene that encodes the alpha-chain of Gs. By
substitution of a single amino acid (Arg-201 or Gln-227), these mutations
inhibit the intrinsic GTPase activity of alpha s, thus stabilizing the protein
in its active conformation. The discovery of mutant alpha s proteins in human
tumours suggests that the alpha s gene can be converted into an oncogene, called
gsp, in cells that proliferate in response to cyclic AMP. Most likely, oncogenes
can result from similar mutations in other G-protein alpha-chain genes.
