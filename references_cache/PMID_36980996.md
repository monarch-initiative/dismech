Multiple synostoses syndrome (OMIM: #186500, #610017, #612961, #617898) is a 
genetically heterogeneous group of autosomal dominant diseases characterized by 
abnormal bone unions. The joint fusions frequently involve the hands, feet, 
elbows or vertebrae. Pathogenic variants in FGF9 have been associated with 
multiple synostoses syndrome type 3 (SYNS3). So far, only five different 
missense variants in FGF9 that cause SYNS3 have been reported in 18 affected 
individuals. Unlike other multiple synostoses syndromes, conductive hearing loss 
has not been reported in SYNS3. In this report, we describe the clinical and 
selected radiological findings in a large multigenerational family with a novel 
missense variant in FGF9: c.430T>C, p.(Trp144Arg). We extend the phenotypic 
spectrum of SYNS3 by suggesting that cleft palate and conductive hearing loss 
are part of the syndrome and highlight the high degree of intrafamilial 
phenotypic variability. These findings should be considered when counseling 
affected individuals.
