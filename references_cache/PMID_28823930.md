Recent work has demonstrated the importance of miRNAs in the pathogenesis of 
various brain disorders including the neurodegenerative disorder spinocerebellar 
ataxia (SCA). This review focuses on the role of miRNAs in the shared 
pathogenesis of the different SCA types. We examine the novel findings of a 
recent cell-type-specific RNA-sequencing study in mouse brain and discuss how 
the identification of Purkinje-cell-enriched miRNAs highlights biological 
pathways that expose the mechanisms behind pervasive Purkinje cell degeneration 
in SCA. These key pathways are likely to contain targets for therapeutic 
development and represent potential candidate genes for genetically unsolved 
SCAs.
