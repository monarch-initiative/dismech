Campomelic dysplasia is a rare hereditary congenital osteochondral dysplasia 
characterized by abnormal bowing of the lower limbs, sex reversal in males, and 
other skeletal and extraskeletal abnormalities. It is usually fatal in the 
neonatal period because of respiratory insufficiency. The diagnosis is usually 
difficult because of its rare presentation and the prognosis is poor. We present 
such a case in a 1-month-old child with typical skeletal abnormalities, whose 
presentation was unusual because of later presentation of respiratory distress 
and lack of genitourinary abnormalities.
