Isolated orbital periphlebitis in the context of nonspecific orbital 
inflammation is rare. This case series describes 3 patients with periphlebitis 
of the superior ophthalmic vein, 2 of which were confirmed with pathology. All 3 
cases had a history of Graves hyperthyroidism. Superior ophthalmic vein 
periphlebitis is a rare form of orbital inflammation presenting with proptosis 
and motility restriction yet few inflammatory signs. It may be associated with 
autoimmune hyperthyroidism and represent a rare feature of thyroid eye disease, 
or perhaps could be an overlap syndrome between thyroid eye disease and 
nonspecific orbital inflammation. Treatment response is variable and may require 
a prolonged course of steroids or other immunosuppressive medications.
