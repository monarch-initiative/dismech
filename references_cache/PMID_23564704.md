# HER2 amplification in gastric cancer is a rare event restricted to the intestinal phenotype.
**Authors:** Cruz-Reyes C, Gamboa-Dominguez A
**Journal:** Int J Surg Pathol (2013)
**DOI:** [10.1177/1066896913481055](https://doi.org/10.1177/1066896913481055)

Cruz-Reyes C(1), Gamboa-Dominguez A.

Author information:
(1)Departamento de Patología, National Institute of Medical Sciences and 
Nutrition Salvador Zubiran, Mexico City, Mexico.

The objectives of this study were to identify HER2 prevalence in gastric cancer 
and correlate it with location, phenotype, and follow-up. Consecutive gastric 
cancer patients with tissue blocks, gross data, and follow-up data, were 
submitted to immunohistochemistry (IHC) with HercepTest. Chromogenic and 
fluorescence in situ hybridization was performed in IHC-positive tumors. A total 
of 269 patients were included with a median age of 61 years. In 172 
gastrectomized patients, histotypes were diffuse (72, 41.8%), intestinal (63, 
36.6%), and mixed (37, 21.5%). HER2 IHC expression was 0 in 167, 2+ in 2, and 3+ 
in 3 tumors. Only endoscopic biopsies were available in 97 patients and HER2 IHC 
expression was 0 in 88, 1+ in 3, 2+ in 4, and 3+ in 2 patients. In all, 10/269 
(3.7%) had HER2 amplification. Amplified tumors were intestinal adenocarcinomas 
located throughout the different regions of the stomach. Heterogeneity was 
documented in 4 widely sampled tumors. HER2 amplification was restricted to the 
intestinal phenotype. It is a rare event and its screening should be driven by 
gastric cancer histotype.
