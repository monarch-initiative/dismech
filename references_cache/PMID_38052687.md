Colonization factors (CFs) are major virulence factors of enterotoxigenic 
Escherichia coli (ETEC). This pathogen is among the most common causes of 
bacterial diarrhea in children in low- and middle-income countries, travelers, 
and livestock. CFs are major candidate antigens in vaccines under development as 
preventive measures against ETEC infections in humans and livestock. Recent 
molecular studies have indicated that newly identified CFs on human ETEC are 
closely related to animal ETEC CFs. Increased knowledge of pathogenic 
mechanisms, immunogenicity, regulation, and expression of ETEC CFs, as well as 
the possible spread of animal ETEC to humans, may facilitate the future 
development of ETEC vaccines for humans and animals. Here, we present an updated 
review of CFs in ETEC.
