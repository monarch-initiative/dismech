# Exclusion of a PAX6, FOXC1, PITX2, and MYCN mutation in another patient with apple peel intestinal atresia, ocular anomalies and microcephaly and review of the literature.
**Authors:** van Bever Y, van Hest L, Wolfs R, Tibboel D, van den Hoonaard TL, Gischler SJ
**Journal:** Am J Med Genet A (2008)
**DOI:** [10.1002/ajmg.a.32169](https://doi.org/10.1002/ajmg.a.32169)

## Content

1. Am J Med Genet A. 2008 Feb 15;146A(4):500-4. doi: 10.1002/ajmg.a.32169.

Exclusion of a PAX6, FOXC1, PITX2, and MYCN mutation in another patient with
apple peel intestinal atresia, ocular anomalies and microcephaly and review of
the literature.

van Bever Y(1), van Hest L, Wolfs R, Tibboel D, van den Hoonaard TL, Gischler
SJ.

Author information:
(1)Department of Clinical Genetics, Erasmus Medical Centre-Sophia Children's
Hospital, Rotterdam, The Netherlands. y.vanbever@erasmusmc.nl

We describe another patient with the combination of apple peel intestinal
atresia, microcephaly, microphthalmia, and anterior eye chamber anomalies.
Development so far seems to be normal, although there is major visual impairment
due to the corneal clouding. Mutation analysis of the PAX6, FOX1, PITX2, and
MYNC genes was normal as was MLPA for these genes. Autosomal recessive
inheritance is possible as recurrence in sibs was described, although germ line
mosaicism or a microdeletion due to a very small parental translocation cannot
be ruled out.
