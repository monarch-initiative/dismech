# Mutations in the chromatin modifier gene KANSL1 cause the 17q21.31 microdeletion syndrome.

We show that haploinsufficiency of KANSL1 is sufficient to cause the 17q21.31 
microdeletion syndrome, a multisystem disorder characterized by intellectual 
disability, hypotonia and distinctive facial features. The KANSL1 protein is an 
evolutionarily conserved regulator of the chromatin modifier KAT8, which 
influences gene expression through histone H4 lysine 16 (H4K16) acetylation. RNA 
sequencing studies in cell lines derived from affected individuals and the 
presence of learning deficits in Drosophila melanogaster mutants suggest a role 
for KANSL1 in neuronal processes.
