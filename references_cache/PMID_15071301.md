Hypoglycorrhachia in Lyme meningitis.

Dayan NE(1), Rubin LG, Di John D, Sood SK.

Author information:
(1)Division of Pediatric Infectious Diseases, Department of Pediatrics,

System, New Hyde Park, NY, USA.

We describe a previously healthy 11-year-old girl with acute neuroborreliosis, 
who presented with left sixth cranial nerve palsy, elevated intracranial 
pressure, markedly elevated cerebrospinal fluid leukocyte count and protein 
concentration and severe hypoglycorrhachia. These laboratory findings are 
atypical for neuroborreliosis.
