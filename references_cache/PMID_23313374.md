Urofacial syndrome (UFS) (or Ochoa syndrome) is an autosomal-recessive disease 
characterized by congenital urinary bladder dysfunction, associated with a 
significant risk of kidney failure, and an abnormal facial expression upon 
smiling, laughing, and crying. We report that a subset of UFS-affected 
individuals have biallelic mutations in LRIG2, encoding leucine-rich repeats and 
immunoglobulin-like domains 2, a protein implicated in neural cell signaling and 
tumorigenesis. Importantly, we have demonstrated that rare variants in LRIG2 
might be relevant to nonsyndromic bladder disease. We have previously shown that 
UFS is also caused by mutations in HPSE2, encoding heparanase-2. LRIG2 and 
heparanase-2 were immunodetected in nerve fascicles growing between muscle 
bundles within the human fetal bladder, directly implicating both molecules in 
neural development in the lower urinary tract.
