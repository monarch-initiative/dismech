# Threonine dehydratase deficiency: a probable cause of non-ketotic hyperglycinaemia.
**Authors:** Krieger I, Booth F
**Journal:** J Inherit Metab Dis (1984)
**DOI:** [10.1007/BF01805800](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF01805800)

## Content

1. J Inherit Metab Dis. 1984;7(2):53-6. doi: 10.1007/BF01805800.

Threonine dehydratase deficiency: a probable cause of non-ketotic 
hyperglycinaemia.

Krieger I, Booth F.

A patient with classical symptoms of non-ketotic hyperglycinaemia (NKH) is 
presented. Threonine dehydratase was undetectable in a liver autopsy specimen, 
which was obtained within 1 h of death and immediately frozen at -70 degrees C. 
Activities of four marker enzymes were normal. This represents the first 
documentation of an inborn error of threonine metabolism and a new explanation 
of NKH.
