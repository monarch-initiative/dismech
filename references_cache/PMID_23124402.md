Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy is the most common genetic cardiovascular disorder 
and the leading cause of sudden cardiac death in the young. This article reviews 
the ventricular arrhythmias associated with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, the 
difficulties in risk stratification, and current and future therapeutic 
strategies.
