Dieulafoy lesions are a rare but life-threatening cause of gastrointestinal 
bleeding. Colonic Dieulafoy lesions are exceptionally rare, comprising only 2% 
of these lesions. We present a case of cecal Dieulafoy lesion as an unusual 
cause of lower gastrointestinal bleeding-along with hemoptysis. An 81-year-old 
male with pulmonary hypertension presented with a one-day history of 
hematochezia. He subsequently developed new small-volume hemoptysis/hematemesis 
with increasing oxygen requirements. Bronchoscopy revealed old blood in the left 
lower lobe, with no active bleeding. The hemoptysis was attributed to severe 
pulmonary hypertension. Colonoscopy revealed a 2-mm cecal Dieulafoy lesion with 
spurting bleeding, which was clipped. We report a rare case of cecal Dieulafoy 
lesion with only 13 other published cases. Our case was complicated by 
hemoptysis creating an interesting diagnostic dilemma. In patients bleeding from 
both oral and anal orifices, a brisk upper gastrointestinal bleed-as well as 
independent causes involving the gastrointestinal and respiratory tracts-should 
be considered.
