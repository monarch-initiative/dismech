A case of a 48-year-old man with an iatrogenic membranous tracheal wall rupture 
after double-lumen intubation during Ivor Lewis esophagogastrectomy is 
presented. Tracheal injury was successfully managed surgically with the use of a 
bovine pericardial patch and reinforcement with the gastric conduit which was 
moved toward the posterior wall of the membranous trachea sealing the wall 
laceration.
