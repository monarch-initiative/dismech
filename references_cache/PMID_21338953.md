Dengue hemorrhagic fever outbreak in children in Port Sudan.

Malik A(1), Earhart K, Mohareb E, Saad M, Saeed M, Ageep A, Soliman A.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pediatric, Red Sea University, Port Sudan, Sudan.

Dengue fever (DF)/dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF) has emerged as a global public 
health problem with countries in Asia and the Pacific sharing more than 70% of 
the disease burden. In 2004-2005 a total of 312 cases admitted to Pediatric and 
Sea Port Hospitals in Port Sudan were clinically diagnosed as DHF. The mortality 
rate recorded was 3.8% (n=12). Of the cases 73.4% were patients 5-15 years of 
age. A total of 91.2% of cases were admitted during May and June 2005 with 49.4% 
residing in the eastern region of Port Sudan. Dengue shock syndrome was observed 
in 37 of 312 (11.9%). All patients had thrombocytopenia with platelets count 
ranged from <100,000 to <150,000 cell/mmÂ³. Of the 40 sera tested using 
RAPID-cassette test in the Khartoum Central Public Health Lab, 36 (90%) were 
dengue IgM positive. A subset of these sera (n=23) were sent to NAMRU-3 and 
confirmed by IgM-capture ELISA; 9 of 23 were PCR positive for dengue serotype 3.
