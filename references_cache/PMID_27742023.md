Systemic rheumatic diseases frequently pose diagnostic challenges to the 
clinician. The precise nature of the disorder can be obscure and different 
disorders can present with similar symptoms, such as joint pain. Plain 
radiographs provide an appropriate starting point for detection of joint 
abnormalities. Musculoskeletal ultrasound and MRI offer greater sensitivity in 
detecting synovitis, tenosynovitis and bony erosions, among other features. 
However, due to the rarity of these diseases and lack of prospective 
longitudinal trials, a broader picture of the epidemiology of these findings and 
their implications for treatment and outcomes remains to be determined.
