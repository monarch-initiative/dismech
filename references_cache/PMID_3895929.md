Noonan syndrome: a review.

Mendez HM, Opitz JM.

After an introduction dealing with the "historical evolution" of the Noonan
syndrome (NS), we try to define the NS phenotype based on clinical descriptions
published since 1883. The theories concerning the cause of the NS are discussed
fully. The peculiar cardiac involvement deserves special attention and raises
the question of whether the Watson and LEOPARD syndromes are indistinguishable
from NS. Finally, the recent contributions to the variability of the NS
phenotype (reports on lymphatic dysplasia, partial deficiency of factor XI,
malignant hyperthermia, perceptual-motor disabilities, and endocrine evaluation)
are also described.
