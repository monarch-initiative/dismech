Chorioretinal lacunae: pathognomonic findings for Aicardi syndrome.

Guerriero S(1), Sciruicchio V, De Blasi R, Furino C, Smaldone G, Ciracì L, 
Galeone D.

Author information:
(1)Department of Ophthalmology and O.R.L., University of Bari, Bari, Italy.

Aicardi syndrome is characterized by agenesis of the corpus callosum, 
chorioretinal lacunae, and infantile spasms. The authors describe the case of a 
girl with chorioretinal lacunae, seizures, and cerebral cyst, but a normal 
corpus callosum. Incomplete forms of Aicardi syndrome are reported in the 
literature, but typical choroidal lacunae are always present and are 
pathognomonic.
