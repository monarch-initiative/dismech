# Fetal akinesia deformation sequence in previable fetuses.
**Authors:** Davis JE, Kalousek DK
**Journal:** Am J Med Genet (1988)
**DOI:** [10.1002/ajmg.1320290110](https://doi.org/10.1002/ajmg.1320290110)

## Content

1. Am J Med Genet. 1988 Jan;29(1):77-87. doi: 10.1002/ajmg.1320290110.

Fetal akinesia deformation sequence in previable fetuses.

Davis JE(1), Kalousek DK.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pathology, Children's Hospital, Vancouver, British Columbia, 
Canada.

We reviewed the morphologic findings of 948 previable fetuses and identified the 
fetal akinesia deformation sequence (FADS) in 16 cases. In eight fetuses who had 
joint contractures, micrognathia, and pulmonary hypoplasia, the cause of fetal 
akinesia could be attributed to an abnormal intrauterine environment restricting 
fetal movement. The other eight fetuses had pterygia across the immobilized 
joints, in addition to main manifestations of FADS. Since most of the fetuses 
with pterygia were of only 8-9 weeks developmental age, we suggest that 
embryonic onset of immobility interferes with limb development and results in 
joint fixation and pterygium formation, in contrast to fetal-onset immobility, 
which causes joint contractures alone.
