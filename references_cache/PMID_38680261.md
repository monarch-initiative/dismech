Pain in chronic pancreatitis (CP) is difficult to manage. Many patients suffer 
from inadequate pain relief, completely incapacitating them in their daily 
activities. Historically, despite their well-known adverse effects, opioids have 
been the pillar of treatment regimens in painful CP. The management is now 
gradually evolving with a better understanding of the underlying pathophysiology 
of CP-related pain. Clinicians should follow a holistic approach to the 
management of CP-associated pain, which must involve lifestyle changes that are 
coupled with analgesic medications and other pain-relieving interventions. 
Furthermore, there is no easy cure for vanquishing CP-associated pain. Each 
patient must be evaluated on a case-by-case basis by a multidisciplinary team to 
decide which treatment option is best suited for that individual.
