# Ovarian Cancer Risk Assessment, Risk Reduction, and Early Detection
**Authors:** Flora Days, Edith Caroline Smith
**Journal:** Clinical Journal for Nurse Practitioners in Women's Health (2025)
**DOI:** [10.1891/cjnpwh-2506](https://doi.org/10.1891/cjnpwh-2506)

## Content

Nurse practitioners (NPs) who provide women’s healthcare have an essential role in cancer risk assessment, risk reduction for prevention, and early detection. Ovarian cancer is a rare but often lethal gynecologic malignancy that can challenge NPs due to its vague symptomatology and lack of early screening tools. For optimal patient care, NPs need to be familiar with ovarian cancer risk factors, common presenting symptoms, available high-risk ovarian cancer screening protocols, and risk-reducing and preventive strategies. Recognizing existing health disparities in ovarian cancer outcomes and using strategies to mitigate inequities can also aid in ovarian cancer risk reduction and prevention.
