Left ventricular systolic dysfunction is a significant cause of morbidity among 
cancer patients in whom this unfortunate complication develops. Investigation 
and management of chemotherapy- and radiation-induced cardiomyopathy in the 
emerging field of cardio-oncology involves a multidisciplinary approach between 
cardiology and oncology departments. The purpose of this article is to provide a 
practical approach to the cardiologist's assessment and management of cancer 
treatment-related cardiomyopathies.
