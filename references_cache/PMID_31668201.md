Viral hemorrhagic fevers represent a group of diseases caused by enveloped RNA 
viruses. The epidemiology is broadly variable, ranging from geographically 
localized to more diffuse infections. Viral hemorrhagic fevers are classified as 
category A bioweapon agents by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. 
Viral hemorrhagic fevers are severe febrile illnesses with hemorrhagic 
phenomena. Laboratory diagnosis takes place in highly specialized reference 
laboratories. Treatment is essentially supportive. In this article, we focus the 
attention on yellow fever and viral hemorrhagic fevers other than Ebola and 
Lassa virus diseases that have been described elsewhere in this issue.
