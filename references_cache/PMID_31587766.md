Empty nose syndrome (ENS) is a controversial condition associated with 
disruption of nasal airflow caused by excessive loss of turbinate tissue. ENS 
arises after total or near-total inferior turbinate resection. Patients present 
with intense fixation on the perception of nasal obstruction. Diagnostic tools 
to assess for empty nose syndrome include a validated patient questionnaire and 
the office cotton test. Treatment involves topical moisturization, 
behavioral/psychiatric assessment/treatment, and surgical reconstruction. 
Current data show promising long-term efficacy after surgical intervention. 
Postprocedural ENS is best prevented by minimizing inferior and middle turbinate 
tissue loss.
