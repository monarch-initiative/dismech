WHAT IS ALREADY KNOWN ABOUT THIS TOPIC? Coal-burning pollution type of endemic 
fluorosis poses a serious health threat to rural inhabitants in certain regions 
of China.
WHAT IS ADDED BY THIS REPORT? The detection rate of dental fluorosis among 
children aged 8-12 years in China's coal-burning endemic fluorosis areas showed 
a significant decreasing trend from 2009 to 2023. Nine provincial-level 
administrative divisions (PLADs) were classified as low-prevalence clusters, 
while only Yunnan was identified as a high-prevalence cluster during 2022-2023.
WHAT ARE THE IMPLICATIONS FOR PUBLIC HEALTH PRACTICE? Prevention and control of 
coal-burning endemic fluorosis should be managed with precision based on the 
epidemiological characteristics of each PLAD. Efforts should enhance 
surveillance and early warning systems while strengthening preventive measures 
in high-risk regions.
