# The presenting symptoms of head and neck cancer.
**Authors:** Robertson MS, Hornibrook J
**Journal:** N Z Med J (1982)

## Content

1. N Z Med J. 1982 May 26;95(708):337-41.

The presenting symptoms of head and neck cancer.

Robertson MS, Hornibrook J.

The late presentation of head and neck malignancies is often attributable to 
failure by the patient and the doctors to appreciate the significance of early 
symptoms. The presenting features of 522 cases are summarised. They emphasize 
that the following clinical features are significant, especially in a patient 
who smokes or drinks: local pain, pain referred to the ear, hoarseness, 
dysphagia, dyspnoea and stridor, persistent sore throat, nasal obstruction, 
bleeding, problems fitting dentures and a neck lump. An adequate history and 
ability to examine the head and neck region are prerequisites to early 
diagnosis. A knowledge of the presenting features of head and neck malignancies 
could be stressed more adequately by public health authorities.
