Tropheryma whipplei Infections, Mexico, 2019-2021.

Delgado-de la Mora J, Grube-Pagola P, Paddock CD, DeLeon-Carnes M, Laga AC, 
Solomon IH, Remes-Troche JM, Baquera-Heredia JJ, Quintero-Bustos G, 
León-Contreras JC, Ángeles-Ángeles A, Martínez-Benítez B.

Whipple's disease is rarely diagnosed in Latin America. We describe 2 patients 
with Tropheryma whipplei infection diagnosed in Mexico during 2019-2021. 
Diagnoses were confirmed by histopathology, electron microscopy, 
immunohistochemistry, and DNA amplification and sequencing analysis of the 16S 
rRNA gene. Clinicians should be aware of T. whipplei infection and associated 
syndromes.
