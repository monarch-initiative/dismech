Peutz-Jeghers syndrome (PJS) is a rare autosomal dominant disorder characterised 
by mucocutaneous pigmentation, gastrointestinal polyps and an increased risk of 
gastrointestinal and other cancers. We report an Indonesian woman, aged 28, with 
black spots on her lips who had multiple polyps extending from the stomach to 
the rectum. Her father and a son also had mucocutaneous lesions but they did not 
undergo gastrointestinal investigations. All three had mutations in the 
serine/threonine kinase 11 gene (STK11).
