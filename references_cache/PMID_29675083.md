Menkes disease (MD) is a rare infantile onset neurodegenerative disorder due to 
mutations in the X linked ATP7A gene. These patients can present with failure to 
thrive, severe psychomotor retardation, seizures and hypopigmented hair, which 
is characteristic of this condition. A number of neuro-radiological findings 
have been reported in this condition. We report the spectrum of 
neuro-radiological findings in three affected boys being treated at our centre. 
We suggest that magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and, in particular magnetic 
resonance angiography (MRA) when taken in the context of the clinical 
presentation may be helpful in making an early diagnosis of this devastating 
condition.
