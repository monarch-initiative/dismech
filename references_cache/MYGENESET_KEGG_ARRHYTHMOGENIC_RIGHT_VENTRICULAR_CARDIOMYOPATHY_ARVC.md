# MYGENESET:KEGG_ARRHYTHMOGENIC_RIGHT_VENTRICULAR_CARDIOMYOPATHY_ARVC  KEGG_ARRHYTHMOGENIC_RIGHT_VENTRICULAR_CARDIOMYOPATHY_ARVC

**MYGENESET:KEGG_ARRHYTHMOGENIC_RIGHT_VENTRICULAR_CARDIOMYOPATHY_ARVC** — KEGG_ARRHYTHMOGENIC_RIGHT_VENTRICULAR_CARDIOMYOPATHY_ARVC (msigdb, C2:CP:KEGG_LEGACY)

## Description

KEGG legacy arrhythmogenic right ventricular cardiomyopathy (ARVC) pathway gene set (MSigDB C2 curated gene set). This is a disease pathway gene set, not a cell-type signature. The member genes are dominated by desmosomal proteins (PKP2, DSP, DSG2, DSC2, JUP) that form the intercalated disc adhesion complex, supplemented by cell-junction and cadherin-catenin components (CDH2, CTNNA3), gap-junction proteins (GJA1, GJA5), and calcium- handling/adrenergic regulators (RYR2, CACNA1C, CACNB2, ADRB1, ADRB2). Unlike dilated cardiomyopathy (dominated by sarcomere and dystrophin- glycoprotein-complex genes) or hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (dominated by thick-filament components), the defining ARVC lesion is desmosome dysfunction: loss of desmosomal integrity in right-ventricular cardiomyocytes leads to myocyte detachment, fibro-fatty replacement, and life-threatening ventricular arrhythmias. As a KEGG legacy pathway it captures the established mechanistic arms and reflects the major gene families whose mutations underlie familial ARVC.
