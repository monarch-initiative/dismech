Renal ciliopathies are a group of disorders characterised by nephronophthisis, 
cystic kidneys or renal cystic dysplasia whose underlying disease pathogenesis 
is related to abnormal structure or function of the primary cilia complex. The 
number of renal ciliopathies continues to expand as genomic and genetic 
approaches identify novel causes. This in turn provides new opportunities to 
explore disease mechanisms and therapeutic approaches to target cystic kidney 
disease and other associated phenotypes. Here we review recent advances in the 
field of renal ciliopathies and how these allow new insights into this 
fascinating spectrum of diseases.
