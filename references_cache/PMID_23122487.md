# Psychosis revealing familial idiopathic basal ganglia calcification.
**Authors:** Nicolas G, Guillin O, Borden A, Bioux S, Lefaucheur R, Hannequin D
**Journal:** Gen Hosp Psychiatry (2013)

Psychosis revealing familial idiopathic basal ganglia calcification.

Nicolas G(1), Guillin O, Borden A, Bioux S, Lefaucheur R, Hannequin D.

Author information:
(1)Department of Neurology, Rouen University Hospital, Rouen, France; Inserm

We describe the case of a 39-year-old woman presenting with auditory 
hallucinations and delusions responsive to antipsychotic drugs. Computerized 
tomography scans revealed basal ganglia calcifications in the proband and in her 
two asymptomatic parents. Extensive etiological clinicobiological assessment 
allowed us to exclude known causes of brain calcifications and diagnose familial 
idiopathic basal ganglia calcification (IBGC). Neurological symptoms associated 
with psychiatric symptoms are common in IBGC. Nevertheless, purely psychiatric 
presentations, as demonstrated by the present case, are possible. However, a 
fortuitous association between asymptomatic IBGC and schizophrenia cannot be 
ruled out. Only brain imaging, followed by an extensive etiological assessment, 
allows for diagnosis of this rare disorder.
