A 35-year-old man had splenic rupture just after starting antimalarial treatment 
with atovaquone-proguanil followed by artesunate for acute Plasmodium ovale 
sensu lato infection. Spleen histology showed a 10-fold accumulation of intact 
P. ovale s.l.-infected erythrocytes in the spleen parenchyma compared to the 
general circulation. Infected erythrocytes also accumulated in small 
extra-splenic blood vessels, suggesting cytoadherence.
