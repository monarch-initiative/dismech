# FGFR3 mutation in thanatophoric dwarfism
**Authors:** Rousseau F, Bonaventure J, Legeai-Mallet L, et al.
**Journal:** Nat Genet (1996)
**DOI:** [10.1038/ng0896-338](https://doi.org/10.1038/ng0896-338)

## Content

FGFR3 mutation in thanatophoric dwarfism.

Nat Genet. 1996 Aug;13(3):338-40. doi: 10.1038/ng0896-338.

Thanatophoric dwarfism (TD) is a sporadic lethal skeletal dysplasia with
micromelic shortening of the limbs, macrocephaly, platyspondyly and reduced
thoracic cavity. In the most common subtype (TD1), femurs are curved, while in
TD2, straight femurs are associated with cloverleaf skull.

Here we show that 31 of 32 TD patients bear mutations in the fibroblast growth
factor receptor 3 gene (FGFR3), with a recurrent C742T change and a recurrent
C746G change being the common mutations causing TD1.
