## Description

KEGG legacy Helicobacter pylori epithelial-cell signaling pathway gene set (MSigDB C2 curated gene set). This is an infection-driven inflammatory disease pathway gene set, not a cell-type signature. H. pylori colonises the gastric mucosa and deploys virulence factors — CagA (injected via the type IV secretion system, T4SS), VacA (vacuolating cytotoxin), and peptidoglycan fragments (recognised by the intracellular receptor NOD1) — to activate the canonical IKK-NF-kappaB cascade and multiple MAPK branches (ERK1/2, p38, JNK) in gastric epithelial cells. The downstream transcriptional output drives robust pro-inflammatory chemokine and cytokine production, most prominently CXCL8 (IL-8), which recruits neutrophils to the gastric mucosa and initiates the acute inflammatory response underlying H. pylori-associated gastritis. Persistent infection establishes a chronic inflammatory microenvironment that is a major risk factor for peptic ulcer disease and intestinal-type gastric cancer. Member genes encode kinases of the IKK complex and MAPK tiers, IkB proteins (NFKBIA), NF-kappaB subunits, chemokines and cytokines (CXCL8, IL-1B), receptor tyrosine kinases (EGFR, MET), and adaptors (SHP2/PTPN11) that mediate cytoskeletal remodelling. As a legacy KEGG pathway it captures the established core mechanism.
