Mosaic Variegated Aneuploidy Syndrome 2 (MVA2; MIM 614114) is a rare autosomal
recessive disorder, characterized by mosaic aneuploidies involving multiple
chromosomes and tissues, caused by biallelic pathogenic variants in the CEP57
gene. Only 10 patients have been reported to date. We report two additional non
related cases born to Moroccan consanguineous parents, carrying the previously
described c.915_925dup11 CEP57 homozygous variant. Common features of these 12
cases include growth retardation, typically of prenatal onset, distinctive
facial features, endocrine, cardiovascular and skeletal, abnormalities while
malignancies have not been reported. This report describes the phenotypical
spectrum of MVA2.
