Marfan syndrome is a genetic disorder of the connective tissue caused by 
mutations in FBN1 gene. There are approximately 2-3,000 Marfan patients in The 
Netherlands. It has a broad clinical spectrum with features in most organ 
systems. Aortic root aneurysm is the most important clinical problem in these 
patients as it is progressive and leads to dissection and rupture if untreated. 
Endocarditis prophylaxis is indicated in all Marfan patients with aortic root 
aneurysm and operated patients. Timely diagnosis and specialized patient care 
are essential for the prognosis of this disease.
