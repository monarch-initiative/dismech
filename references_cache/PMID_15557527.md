CSF characteristics in early-onset multiple sclerosis.

Pohl D(1), Rostasy K, Reiber H, Hanefeld F.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pediatrics and Pediatric Neurology, Georg-August-University 
Goettingen, Robert-Koch-Strasse 40, 37075 Goettingen, Germany. 
dpohl@med.uni-goettingen.de

The authors studied CSF characteristics in 136 patients with multiple sclerosis 
(MS) with a disease onset before age 16. In the initial diagnostic lumbar 
puncture, CSF-pleocytosis was observed in 66%, blood-CSF barrier dysfunction in 
13%, and oligoclonal IgG in 92% of the early-onset MS (EOMS) patients. CSF 
oligoclonal IgG supports the early diagnosis of MS in childhood with a 
sensitivity similar to adult-onset MS.
