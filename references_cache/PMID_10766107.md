# The Mackworth Clock Test: a computerized version.
**Authors:** Lichstein KL, Riedel BW, Richman SL
**Journal:** J Psychol (2000)
**DOI:** [10.1080/00223980009600858](https://doi.org/10.1080/00223980009600858)

The Mackworth Clock Test (MCT; N. H. Mackworth, 1948) was developed to evaluate 
vigilance in British Air Force radar technicians during World War II. Homemade 
versions of the MCT have since varied with respect to both the characteristics 
of the device and the procedures of its administration. This article is a report 
on a computerized version of the MCT developed by the authors to closely emulate 
Mackworth's test. MCT data were collected from 25 undergraduate students; their 
performance was found to be equivalent to Mackworth's participants' data. This 
is the first MCT version that has been validated against the original.
