Leptospirosis is an infectious disorder of animals and humans and is the most 
common zoonotic infection in the world. This infection is easily transmitted 
from infected animals through their urine, either directly or through infected 
soil or water. Leptospirosis can cause a self-limiting influenza-like illness or 
a much more serious disease. This condition is known as Weil disease, and it can 
progress to multiorgan failure with the potential for death.
