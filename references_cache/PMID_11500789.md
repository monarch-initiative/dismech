# The frequency of Tay-Sachs disease causing mutations in the Brazilian Jewish population justifies a carrier screening program
**Authors:** R Rozenberg, L da V Pereira
**Journal:** Sao Paulo Med J (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1590/S1516-31802001000400007](https://doi.org/10.1590/S1516-31802001000400007)

## Content

Context: Tay-Sachs disease is an autosomal recessive disease characterized by progressive neurologic degeneration, fatal in early childhood. In the Ashkenazi Jewish population the disease incidence is about 1 in every 3,500 newborns and the carrier frequency is 1 in every 29 individuals. Carrier screening programs for Tay-Sachs disease have reduced disease incidence by 90% in high-risk populations in several countries.

Results: Eight carriers were detected corresponding to a carrier frequency of 1 in every 33 individuals in the Ashkenazi Jewish fraction of the sample. Conclusion: The frequency of Tay-Sachs disease carriers among the Ashkenazi Jewish population of Brazil is similar to that of other countries where carrier screening programs have led to a significant decrease in disease incidence.
