A semirigid tetraacid linker H4L functionalized with 1,2,3-triazole was 
rationally designed and synthesized to access nitrogen-rich MOFs for selective 
adsorption of CO2. The cadmium MOF, that is, Cd-L, obtained by the reaction of 
H4L with Cd(NO3)2, is found to be a 3D porous framework structure that is robust 
to desolvation. Crystal structure analysis reveals channels that are decorated 
by the triazole moieties of L. Gas adsorption studies show that Cd-L MOF permits 
remarkable CO2 uptake to the extent of 99 and 1000 cc/g at 1 and 30 bar, 
respectively, at 0 Â°C. While literature survey reveals that MIL-112, constructed 
from a 1,2,3-triazole functionalized linker, exhibits no porosity to gas 
adsorption due to structural flexibility, the results with Cd-L MOF described 
herein emphasize how rigidification of the organic linker improves gas uptake 
properties of the resultant MOF.
