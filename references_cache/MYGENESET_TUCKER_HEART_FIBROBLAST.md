# MYGENESET:TUCKER_HEART_FIBROBLAST  TUCKER_HEART_FIBROBLAST

**MYGENESET:TUCKER_HEART_FIBROBLAST** — TUCKER_HEART_FIBROBLAST (msigdb, C8)

## Description

Single-cell marker signature of cardiac fibroblasts from the human heart (Tucker et al. heart cell atlas, MSigDB C8). Cardiac fibroblasts (CL:0002548, fibroblast of cardiac tissue) are stromal cells embedded in the myocardial interstitium that maintain cardiac structural integrity by synthesising and remodelling extracellular matrix components, principally fibrillar collagens, fibronectin, and proteoglycans. Unlike fibroblasts in other organs, cardiac fibroblasts are continuously exposed to the cyclic mechanical loading of the beating heart, making mechanosensory signalling a defining feature of their biology and distinguishing them from lung or skin fibroblasts. Upon myocardial injury, cardiac fibroblasts activate and can transdifferentiate into myofibroblasts, producing a reparative collagen-rich scar. The signature is dominated by ECM structural and biosynthetic genes, consistent with the primary secretory and organisational function of this cell type in the cardiac stroma.
