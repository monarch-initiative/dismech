Central nervous system (CNS) infection is a medical emergency with an important 
cause of mortality worldwide. The 79 patients with confirmed acute CNS infection 
(48 bacterial and 31 viral meningitis) were evaluated. Bacterial meningitis 
score, cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)/serum glucose ratio, and CSF/serum albumin 
ratio had the highest area under the curves (0.873, 0.843, 0.810, respectively) 
for discriminating bacterial meningitis. Neutrophil-to-lymphocyte (NLR), 
platelet-to-lymphocyte ratio (PLR) and CSF lactate dehydrogenase have a good 
ability for the differential diagnosis of bacterial meningitis. CSF/serum 
glucose ratio, NLR (with a cut-off value> 8.87), large unstained cell, total 
protein, albumin, and procalcitonin levels were found to be predictors for 
mortality. NLR can be used as a biomarker to differentiate bacterial meningitis 
from viral meningitis and to predict the prognosis of CNS infection. CSF/serum 
albumin ratio and CSF lactate dehydrogenase can be used to predict bacterial 
meningitis as well as CSF/serum glucose ratio.
