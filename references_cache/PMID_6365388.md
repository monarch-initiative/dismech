# Transitions in collagen types during endochondral ossification in human growth cartilage.
**Authors:** Yasui N, Ono K, Konomi H, Nagai Y
**Journal:** Clin Orthop Relat Res (1984)

## Content

1. Clin Orthop Relat Res. 1984 Mar;(183):215-8.

Transitions in collagen types during endochondral ossification in human growth 
cartilage.

Yasui N, Ono K, Konomi H, Nagai Y.

Immunohistochemical staining for Types I and II collagen in human growth 
cartilage showed that cartilage matrix consists of Type II collagen, whereas 
bone matrix contains Type I collagen. Endochondral ossification began with the 
deposition of Type I collagen by cells derived from bone marrow on the surface 
of the eroded cartilage. However, territorial matrix of the last hypertrophic 
chondrocytes contained both Types I and II collagen, thereby indicating that the 
degenerating chondrocytes initiate the synthesis of Type I collagen. This 
matrix, consisting of Types I and II collagen, is then replaced by newly formed 
osteoid.
