# The Role of 18 F-FDG PET/CT Combined With MPI in Cogan's Syndrome With Multiple Arteritis and Coronary Involvement: A Case Report.

We present a case of a 49-year-old woman who complained of chest pain after 
activity for 3 months. She had undergone percutaneous coronary intervention 
(PCI) on the left main (LM) and right coronary artery (RCA) for acute myocardial 
infarction (AMI) 1 year ago. At this admission, a coronary angiogram (CAG) 
revealed stent restenosis. Then, 99m Tc-Sestamibi myocardial perfusion imaging 
(MPI) and 18 F-FDG PET/CT showed multiple arteritis involving the coronary 
artery with no obvious myocardial ischemia. This suggests that the patient 
should be treated with anti-inflammatory measures instead of repeating 
revascularization caused by persistent stent stenosis, making this an extremely 
rare case of Cogan's syndrome (CS).
