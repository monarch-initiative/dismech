Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) is a complex, chronic autoimmune disease. The 
etiology of SLE is multifactorial and includes potential environmental triggers, 
which may occur sequentially (the "multi-hit" hypothesis). This review focuses 
on SLE risk potentially associated with environmental factors including 
infections, the microbiome, diet, respirable exposures (eg, crystalline silica, 
smoking, air pollution), organic pollutants, heavy metals, and ultraviolet 
radiation.
