Two unrelated patients with autosomal dominant omodysplasia and FRIZZLED2 
mutations.

Warren HE(1), Louie RJ(1), Friez MJ(1), Frías JL(2), Leroy JG(1), Spranger 
JW(1), Skinner SA(1), Champaigne NL(1).

Author information:
(1)Greenwood Genetic Center Greenwood South Carolina.
(2)Department of Pediatrics University of South Florida Tampa Florida.

Presented are two patients with autosomal dominant omodysplasia and mutations in 
the FZD2 gene. The mutations identified have been recently reported, suggesting 
the possibility of recurrent mutations. The phenotypes of these patients overlap 
with what has been previously reported, though intellectual disability as seen 
in our patient is not typical.
