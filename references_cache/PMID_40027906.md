• ATS can be discovered by high-quality fetal imaging. • Postnatal genetic
testing is required for confirmation of the diagnosis. • A homozygous variant in
SLC2A10 (c.173 C > T; p.Ala58Val) is newly described. • Multidisciplinary
follow-up for children with ATS is essential.
