To determine whether TXNDC15 variation causes Meckel-Gruber syndrome (MKS), we 
assessed the pathogenicity of the frameshift variant c.560delA. A CRISPR-Cas9 
generated mouse model carrying the equivalent Txndc15 c.512delA mutation was 
analyzed at embryonic day 15.5. Homozygous Txndc15mt/mt embryos displayed the 
complete MKS phenotype-fetal lethality, exencephaly, omphalocele, post-axial 
polydactyly, and polycystic kidneys-together with markedly reduced TXNDC15 
protein in brain, liver, and kidney. These findings confirm TXNDC15 as a bona 
fide MKS disease gene.
