Adult-onset Still's disease should be considered in young adults presenting with
fever of unknown origin and inflammatory arthritis, even in the absence of rash
or sore throat. Recognition of the Wissler-Fanconi variant and early ferritin
testing can prevent diagnostic delay and enable prompt, effective
immunosuppressive therapy.
