Omodysplasia is an extremely rare skeletal dysplasia. Since introducing this 
phenotype as a new syndrome, ten cases of the autosomal dominant type of this 
disease have been reported. Here, we present a new patient and review published 
articles in this field to provide a clinical diagnostic criterion.
