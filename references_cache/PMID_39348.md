Biochemistry and the schizophrenias.

Smythies JR.

This paper reviews the present status of the transmethylation and the dopamine 
hypotheses of schizophrenia and presents recent data on the definitive 
identification and measurement of the endogenous hallucinogen, 
dimethyltryptamine (DMT) in human cerebrospinal fluid and urine. Elevated levels 
are found in some cases of schizophrenia and of liver disease. Evidence is 
discussed showing that DMT may be a normal neuroregulatory agent which is 
responsive to stress or emotional reactions.
