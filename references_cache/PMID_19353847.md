# A novel homozygous SCO2 mutation, p.G193S, causing fatal infantile cardioencephalomyopathy.
**Authors:** Mobley BC, Enns GM, Wong LJ, Vogel H
**Journal:** Clin Neuropathol (2009)
**DOI:** [10.5414/npp28143](https://doi.org/10.5414/npp28143)

## Content

1. Clin Neuropathol. 2009 Mar-Apr;28(2):143-9. doi: 10.5414/npp28143.

A novel homozygous SCO2 mutation, p.G193S, causing fatal infantile

Mobley BC(1), Enns GM, Wong LJ, Vogel H.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pathology, Stanford University School of Medicine, Stanford, CA 
94305, USA. bretmobley@gmail.com

Cytochrome c oxidase (COX) deficiency is a frequent cause of mitochondrial 
disease in infants. Mutations in the COX assembly gene SCO2 cause fatal 
infantile cardioencephalomyopathy. All patients reported to date with SCO2 
deficiency share a common p.E140K mutation in at least 1 allele. In order to 
further the understanding of the genotype-phenotype spectrum associated with 
fatal infantile cardioencephalomyopathy, we describe a novel homozygous SCO2 
mutation p.G193S in a patient with fatal infantile cardioencephalomyopathy born 
to consanguineous parents of Indian ancestry.
