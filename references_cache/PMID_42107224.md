Depth of invasion (DOI) is an important prognostic factor in oral cavity 
squamous cell carcinoma (OSCC) and has gained prominence after incorporation 
into the AJCC staging system. Although DOI correlates strongly with cervical 
nodal metastasis, its independent effect on survival, interaction with nodal 
stage, subsite variations, and role in guiding adjuvant therapy in node-negative 
disease remain uncertain. Objectives To evaluate the prognostic significance of 
DOI in relation to pathological nodal (pN) stage, assess its association with 
adverse histopathologic features, examine subsitespecific recurrence patterns, 
and identify a DOI threshold associated with recurrence risk in early-stage 
disease. Methods A retrospective analysis was performed on a prospectively 
maintained cohort of 469 patients with OSCC treated surgically between 2014 and 
2024. DOI was correlated with nodal status, histopathologic risk factors, 
recurrence-free survival (RFS), overall survival (OS), and disease-specific 
survival (DSS). Survival analyses were performed using Kaplan-Meier methods and 
Cox proportional hazards regression. Maximally selected rank statistics 
identified the optimal DOI threshold for recurrence. Results Increasing DOI was 
associated with higher nodal metastasis and adverse histopathologic features. In 
early node-negative patients, DOI did not independently influence OS, DSS. 
Adjuvant radiotherapy showed no survival benefit. Subsite analysis revealed no 
DOI-dependent recurrence gradient. A DOI threshold of 1.4 cm predicted 
recurrence risk but not OS or DSS after adjusting for nodal status. Conclusion 
DOI predicts nodal metastasis and recurrence but does not independently 
determine survival. In early node-negative OSCC, escalation of adjuvant therapy 
based solely on DOI may be unwarranted.
