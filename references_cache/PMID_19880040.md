Pediatric melanoma is rare but increasing in incidence. Because early diagnosis 
and treatment improves prognosis, clinicians need to include it as a possible 
diagnosis when evaluating a pigmented lesion in a pediatric patient. Some risk 
factors for melanoma include xeroderma pigmentosum, giant congenital melanocytic 
nevi, dysplastic nevus syndrome, atypical nevi, many acquired melanocytic nevi, 
family history of melanoma, and immunosuppression. Definitive treatment is with 
surgical excision. Adjuvant therapies such as chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and 
radiation therapy can be used in advanced cases.
