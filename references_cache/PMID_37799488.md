Cholestatic pruritus is a debilitating symptom associated with many liver 
diseases and is often refractory to medical management. Nasobiliary drainage is 
a relatively safe and effective method for treating intractable cholestatic 
pruritus. It should be considered for patients with refractory cholestatic 
pruritus who have failed or have contraindications to medical therapy as a 
predictor of response before surgical fistula creation.
