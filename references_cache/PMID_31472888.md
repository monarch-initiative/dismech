Hypertensive heart disease represents a spectrum of illnesses from uncontrolled 
hypertension to heart failure. The authors discuss the natural history and 
pathogenesis of heart failure owing to hypertensive heart disease, reviewing the 
important role of left ventricular hypertrophy as the inciting process leading 
to diastolic dysfunction and heart failure with preserved ejection fraction. 
They describe the various mechanisms by which a subset of patients ultimately 
develops systolic heart failure. They discuss management strategies for 
hypertensive heart disease at all stages of the disease process. Treatment in 
the initial stages before onset of heart failure may result in regression of 
disease.
