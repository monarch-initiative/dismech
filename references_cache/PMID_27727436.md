Quantitative analysis of urine acylglycines has shown to be a highly sensitive
and specific method with proven clinical utility for the diagnosis of several
inherited metabolic disorders including: medium chain acyl-CoA dehydrogenase
deficiency, multiple acyl-CoA dehydrogenase deficiency, short chain acyl-CoA
dehydrogenase deficiency, 3-methylcrotonyl-CoA carboxylase deficiency,
2-methylbutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase deficiency, isovaleric acidemia, propionic
academia, and isobutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase deficiency. Here, a method that is
currently performed using ultra-performance liquid chromatography/tandem mass
spectrometry (UPLC-MS/MS) is described. © 2016 by John Wiley & Sons, Inc.
