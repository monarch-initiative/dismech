## Summary

BCR::ABL1 fusion is a diagnostic criterion for Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML).

## Description

Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML) is a well-defined myeloproliferative neoplasm characterized by the t(9;22)(q34;q11.2) forming the BCR::ABL1 fusion. The fusion is expressed from the derivative chromosome 22, known also as Philadelphia (Ph) chromosome. This fusion results in a constitutively active BCR::ABL1 tyrosine kinase, representing the initiating and defining molecular event in CML pathogenesis. Professional guidelines (WHO) list detection of BCR::ABL1 fusion as a diagnostic criterion for CML (WHO classification of tumors series, 5th ed.; vol. 7).
