Camurati-Engelmann disease (CED, MIM 131300) is an autosomal dominant, 
progressive diaphyseal dysplasia characterized by hyperosteosis and sclerosis of 
the diaphyses of long bones. We recently assigned the CED locus to an interval 
between D19S422 and D19S606 at chromosome 19q13.1-q13.3, which two other groups 
confirmed. As the human transforming growth factor-1 gene (TGFB1) is located 
within this interval, we considered it a candidate gene for CED.
