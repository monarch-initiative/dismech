# MYGENESET:WP_AFFECTED_PATHWAYS_IN_DUCHENNE_MUSCULAR_DYSTROPHY  WP_AFFECTED_PATHWAYS_IN_DUCHENNE_MUSCULAR_DYSTROPHY

**MYGENESET:WP_AFFECTED_PATHWAYS_IN_DUCHENNE_MUSCULAR_DYSTROPHY** — WP_AFFECTED_PATHWAYS_IN_DUCHENNE_MUSCULAR_DYSTROPHY (msigdb, C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS)

## Description

WikiPathways WP5356 gene set (MSigDB C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS) representing the affected pathways in Duchenne muscular dystrophy (DMD). DMD is caused by loss-of-function mutations in the DMD gene encoding dystrophin, a large cytoskeletal protein that forms the core scaffold of the dystrophin-associated glycoprotein complex (DGC). Loss of DGC integrity renders the sarcolemma mechanically fragile, leading to contraction-induced micro-tears, unregulated calcium influx, activation of calcium-dependent proteases, chronic inflammatory infiltration, progressive muscle necrosis, and fibrosis. The member genes span the DGC structural components (DMD, DAG1, sarcoglycans, syntrophins, dystrobrevins), calcium-handling regulators, inflammatory mediators, and the compensatory regenerative response, collectively capturing the major pathomechanistic arms of DMD in skeletal muscle.
