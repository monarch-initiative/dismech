BACKGROUND: Aicardi syndrome (AS) is a rare neurodevelopmental disorder 
characterized by the triad of corpus callosum agenesis, chorioretinal lacunae, 
and infantile spasms. Most patients with AS also have intractable epilepsy, 
moderate to severe learning disability, and a reduced life expectancy. An 
X-linked dominant inheritance caused by de novo mutations pattern, lethal in 
males, is postulated, but the gene has not yet been isolated. There are three 
case reports of 47 XXY males with classic features of AS who all had severe 
developmental disability.
CASE REPORT: We report a case of a 3.5-year old 47 XXY male with the classic 
triad of Aicardi syndrome but with good seizure control and mild learning 
disability.
