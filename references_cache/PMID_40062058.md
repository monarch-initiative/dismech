Peutz-Jeghers syndrome (PJS) is an uncommon autosomal dominant disorder that 
manifests as mucocutaneous pigmentation and hamartomatous polyps in the 
gastrointestinal system. Pigmentation of the skin and mucous membranes may be 
present from birth, but it typically appears in early childhood and can 
sometimes develop later. In addition to an increased lifelong risk of cancers 
and problems, such as gastrointestinal bleeding from polyposis, hamartomatous 
polyps can develop in the stomach, small bowel, or colon. In order to prevent 
difficulties associated with intestinal and extraintestinal malignancies in 
patients diagnosed with PJS, routine screening is essential. We report a case of 
a 19-year-old young girl who presented with intermittent episodes of abdominal 
pain and a few occasions of fresh per rectal bleeding, as well as features of 
anaemia.
