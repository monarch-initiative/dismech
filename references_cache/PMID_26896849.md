In Arabidopsis, jasmonate (JA)-signaling plays a key role in mediating Fusarium 
oxysporum disease outcome. However, the roles of JASMONATE ZIM-domain (JAZ) 
proteins that repress JA-signaling have not been characterized in host 
resistance or susceptibility to this pathogen. Here, we found most JAZ genes are 
induced following F. oxysporum challenge, and screening T-DNA insertion lines in 
Arabidopsis JAZ family members identified a highly disease-susceptible JAZ7 
mutant (jaz7-1D). This mutant exhibited constitutive JAZ7 expression and 
conferred increased JA-sensitivity, suggesting activation of JA-signaling. 
Unlike jaz7 loss-of-function alleles, jaz7-1D also had enhanced JA-responsive 
gene expression, altered development and increased susceptibility to the 
bacterial pathogen PstDC3000 that also disrupts host JA-responses. We also 
demonstrate that JAZ7 interacts with transcription factors functioning as 
activators (MYC3, MYC4) or repressors (JAM1) of JA-signaling and contains a 
functional EAR repressor motif mediating transcriptional repression via the 
co-repressor TOPLESS (TPL). We propose through direct TPL recruitment, in 
wild-type plants JAZ7 functions as a repressor within the JA-response network 
and that in jaz7-1D plants, misregulated ectopic JAZ7 expression hyper-activates 
JA-signaling in part by disturbing finely-tuned COI1-JAZ-TPL-TF complexes.
