Comment in
    Nat Genet. 2006 Aug;38(8):866-7. doi: 10.1038/ng0806-866.

Aicardi-Goutières syndrome (AGS) is an autosomal recessive neurological
disorder, the clinical and immunological features of which parallel those of
congenital viral infection. Here we define the composition of the human
ribonuclease H2 enzyme complex and show that AGS can result from mutations in
the genes encoding any one of its three subunits. Our findings demonstrate a
role for ribonuclease H in human neurological disease and suggest an
unanticipated relationship between ribonuclease H2 and the antiviral immune
response that warrants further investigation.
