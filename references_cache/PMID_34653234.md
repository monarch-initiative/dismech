Comment in
    Nat Rev Neurol. 2021 Dec;17(12):726. doi: 10.1038/s41582-021-00587-5.

Angelman syndrome (AS) is a neurogenetic disorder characterized by severe
developmental delay with absence of speech, happy disposition, frequent
laughter, hyperactivity, stereotypies, ataxia and seizures with specific EEG
abnormalities. There is a 10-15% of patients with an AS phenotype whose genetic
cause remains unknown (Angelman-like syndrome, AS-like). Whole-exome sequencing
(WES) was performed on a cohort of 14 patients with clinical features of AS and
no molecular diagnosis. As a result, we identified 10 de novo and 1 X-linked
pathogenic/likely pathogenic variants in 10 neurodevelopmental genes (SYNGAP1,
VAMP2, TBL1XR1, ASXL3, SATB2, SMARCE1, SPTAN1, KCNQ3, SLC6A1 and LAS1L) and one
deleterious de novo variant in a candidate gene (HSF2). Our results highlight
the wide genetic heterogeneity in AS-like patients and expands the differential
diagnosis.
