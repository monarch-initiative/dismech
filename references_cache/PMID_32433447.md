Hodgkin lymphoma classically presents as lymphadenopathy with contiguous spread 
with or without associated B symptoms including night sweats, weight loss, and 
fever. With timely diagnosis and treatment, this is a potentially curable 
disease. This report describes a unique case of Hodgkin lymphoma that presented 
with localized back pain without overt evidence of lymph node involvement. This 
nonclassic presentation offers a diagnostic challenge that emphasizes the 
importance of keeping a broad differential in an adolescent or young adult 
patient with lytic bone lesions.
