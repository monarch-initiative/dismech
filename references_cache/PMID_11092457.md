Rieger syndrome (RIEG 1; MIM 180500) is an autosomal dominant disorder of 
morphogenesis. It is a phenotypically heterogeneous disorder characterized by 
malformations of the eyes, teeth, and umbilicus. RIEG belongs to the 
Axenfeld-Rieger group of anomalies, which includes Axenfeld anomaly and Rieger 
anomaly (or Rieger eye malformation), which display ocular features only. 
Recently, mutations in the homeodomain transcription factor, PITX2, have been 
shown to be associated with Rieger syndrome. This review discusses the clinical 
manifestations of Rieger syndrome and how they correlate with the current 
molecular and biochemical studies on this human disorder.
