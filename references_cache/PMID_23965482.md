# Incidence and prevalence of polymyositis and dermatomyositis in a health management organization in Buenos Aires.
**Authors:** Rosa J, Garrot LF, Navarta DA, Saucedo C, Scolnik M, Bedran Z, Garcia MV, Sabelli M, Catoggio LJ, Soriano ER
**Journal:** J Clin Rheumatol (2013)
**DOI:** [10.1097/RHU.0b013e3182a21ba8](https://doi.org/10.1097/RHU.0b013e3182a21ba8)

1. J Clin Rheumatol. 2013 Sep;19(6):303-7. doi: 10.1097/RHU.0b013e3182a21ba8.

Incidence and prevalence of polymyositis and dermatomyositis in a health 
management organization in Buenos Aires.

Rosa J(1), Garrot LF, Navarta DA, Saucedo C, Scolnik M, Bedran Z, Garcia MV, 
Sabelli M, Catoggio LJ, Soriano ER.

Author information:
(1)Servicio de Clínica Médica, Hospital Italiano de Buenos Aires, Argentina.

BACKGROUND: Polymyositis (PM) and dermatomyositis (DM) are infrequent diseases. 
Data on incidence and prevalence are scarce and conflicting. There are no such 
data in Latin America and in Argentina in particular.
OBJECTIVES: We undertook to examine the incidence and prevalence of PM/DM in the 
prepaid health maintenance organization (HMO) of our hospital, in the city of 
Buenos Aires.
METHODS: Members of the HMO between January 1999 and June 2009 were identified 
from medical records of patients followed up by us at the HMO. Incident cases 
and prevalence were calculated at the end of the period.
RESULTS: During the study period, 146,747 persons contributed a total of 
937,902.6 person-years (mean age was 46.6 [SD, 18.4] years, and 59% were 
female). Ten incident cases were detected, 7 women and 3 men with a global 
incidence rate (IR) of 1.07 per 100,000 person-years (95% confidence interval 
[CI], 0.5-1.84). Three subjects had DM with an IR of 0.32 per 100,000 
person-years (95% CI, 0.1-0.99), and 7 had PM with an IR of 0.75 per 100,000 
person-years (95% CI, 0.35-0.16). On June 1, 2009, 17 prevalent cases were 
detected, with a mean age of 48.9 (SD, 17.7) years; 76% were female, 
representing a prevalence of 17.4 per 100,000 persons (95% CI, 10.1-27.8). Among 
the 17 patients with idiopathic inflammatory myopathy, 10 patients had DM, with 
a prevalence of 10.22 per 100,000 persons (95% CI, 4.9-18.8), and 7 had PM 
(prevalence, 7.2 per 100,000 persons [95% CI, 2.9-14.7]).
CONCLUSIONS: It is difficult to compare studies from different populations and 
using different ascertainment techniques. These first data from Latin America 
are in general agreement with many studies.
