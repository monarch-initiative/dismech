There are several infections in adults that warrant special consideration in 
pregnant women given the potential fetal consequences. Among these are 
toxoplasmosis, parvovirus B19, and cytomegalovirus. These infections have an 
important impact on the developing fetus, depending on the timing of infection. 
This article reviews the modes of transmission as well as maternal and neonatal 
effects of each of these infections. In addition, the article outlines 
recommended testing, fetal surveillance, and treatment where indicated.
