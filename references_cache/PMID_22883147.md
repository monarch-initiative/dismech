# RBPJ mutations identified in two families affected by Adams-Oliver syndrome.
**Authors:** Hassed SJ, Wiley GB, Wang S, Lee JY, Li S, Xu W, Zhao ZJ, Mulvihill JJ, Robertson J, Warner J, Gaffney PM

Through exome resequencing, we identified two unique mutations in recombination 
signal binding protein for immunoglobulin kappa J (RBPJ) in two independent 
families affected by Adams-Oliver syndrome (AOS), a rare multiple-malformation 
disorder consisting primarily of aplasia cutis congenita of the vertex scalp and 
transverse terminal limb defects. These identified mutations link RBPJ, the 
primary transcriptional regulator for the Notch pathway, with AOS, a human 
genetic disorder. Functional assays confirmed impaired DNA binding of mutated 
RBPJ, placing it among other notch-pathway proteins altered in human genetic 
syndromes.
