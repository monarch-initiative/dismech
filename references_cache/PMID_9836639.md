Comment in
    Science. 1998 Dec 4;282(5395):1794-5. doi: 10.1126/science.282.5395.1794b.

The M-current regulates the subthreshold electrical excitability of many 
neurons, determining their firing properties and responsiveness to synaptic 
input. To date, however, the genes that encode subunits of this important 
channel have not been identified. The biophysical properties, sensitivity to 
pharmacological blockade, and expression pattern of the KCNQ2 and KCNQ3 
potassium channels were determined. It is concluded that both these subunits 
contribute to the native M-current.
