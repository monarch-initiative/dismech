Stroke is a complication of many central nervous system (CNS) infections, but 
only a few present with stroke without other symptoms or signs of CNS infection. 
Chief among these are varicella zoster virus (VZV) and syphilis. Delayed 
cerebral vasculopathy after successful treatment of bacterial meningitis, most 
commonly pneumococcal, is an emerging entity with uncertain pathogenesis.
