This article reviews the neurologic conditions associated with a high prevalence 
of bladder dysfunction and about which significant advances in understanding 
have occurred in recent years. The importance of the frontal lobes for bladder 
control has been confirmed through functional brain imaging, and recent findings 
in the elderly with incontinence suggest the problem may result from 
disconnection of important frontal areas caused by white matter disease. The 
very different urologic profile of the two sometimes-confused conditions, 
multiple system atrophy and Parkinson's disease, is clarified. The advances in 
treatments for multiple sclerosis in recent years have been remarkable and are 
briefly described.
