Primrose syndrome and 3q13.31 microdeletion syndrome are clinically related 
disorders characterized by tall stature, macrocephaly, intellectual disability, 
disturbed behavior and unusual facial features, with diabetes, deafness, 
progressive muscle wasting and ectopic calcifications specifically occurring in 
the former. We report that missense mutations in ZBTB20, residing within the 
3q13.31 microdeletion syndrome critical region, underlie Primrose syndrome. This 
finding establishes a genetic link between these disorders and delineates the 
impact of ZBTB20 dysregulation on development, growth and metabolism.
