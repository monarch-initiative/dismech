Epithelioid sarcoma first described by Enzinger (Cancer 26:1029-41, 1970) is a 
rare soft-tissue sarcoma typically presenting as a subcutaneous or deep dermal 
mass in distal portions of the extremities of adolescents and young adults. They 
are frequently mistaken for ulcers, abscesses, or infected warts that fail 
medical management. Patients often develop multiple local recurrences of long 
duration, with subsequent metastases in 30 to 50% of cases (Chase and Enzinger 
(Am J Surg Pathol 9:241-63, 1985)). We here report a case of left thumb 
epithelioid sarcoma that presented as an ulcer and subsequently metastasized to 
forearm, arm, axillary lymph nodes, and lungs.
