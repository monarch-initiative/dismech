[Adrenal gland diseases: Addison's Disease].

[Article in German]

Chifu I, Hahner S.

Addison's disease typically results from the autoimmune destruction of the 
adrenal cortex and requires lifelong replacement with glucocorticoids and 
mineralocorticoids. Main symptoms are non-specific and, therefore, often 
overlooked or misleading. Patients are frequently not diagnosed until 
experiencing a life-threatening adrenal crisis. This article highlights the 
essential clinical characteristics, diagnostic aspects and principles of 
management of adrenal insufficiency.
