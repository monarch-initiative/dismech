# D-2-Hydroxyglutaric aciduria: biochemical marker or clinical disease entity?
**Journal:** Ann Neurol (1999)
**DOI:** [10.1002/1531-8249(199901)45:1<111::aid-art17>3.0.co;2-n](https://doi.org/10.1002/1531-8249(199901)45:1%3C111::aid-art17%3E3.0.co;2-n)

## Content

D-2-Hydroxyglutaric aciduria has been observed in patients with extremely variable clinical symptoms, creating doubt about the existence of a disease entity related to the biochemical finding. An international survey of patients with D-2-hydroxyglutaric aciduria was initiated to solve this issue. The clinical history, neuroimaging, and biochemical findings of 17 patients were studied. Ten of the patients had a severe early-infantile-onset encephalopathy characterized by epilepsy, hypotonia, cerebral visual failure, and little development.
