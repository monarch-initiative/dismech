# MYGENESET:WP_MEASLES_VIRUS_INFECTION  WP_MEASLES_VIRUS_INFECTION

**MYGENESET:WP_MEASLES_VIRUS_INFECTION** — WP_MEASLES_VIRUS_INFECTION (msigdb, C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS)

## Description

WikiPathways WP_MEASLES_VIRUS_INFECTION gene set (MSigDB C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS, 136 genes). This is a host-gene pathway set capturing the major cellular programmes engaged during measles virus (MeV) infection of human cells. Three interlinked biological axes are represented: (1) innate antiviral defence — cytoplasmic recognition of viral RNA through RIG-I (DDX58) and MDA5 (IFIH1), MAVS-mediated signalling through TBK1 to IRF3/IRF7, and induction of type I/III interferons and interferon-stimulated genes (IFIT1/2/3, MX1, OAS1/2/3, ISG15, RSAD2/viperin); (2) viral entry via host-surface receptors — SLAMF1 (CD150) on lymphoid cells and PVRL4 (nectin-4) on epithelial cells, which determine MeV tropism and underlie lymphocyte infection and subsequent depletion; and (3) measles-induced immune suppression — depletion of CD150-expressing memory T and B lymphocytes, DC-mediated T-cell silencing via IL-12 suppression, and Th1-to-Th2 immune skewing that creates the transient "immune amnesia" characteristic of measles infection. The gene set does not contain viral protein-coding sequences; the MeV V, P, and C proteins that directly antagonise type I IFN production and signalling are viral genes absent from this human host gene set by design.
