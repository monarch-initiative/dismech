Destruction of Microorganisms During Thawing of Skim Milk.

Gebre-Egziabher A(1), Thomson B(1), Blankenagel G(1).

Author information:
(1)Department of Dairy and Food Science, University of Saskatchewan, Saskatoon, 
Saskatchewan, Canada S7N OWO.

The viability of four species of microorganisms ( Escherichia coli , 
Staphylococcus aureus , Pseudomonas aeruginosa and Saccharomyces cerevisiae ) 
during rapid and slow thawing of frozen milk was investigated. Results indicated 
that the destruction of microbial cells was significantly greater when skim milk 
was thawed slowly. Recovery of viable organisms by plating was generally 
slightly higher when peptone water was used as a diluent, although differences 
were not statistically significant.
