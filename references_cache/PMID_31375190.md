Management of chronic diseases is often palliative by definition. Empowering 
primary care providers to manage symptoms and effectively prognosticate is 
necessary for this challenging population. In this article, the authors focus 
specifically on end-stage congestive heart failure, chronic obstructive 
pulmonary disease, end-stage kidney disease, and end-stage liver disease and how 
palliative principles can guide decision making and symptom management in these 
disease states. Special considerations in advance care planning, initiation and 
cessation of advanced therapies, and discussions on when to initiate hospice are 
included in this article.
