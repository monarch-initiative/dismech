Febrile ulceronecrotic Mucha-Habermann disease is a rare and severe variant of
pityriasis lichenoides, characterized by sudden onset of generalized
ulceronecrotic papules that rapidly coalesce into ulcers associated with high
fever. Systemic manifestations such as intravascular disseminated coagulation
and pulmonary, cardiac, gastrointestinal, and central nervous system involvement
are common. Treatment is based on oral corticosteroids, immunosuppressive drugs
such as methotrexate, and general supportive treatment. The present case
describes a stepwise approach to a patient with Mucha-Habermann disease with
insufficient response to methotrexate.
