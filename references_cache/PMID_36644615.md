Pituitary haemochromatosis is an endocrine disorder caused by the accumulation 
of iron due to a lack of absorption during haemochromatosis. The characteristic 
appearance on MRI is a T2 and T2* hyposignal of the anterior pituitary gland 
without enhancement, respecting the pituitary stalk and the posterior pituitary 
gland.
