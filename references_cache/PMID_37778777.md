Maternal-to-child transmission of hepatitis B virus (HBV) and hepatitis delta 
virus (HDV) can lead to the risk of progressive liver disease in infants, but 
fortunately effective interventions exist to decrease transmission. Counseling 
on the risk of maternal-to-child transmission, care pathways to decrease 
transmission, and the implications of HBV and HDV on pregnancy outcomes are the 
key components of caring for pregnant people living with HBV and HDV.
