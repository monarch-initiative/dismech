# Prevalence of erythromelalgia in the United States: a cross-sectional study using the All of Us database.
**Authors:** Sidiq SA, Asad U, Ren V
**Journal:** Arch Dermatol Res (2024)
**DOI:** [10.1007/s00403-024-03406-1](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00403-024-03406-1)

## Content

1. Arch Dermatol Res. 2024 Sep 27;316(9):646. doi: 10.1007/s00403-024-03406-1.

Prevalence of erythromelalgia in the United States: a cross-sectional study 
using the All of Us database.

Sidiq SA(1), Asad U(2), Ren V(2).

Author information:
(1)Baylor College of Medicine, Houston, TX, USA. sameer.sidiq@bcm.edu.
(2)Department of Dermatology, Baylor College of Medicine, Houston, TX, USA.

Erythromelalgia is a rare condition with no published estimates of prevalence 
from a diverse, nationwide cohort of American patients. We performed a 
cross-sectional analysis of the All of Us database to estimate the prevalence of 
erythromelalgia overall and by age groups. Of 410,361 participants, 47 were 
diagnosed with erythromelalgia, representing an overall age-adjusted prevalence 
of 10.3 (95% CI 7.4-13.3) per 100,000 people. The prevalence was higher in the 
65 + age group (16, 95% CI 9.5-23, per 100,000 people) than the less than 65 age 
group (9.1, 95% CI 5.5-13, per 100,000 people). Further studies are needed to 
determine the burden of erythromelalgia in specific subgroups, such as race, 
within the United States.
