# MYGENESET:WP_HEAD_AND_NECK_SQUAMOUS_CELL_CARCINOMA  WP_HEAD_AND_NECK_SQUAMOUS_CELL_CARCINOMA

**MYGENESET:WP_HEAD_AND_NECK_SQUAMOUS_CELL_CARCINOMA** — WP_HEAD_AND_NECK_SQUAMOUS_CELL_CARCINOMA (msigdb, C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS)

## Description

WikiPathways head and neck squamous cell carcinoma pathway gene set (MSigDB C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS, 74 genes). This is a cancer disease pathway gene set representing the principal mechanistic arms of HNSCC oncogenesis: EGFR/RTK overexpression and activation (EGFR is overexpressed in ~90% of HNSCC cases) driving downstream RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK (MAPK cascade) and PI3K/AKT/mTOR cascades that promote tumour cell proliferation and survival. Cell-cycle deregulation through loss or inactivation of TP53 and CDKN2A (p16/INK4A) together with cyclin D1 (CCND1) amplification and CDK4/6 activity allows G1/S checkpoint bypass. Apoptosis evasion via BCL2-family members and AKT-mediated survival signalling enables tumour progression, invasion, and metastasis. The gene set spans both HPV-negative (tobacco/alcohol-associated, high TP53/CDKN2A mutation load) and HPV-positive (oropharyngeal, PI3K-pathway enriched) HNSCC subtypes.
