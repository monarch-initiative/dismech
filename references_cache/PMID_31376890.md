Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is commonly associated with pulmonary disease that can 
affect any anatomic compartment of the thorax. The most common intrathoracic 
manifestations of RA include interstitial lung disease, airway disease, pleural 
disease, rheumatoid nodules, and drug-induced toxicity. Patients with RA with 
thoracic involvement often present with nonspecific respiratory symptoms, 
although many are asymptomatic. Therefore, clinicians should routinely consider 
pulmonary disease when evaluating any patient with RA, particularly one with 
known risk factors. The optimal screening, diagnostic, and treatment strategies 
for RA-associated pulmonary disease remain uncertain and are the focus of 
ongoing investigation.
