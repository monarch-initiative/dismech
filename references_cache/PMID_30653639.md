# Common Neonatal Skin Lesions: Melanocytic Nevi, Pigment Alterations, and Nonmelanocytic Nevi.
**Authors:** Habeshian KA, Kirkorian AY
**Journal:** Pediatr Ann (2019)
**DOI:** [10.3928/19382359-20181207-01](https://doi.org/10.3928/19382359-20181207-01)

## Content

1. Pediatr Ann. 2019 Jan 1;48(1):e23-e29. doi: 10.3928/19382359-20181207-01.

Common Neonatal Skin Lesions: Melanocytic Nevi, Pigment Alterations, and 
Nonmelanocytic Nevi.

Habeshian KA, Kirkorian AY.

Birthmarks are common in the healthy population and are generally harmless. 
Certain presentations, however, raise concern for associated syndromes or 
potential complications. It is important for pediatricians to be familiar with 
both harmless and potentially concerning birthmarks. This article discusses 
congenital melanocytic nevi, café-au-lait macules, hypomelanotic macules, nevus 
depigmentosus, nevus anemicus, epidermal nevi, and nevus sebaceous, including 
potential syndromes and complications. [Pediatr Ann. 2019;48(1):e23-e29.].
