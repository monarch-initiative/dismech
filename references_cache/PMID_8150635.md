# [Wissler's allergic subsepsis].
**Authors:** Fink-Puches R, Smolle J, Kerl H
**Journal:** Hautarzt (1994)
**DOI:** [10.1007/pl00013261](https://doi.org/10.1007/pl00013261)

[Wissler's allergic subsepsis].

[Article in German]

Fink-Puches R(1), Smolle J, Kerl H.

Author information:
(1)Universitätsklinik für Dermatologie und Venerologie, Graz.

A 20-year-old female patient with the typical signs of Wissler's subsepsis 
allergica (Wissler-Fanconi syndrome) is described. This rare disease is 
characterized by four typical symptoms: polymorphous exanthemas, recurrent high 
fever, leucocytosis and arthralgia. In the early stages it is difficult to 
differentiate from septicaemia. This syndrome has sometimes been considered 
equivalent to or an initial stage of Still's disease (juvenile rheumatoid 
arthritis) progressing to degenerative arthritis in many patients, whereas other 
authors have classified it as a separate entity with good prognosis. The present 
case demonstrates that Wissler's subsepsis allergica should be considered when 
ever transient polymorphous exanthema is accompanied by high recurrent fever, 
leucocytosis and arthralgia.
