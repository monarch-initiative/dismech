# Severe hypertelorism, midface prominence, prominent/simple ears, severe myopia, borderline intelligence, and bone fragility in two brothers: new syndrome?
**Authors:** Hamamy HA, Teebi AS, Oudjhane K, Shegem NN, Ajlouni KM
**Journal:** Am J Med Genet A (2007)
**DOI:** [10.1002/ajmg.a.31594](https://doi.org/10.1002/ajmg.a.31594)

## Content

1. Am J Med Genet A. 2007 Feb 1;143A(3):229-34. doi: 10.1002/ajmg.a.31594.

Severe hypertelorism, midface prominence, prominent/simple ears, severe myopia,
borderline intelligence, and bone fragility in two brothers: new syndrome?

Hamamy HA(1), Teebi AS, Oudjhane K, Shegem NN, Ajlouni KM.

Author information:
(1)National Center for Diabetes, Endocrinology and Genetics, Amman, Jordan.

We report on two brothers, born to double first cousin Jordanian Arab parents,
with a syndrome comprising severe hypertelorism with upslanted palpebral
fissures, brachycephaly, abnormal ears, sloping shoulders, enamel hypoplasia,
and osteopenia with repeated fractures. Both have severe myopia, mild to
moderate sensori-neural hearing loss and borderline intelligence. Results of
chromosome analysis were normal as was a FISH assay for subtelomeric
rearrangements. The father has mild hypertelorism but the family history is
otherwise unremarkable. We think that this represents a previously unrecognized
autosomal or X-linked recessive syndrome.
