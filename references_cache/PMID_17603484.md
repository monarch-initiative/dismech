Common variants in WFS1 confer risk of type 2 diabetes.

We studied genes involved in pancreatic beta cell function and survival, 
identifying associations between SNPs in WFS1 and diabetes risk in UK 
populations that we replicated in an Ashkenazi population and in additional UK 
studies. In a pooled analysis comprising 9,533 cases and 11,389 controls, SNPs 
in WFS1 were strongly associated with diabetes risk. Rare mutations in WFS1 
cause Wolfram syndrome; using a gene-centric approach, we show that variation in 
WFS1 also predisposes to common type 2 diabetes.
