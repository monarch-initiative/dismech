# Human CHN1 mutations hyperactivate alpha2-chimaerin and cause Duane's retraction syndrome.

Duane's retraction syndrome (DRS) is a complex congenital eye movement disorder 
caused by aberrant innervation of the extraocular muscles by axons of brainstem 
motor neurons. Studying families with a variant form of the disorder 
(DURS2-DRS), we have identified causative heterozygous missense mutations in 
CHN1, a gene on chromosome 2q31 that encodes alpha2-chimaerin, a Rac guanosine 
triphosphatase-activating protein (RacGAP) signaling protein previously 
implicated in the pathfinding of corticospinal axons in mice. We found that 
these are gain-of-function mutations that increase alpha2-chimaerin RacGAP 
activity in vitro. Several of the mutations appeared to enhance alpha2-chimaerin 
translocation to the cell membrane or enhance its ability to self-associate. 
Expression of mutant alpha2-chimaerin constructs in chick embryos resulted in 
failure of oculomotor axons to innervate their target extraocular muscles. We 
conclude that alpha2-chimaerin has a critical developmental function in ocular 
motor axon pathfinding.
