# Orthopaedic management in four cases of mucolipidosis type III.
**Authors:** Hetherington C, Harris NJ, Smith TW
**Journal:** J R Soc Med (1999)
**DOI:** [10.1177/014107689909200508](https://doi.org/10.1177/014107689909200508)

1. J R Soc Med. 1999 May;92(5):244-6. doi: 10.1177/014107689909200508.

Orthopaedic management in four cases of mucolipidosis type III.

Hetherington C(1), Harris NJ, Smith TW.

Author information:
(1)Department of Orthopaedics, Sheffield Children's Hospital, UK.

Four patients with mucolipidosis type III, three of them brothers, were seen
initially in the first two decades of life. Their main symptoms were carpal
tunnel syndrome, trigger fingers and generalized joint stiffness. Radiographs
showed spinal deformities and hip dysplasia, but these were not causing pain.
Carpal tunnel syndrome was treated surgically but joint stiffness and hip and
knee contractures were managed by physiotherapy. Up to the age of 24 none of
these patients has had pelvic osteotomy for hip dysplasia; this operation, not
yet reported in mucolipidosis type III, may eventually be necessary.
