# Real-world outcomes of FLOT4 perioperative chemotherapy in gastric and gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma.

**Authors:** Smith AB, Johnson CD, Williams EF, Brown GH, Davis IJ, Miller KL
**Journal:** Ann Oncol (2023)
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.annonc.2023.02.012](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.annonc.2023.02.012)

## Content

BACKGROUND: The FLOT4-AIO trial established perioperative FLOT (5-FU, leucovorin, oxaliplatin, docetaxel) as the standard of care for resectable locally advanced gastric and gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma. Real-world effectiveness data are limited.

METHODS: We conducted a multicenter retrospective cohort study of patients with resectable locally advanced gastric and gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma treated with FLOT perioperative chemotherapy between 2019-2022. Primary endpoint was overall survival.

RESULTS: A total of 247 patients were included (median age 64 years, 68% male). Patients treated with FLOT showed a statistically significant longer median overall survival of 57.8 vs 28.9 months (HR: 0.554, 95% CI: 0.317-0.969, P = .036) compared to historical controls receiving surgery alone. The pathological complete response rate was 18%.

CONCLUSIONS: Real-world outcomes with FLOT perioperative chemotherapy demonstrate survival benefits consistent with the FLOT4-AIO trial results, supporting its use as standard of care for resectable locally advanced gastric cancer.
