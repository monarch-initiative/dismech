In a cohort of infants, we found that lack of the Lewis histo-blood group 
antigen was associated with increased susceptibility to shigellosis. Broadly 
inhibiting fucosylation in epithelial cells in vitro decreased invasion by 
Shigella flexneri. These results support a role for fucosylated glycans in 
susceptibility to shigellosis.
