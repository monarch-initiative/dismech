Cogan's syndrome is a rare autoimmune disease that usually affects young 
Caucasian adults and is classically defined as the combination of nonsyphilitic 
interstitial keratitis and audiovestibular symptoms resembling Meniere's 
disease, both of them developed in an interval of less than two years. 
Nevertheless, cases with atypical ophthalmologic and audiovestibular features, 
with systemic manifestations or affecting children and older patients have also 
been reported, expanding the clinical spectrum of Cogan's syndrome. Herein, we 
present the case of a late-onset Cogan's syndrome associated with a large-vessel 
vasculitis.
