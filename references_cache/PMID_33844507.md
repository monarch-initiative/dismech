Vibrio cholerae is the causative agent of cholera, a diarrheal disease that 
kills tens of thousands of people each year. Cholera is transmitted primarily by 
the ingestion of drinking water contaminated with fecal matter, and a safe water 
supply remains out of reach in many areas of the world. In this Review, we 
discuss host and environmental factors that impact the susceptibility to
