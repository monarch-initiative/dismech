Sarcoidosis in Northern Ontario hard-rock miners: A case series.

Oliver LC(1)(2), Sampara P(2), Pearson D(2), Martell J(2), Zarnke AM(2)(3)(4).

Sarcoidosis is a rare multisystem granulomatous disease traditionally considered
to be of unknown etiology. The notion that sarcoidosis has no known cause is
called into question with the increasing number of case reports and
epidemiologic studies showing associations between occupational exposures and
disease published in the past 10-20 years. Occupational exposures for which
associations are strongest and most consistent are silica and other inorganic
dusts, World Trade Center (WTC) dust, and metals. Occupations identified as
at-risk for sarcoidosis include construction workers; iron-foundry and
diatomaceous earth workers; WTC emergency responders; and metal workers.
