# MYGENESET:WP_HEPATITIS_C_AND_HEPATOCELLULAR_CARCINOMA  WP_HEPATITIS_C_AND_HEPATOCELLULAR_CARCINOMA

**MYGENESET:WP_HEPATITIS_C_AND_HEPATOCELLULAR_CARCINOMA** — WP_HEPATITIS_C_AND_HEPATOCELLULAR_CARCINOMA (msigdb, C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS)

## Description

WikiPathways WP3646 gene set (MSigDB C2:CP:WIKIPATHWAYS). This pathway gene set spans two interlinked biological programmes: (1) the innate antiviral defence triggered by HCV infection — pattern recognition via RIG-I (DDX58) and MDA5 (IFIH1), MAVS-mediated signalling, IRF3/IRF7 activation, and induction of type I interferon-stimulated genes (IFIT family, MX1/2, OAS family, ISG15), together with HCV immune-evasion mechanisms (NS3/4A-mediated cleavage of MAVS and TRIF); and (2) the oncogenic reprogramming that drives hepatocellular carcinoma — Wnt/beta-catenin pathway activation (CTNNB1, APC, FZD family), MAPK/RAS proliferative signalling (KRAS, BRAF, MAP2K1, MAPK1/3), PI3K/AKT survival signalling, and dysregulation of apoptosis (TP53, BCL2 family, CASP3/9). This dual structure reflects the disease trajectory from chronic HCV infection and immune evasion through cirrhosis to hepatocellular carcinoma.
