# Two sibs with fibrochondrogenesis.
**Authors:** Leeners B, Funk A, Cotarelo CL, Sauer I

**DOI:** [10.1002/ajmg.a.20620](https://doi.org/10.1002/ajmg.a.20620)

Two sibs with fibrochondrogenesis.

Leeners B(1), Funk A, Cotarelo CL, Sauer I.

Author information:
(1)Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology at the Rhenish-Westphalian Technical 
University Hospital Aachen, Germany. Brigitte.Leeners@usz.ch

Fibrochondrogenesis is one of the rare lethal osteochondrodysplasias, which show 
abnormal maturation, and disturbed growth of cartilage and bones. These 
disorders are a heterogenous group of genetic disorders with a total incidence 
of 1-3 in 10,000 births. Only 13 cases of fibrochondrogenesis have been 
published since Lazzaroni-Fossati et al. [1978] first described the disorder. We 
report on two sibs that occurred in a consanguineous couple and discuss the 
ultrasonographic, clinical, radiological, and pathological characteristics of 
this disorder. This occurrence confirms autosomal recessive inheritance of 
fibrochondrogenesis.
