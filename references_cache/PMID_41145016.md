# Prognostic impact of pleural lavage cytology in EGFR-stratified resected non-small cell lung cancer.

INTRODUCTION: Positive pleural lavage cytology (PLC) indicates microscopic
pleural dissemination and is associated with poor prognosis; however, it is not
incorporated into the current TNM staging system and rarely guides adjuvant
therapy. The prognostic importance of PLC positivity in patients with
EGFR-mutated non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) remains unclear.
METHODS: We retrospectively analyzed clinical data from 576 patients with
pathological stage I-IIIA NSCLC who underwent curative resection between 2012
and 2019. PLC was performed intraoperatively by washing the tumor surface with
saline. Prognosis associated with PLC status was compared between the
PLC-positive and PLC-negative groups in the overall cohort and within the
EGFR-mutated (n = 188) and wild-type (n = 370) subgroups. Recurrence-free
survival (RFS) and overall survival (OS) were assessed by Kaplan-Meier and Cox
analysis.
RESULTS: PLC positivity was observed in 30 patients (5.2 %) and was
significantly associated with advanced pathological stage and pleural, vascular,
and lymphatic invasion (p < 0.05). PLC-positive patients had significantly
shorter RFS and OS in the overall cohort (23.3 and 60.0 months, respectively;
both p < 0.001) and in both the EGFR-mutated subgroup and the wild-type
subgroup. The multivariate analysis confirmed that PLC positivity was an
independent predictor for poor RFS (HR 2.13, 95 % CI: 1.27-3.59, p = 0.0043) and
OS (HR 1.77, 95 % CI: 1.00-3.13, p = 0.0484).
CONCLUSIONS: PLC positivity was identified as an independent prognostic
indicator of RFS and indicated biological aggressiveness that approximated stage
III disease in terms of early recurrence. PLC-positive, EGFR-mutated patients
might benefit from adjuvant osimertinib therapy, as supported by ADAURA
findings, although prospective validation is needed.
