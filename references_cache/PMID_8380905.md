# The gene involved in X-linked agammaglobulinaemia is a member of the src family of protein-tyrosine kinases.

X-linked agammaglobulinaemia (XLA) is a human immunodeficiency caused by failure 
of pre-B cells in the bone marrow to develop into circulating mature B cells. A 
novel gene has been isolated which maps to the XLA locus, is expressed in B 
cells, and shows mutations in families with the disorder. The gene is a member 
of the src family of proto-oncogenes which encode protein-tyrosine kinases. This 
is, to our knowledge, the first evidence that mutations in a src-related gene 
are involved in human genetic disease.
