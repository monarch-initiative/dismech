Reactive arthritis is classified as a spondyloarthropathy. Current concepts of 
disease suggest an infectious trigger, followed by inflammatory arthritis. 
Several mechanisms have been proposed to explain the interaction of host 
susceptibility and microorganism. Diagnosis relies on a compatible clinical 
syndrome and microbiologic confirmation of the pathogen. Antibiotic therapy 
seems useful in Chlamydia-triggered arthritis. The role of antibiotics in 
arthritis triggered by enteric pathogens is less clear. The role of tumor 
necrosis factor alpha inhibitors in therapy is evolving. Many patients have a 
course limited to a few months, but others experience extraarticular disease and 
more prolonged courses.
