Malignant peritoneal mesothelioma (MPM) is a rare and very aggressive malignancy
of serosal membranes, which typically presents with abdominal pain, distension,
and ascites. Due to its rarity and nonspecific symptoms, it is usually diagnosed
late, when the disease burden is extensive and the therapy is inevitably
palliative. It represents a complex challenge for clinicians because the
treatment options are very poor and the illness has a great impact on patients'
life. We present a complex case of a young patient with MPM who was admitted to
our palliative care unit.
