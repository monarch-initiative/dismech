# SHP-2 tyrosine phosphatase as an intracellular target of Helicobacter pylori CagA protein.

**Authors:** Higashi H, Tsutsumi R, Muto S, Sugiyama T, Azuma T, Asaka M, Hatakeyama M
**Journal:** Science (2002)
**DOI:** [10.1126/science.1067147](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1067147)

## Content

Helicobacter pylori CagA is the first identified bacterial protein that is translocated into host cells via bacterial type IV secretion. In gastric epithelial cells, CagA undergoes tyrosine phosphorylation by host kinases and subsequently associates with intracellular signaling molecules. Here, we report that the tyrosine-phosphorylated form of CagA specifically binds to and activates SHP-2 phosphatase, thereby inducing morphological transformation of gastric epithelial cells (hummingbird phenotype). CagA also binds to the adaptor protein Grb2, leading to activation of the ERK signaling pathway. These results indicate that CagA hijacks multiple host cell signaling pathways through protein-protein interactions and suggest that persistent tyrosine phosphorylation and signaling of CagA may be directly linked to gastric carcinogenesis.
