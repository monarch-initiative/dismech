Managing patients unable to produce sex steroids using gonadotropins to mimic 
minipuberty in hypogonadotropic hypogonadism, or sex steroids in patients with 
Klinefelter or Turner syndrome, is promising. There is a need to pursue research 
in this area, with large prospective cohorts and long-term data before these 
treatments can be routinely considered.
