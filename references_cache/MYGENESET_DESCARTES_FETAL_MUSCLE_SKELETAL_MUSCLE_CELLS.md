# MYGENESET:DESCARTES_FETAL_MUSCLE_SKELETAL_MUSCLE_CELLS  DESCARTES_FETAL_MUSCLE_SKELETAL_MUSCLE_CELLS

**MYGENESET:DESCARTES_FETAL_MUSCLE_SKELETAL_MUSCLE_CELLS** — DESCARTES_FETAL_MUSCLE_SKELETAL_MUSCLE_CELLS (msigdb, C8)

## Description

Single-cell marker signature of skeletal muscle cells (myocytes/myofibers) from human fetal skeletal muscle (Descartes atlas, MSigDB C8). Fetal skeletal muscle cells are striated, voluntary-muscle lineage cells that arise from myoblast fusion, progressively assemble sarcomeric contractile apparatus, and undergo myogenesis to form mature multinucleated myofibers. They are fundamentally distinct from cardiac muscle cells: contraction is voluntary, innervated by somatic motor neurons, and sarcomere composition is dominated by fetal/neonatal skeletal myosin heavy-chain isoforms (MYH3, MYH8) rather than cardiac isoforms (MYH6, MYH7). The fetal context means these cells are in active developmental transition, making myogenesis and fiber maturation processes biologically prominent alongside structural contractile genes.
