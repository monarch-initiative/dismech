Dientamoeba fragilis has emerged as an important and underrecognized cause of 
gastrointestinal illness. We report a familial cluster of D. fragilis associated 
with marked peripheral eosinophilia and gastrointestinal symptoms. Dientamoeba 
fragilis infection should be considered in the setting of unexplained 
eosinophilia. If confirmed, screening of household members should be considered.
