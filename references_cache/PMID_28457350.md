The 2014 to 2016 Ebola virus disease (EVD), through the sheer size of the 
outbreak and combined experience within both resource-rich and resource-poor 
settings, allowed for more information to be gained about the clinical and 
pathologic features of EVD. This review highlights the range of aspects of EVD 
that the authors find are relevant to laboratory medicine, including the need 
for robust prediagnostic and laboratory processing algorithms to inform sampling 
of suspect patients, the vast majority of whom, in resource-rich settings, will 
have another diagnosis.
