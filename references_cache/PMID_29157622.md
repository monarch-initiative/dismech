Important progress has been made in the treatment of idiopathic multicentric 
Castleman disease (iMCD) with the introduction of interleukin-6 targeting 
monoclonal antibodies. This article describes the clinical results obtained with 
different treatment modalities and uses this evidence to provide treatment 
guidelines for the practicing clinician. Much is still to be learned about the 
pathophysiology of iMCD and further research is urgently needed to develop novel 
and curative treatment approaches for all patients.
