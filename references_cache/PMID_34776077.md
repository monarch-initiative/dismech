The inherited connective tissue disorders (Marfan syndrome, Loeys-Dietz syndrome 
[LDS], and Ehlers-Danlos syndrome [EDS]) involve connective tissue of various 
organ systems. These pathologies share many common features, nonetheless 
compared to Marfan syndrome, LDS' cardiovascular manifestations tend to be more 
severe. In contrast, no association is reported between LDS and the presence of 
ectopia lentis. The EDS are currently classified into thirteen subtypes. There 
is substantial symptoms overlap between the EDS subtypes, and they are 
associated with an increased incidence of cardiovascular abnormalities, such as 
mitral valve prolapse and aortic dissection.
