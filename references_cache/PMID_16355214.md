Angiogenesis as a therapeutic target.

Ferrara N(1), Kerbel RS.

Author information:
(1)Genentech, 1 DNA Way, South San Francisco, California 94080, USA. nf@gene.com

Inhibiting angiogenesis is a promising strategy for treatment of cancer and 
several other disorders, including age-related macular degeneration. Major 
progress towards a treatment has been achieved over the past few years, and the 
first antiangiogenic agents have been recently approved for use in several 
countries. Therapeutic angiogenesis (promoting new vessel growth to treat 
ischaemic disorders) is an exciting frontier of cardiovascular medicine, but 
further understanding of the mechanisms of vascular morphogenesis is needed 
first.
