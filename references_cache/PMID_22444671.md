Mutations in the glycosylphosphatidylinositol gene PIGL cause CHIME syndrome.

Ng BG(1), Hackmann K, Jones MA, Eroshkin AM, He P, Wiliams R, Bhide S, Cantagrel 
V, Gleeson JG, Paller AS, Schnur RE, Tinschert S, Zunich J, Hegde MR, Freeze HH.

Author information:
(1)Genetic Disease Program, Sanford Children's Health Research Center,

CHIME syndrome is characterized by colobomas, heart defects, ichthyosiform 
dermatosis, mental retardation (intellectual disability), and ear anomalies, 
including conductive hearing loss. Whole-exome sequencing on five previously 
reported cases identified PIGL, the de-N-acetylase required for 
glycosylphosphatidylinositol (GPI) anchor formation, as a strong candidate. 
Furthermore, cell lines derived from these cases had significantly reduced 
levels of the two GPI anchor markers, CD59 and a GPI-binding toxin, aerolysin 
(FLAER), confirming the pathogenicity of the mutations.
