Chronic infantile neurological cutaneous and articular (CINCA) syndrome is a 
rare autosomal dominant autoinflammatory disease. We report the cases of 
monozygotic twins with CINCA syndrome whose predominant ocular manifestation was 
inflammatory rod-cone retinal dystrophy. Atypically, there were significant 
differences between twins in phenotype severity, suggestive of epigenetic 
differences and/or involvement of environmental factors.
