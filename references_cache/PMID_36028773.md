Circadian rhythms and sleep are fundamental biological processes integral to 
human health. Their disruption is associated with detrimental physiological 
consequences, including cognitive, metabolic, cardiovascular and immunological 
dysfunctions. Yet many of the molecular underpinnings of sleep regulation in 
health and disease have remained elusive. Given the moderate heritability of 
circadian and sleep traits, genetics offers an opportunity that complements 
insights from model organism studies to advance our fundamental molecular 
understanding of human circadian and sleep physiology and linked chronic disease 
biology. Here, we review recent discoveries of the genetics of circadian and 
sleep physiology and disorders with a focus on those that reveal causal 
contributions to complex diseases.
