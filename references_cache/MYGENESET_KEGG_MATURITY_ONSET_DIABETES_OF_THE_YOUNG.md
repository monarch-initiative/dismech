# MYGENESET:KEGG_MATURITY_ONSET_DIABETES_OF_THE_YOUNG  KEGG_MATURITY_ONSET_DIABETES_OF_THE_YOUNG

**MYGENESET:KEGG_MATURITY_ONSET_DIABETES_OF_THE_YOUNG** — KEGG_MATURITY_ONSET_DIABETES_OF_THE_YOUNG (msigdb, C2:CP:KEGG_LEGACY)

## Description

KEGG legacy maturity-onset diabetes of the young (MODY) pathway gene set (MSigDB C2 curated gene set). MODY is a rare monogenic, autosomal-dominant form of diabetes caused by heterozygous loss-of-function mutations in genes encoding pancreatic beta-cell transcription factors and glucokinase. The principal MODY subtypes represented in this pathway are driven by mutations in HNF4A (MODY1), GCK (MODY2), HNF1A (MODY3), PDX1 (MODY4), HNF1B (MODY5), NEUROD1 (MODY6), and PAX4 (MODY9). HNF4A, HNF1A, HNF1B, PDX1, NEUROD1, and PAX4 are RNA polymerase II transcription factors essential for endocrine pancreas development and for maintenance of mature beta-cell identity and function; their loss impairs beta-cell differentiation and glucose-stimulated insulin secretion. GCK encodes glucokinase, the pancreatic glucose sensor that couples glycolytic flux to insulin secretion. The gene set captures two principal MODY mechanisms: impaired transcriptional regulation of beta-cell identity and function (TF subtypes), and defective glucose sensing that reduces glucose-stimulated insulin secretion (GCK subtype).
