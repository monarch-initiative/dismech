Thallium (T1+) is a toxic heavy metal which was accidentally discovered by Sir 
William Crookes in 1861 by burning the dust from a sulfuric acid industrial 
plant. He observed a bright green spectral band that quickly disappeared. 
Crookes named the new element 'Thallium' (after thallos meaning young shoot). In 
1862, Lamy described the same spectral line and studied both the physical and 
chemical properties of this new element (Prick, J.J.G., 1979. Thallium 
poisoning. In: Vinkrn, P.J., Bruyn, G.W. (Eds.), Intoxication of the Nervous 
System, Handbook of Clinical Neurology, vol. 36. North-Holland, New York. pp. 
239-278).
