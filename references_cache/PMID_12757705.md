# Multiple sulfatase deficiency is caused by mutations in the gene encoding the human C(alpha)-formylglycine generating enzyme.

C(alpha)-formylglycine (FGly) is the catalytic residue in the active site of
eukaryotic sulfatases. It is posttranslationally generated from a cysteine in
the endoplasmic reticulum. The genetic defect of FGly formation causes multiple
sulfatase deficiency (MSD), a lysosomal storage disorder. We purified the FGly
generating enzyme (FGE) and identified its gene and nine mutations in seven MSD
patients. In patient fibroblasts, the activity of sulfatases is partially
restored by transduction of FGE encoding cDNA, but not by cDNA carrying an MSD
mutation. The gene encoding FGE is highly conserved among pro- and eukaryotes
and has a paralog of unknown function in vertebrates. FGE is localized in the
endoplasmic reticulum and is predicted to have a tripartite domain structure.
