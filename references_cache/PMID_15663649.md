Epidermolytic hyperkeratosis is an unusual type of ichthyosis. This inherited
keratinization disorder is characterized clinically by erythema, blistering, and
peeling shortly after birth. It may resolve and be replaced with thick scaling.
It can lead to life-threatening complications, such as sepsis. Histologically,
there is a hyperkeratosis and vacuolar degeneration. Genetically, this is an
autosomal dominant disease with complete penetrance; however, 50% are
spontaneous mutations. The clinical phenotype is a result of alterations in the
gene(s) for keratin 1 and/or 10. We review this disorder and its therapy, which
is mainly symptomatic with emollients and retinoids.
