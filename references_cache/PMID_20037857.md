# [Jeune syndrome (asphyxiating thoracic dystrophy): a case report].

Jeune syndrome or asphyxiating thoracic dystrophy is a rare autosomal recessive 
skeletal dysplasia characterized by a small thorax, short-limbed dwarfism, renal 
and hepatic anomalies. It is estimated to occur in 1 per 100.000-130.000 live 
births. Radiological confirmation of diagnosis is essential. Prognosis of the 
disease is due to the severity of chest deformities and most of the patients are 
lost in their first year because of respiratory problems. We presented a 
19-month-old female patient who admitted to our clinic because of recurrent 
lower respiratory tract infections and diagnosed as Jeune syndrome and reported 
due to its rarity.
