In this article, we review the virology, pathology, epidemiology and clinical 
spectrum of intrauterine human parvovirus B19 (B19V) infection, including 
intrauterine fetal death, non-immune hydrops fetalis, thrombocytopenia and 
neurological manifestations such as pediatric stroke and perivascular 
calcifications. In addition, we discuss the new insights into the 
neurodevelopmental outcome of intrauterine B19V infection. Current diagnosis and 
management of B19V infection is summarized, including a diagnostic and follow-up 
flowchart for practical clinical use.
