Spondylometaphyseal dysplasia with cerebral hypomyelination (SMD-H) is a very 
rare but distinctive phenotype, unusually combining spondylometaphyseal 
dysplasia with hypomyelinating leukodystrophy. Recently, SMD-H has been 
associated with variants confined to a specific intra-genic locus involving Exon 
7, suggesting that AIFM1 plays an important role in bone development and 
metabolism as well as cerebral myelination. Here we describe two further 
affected boys, one with a novel intronic variant associated with skipping of 
Exon 7 of AIFM1 and the other a synonymous variant within Exon 7 of AIFM1. We 
describe their clinical course and radiological and genetic findings, providing 
further insight into the natural history of this condition.
