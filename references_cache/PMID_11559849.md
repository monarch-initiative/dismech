# Mutation of PVRL1 is associated with sporadic, non-syndromic cleft lip/palate in northern Venezuela.
**Authors:** Sözen MA, Suzuki K, Tolarova MM, Bustos T, Fernández Iglesias JE, Spritz RA
**Journal:** Nat Genet (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1038/ng740](https://doi.org/10.1038/ng740)

## Content

1. Nat Genet. 2001 Oct;29(2):141-2. doi: 10.1038/ng740.

Mutation of PVRL1 is associated with sporadic, non-syndromic cleft lip/palate in 
northern Venezuela.

Sözen MA(1), Suzuki K, Tolarova MM, Bustos T, Fernández Iglesias JE, Spritz RA.

Author information:
(1)Human Medical Genetics Program, University of Colorado Health Sciences 
Center, Denver, Colorado, USA.

Non-syndromic cleft lip with or without cleft palate (CL/P, MIM 119530) is among 
the most common of major birth defects. Homozygosity for a nonsense mutation of 
PVRL1, W185X, results in an autosomal recessive CL/P syndrome on Margarita 
Island, CLPED1 (ref. 1). Here we demonstrate highly significant association 
between heterozygosity for this mutation and sporadic, non-syndromic CL/P in 
northern Venezuela.
