# MYGENESET:DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_ASTROCYTES  DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_ASTROCYTES

**MYGENESET:DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_ASTROCYTES** — DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_ASTROCYTES (msigdb, C8)

## Description

Single-cell marker signature of astrocytes from human fetal cerebrum (Descartes atlas). Astrocytes are the most abundant class of macroglial cells in the central nervous system and are defined by expression of glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP), a type III intermediate filament. In the developing cerebrum, astrocytes provide structural and metabolic support to neurons, clear synaptic glutamate via high-affinity excitatory amino acid transporters (EAAT1/SLC1A3, EAAT2/SLC1A2), modulate synaptic transmission, and regulate the extracellular ionic environment. Their differentiation from radial glia is a defining event of mid-to-late fetal brain development.
