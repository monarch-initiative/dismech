**Authors:** Horton WA, Rimoin DL
**Journal:** Pediatr Res (1979)
**DOI:** [10.1203/00006450-197911000-00012](https://doi.org/10.1203/00006450-197911000-00012)

## Content

1. Pediatr Res. 1979 Nov;13(11):1266-70. doi: 10.1203/00006450-197911000-00012.

Kniest dysplasia. A histochemical study of the growth plate.

Horton WA, Rimoin DL.

Chondro-osseous tissue from four patients with the Kniest dysplasia was studied 
histochemically using a new plastic embedding technique. Extensive vacuolar 
changes were observed p--1 throughout the endochondral growth plate and adjacent 
resting cartilage. These changes occurred within the cartilage matrix and also 
in the lacunae of degenerating chrondrocytes. The septa of the lesions contained 
chondroitin sulfate, but little keratan sulfate or collagen. Resting cartilage 
not adjacent to the growth plate stained irregularly and showed few of the 
vacuolar lesions, and chondrocytes were enlarged and contained cytoplasic 
inclusions, but no vacuolar material. Thus, there appears to be a sequence of 
events initiated by cellular accumulation of a substance and progressing to 
cellular and matrix degeneration.
