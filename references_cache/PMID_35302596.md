PURPOSE: KRAS mutation (MT) is a major oncogenic driver in pancreatic ductal 
adenocarcinoma (PDAC). A small subset of PDACs harbor KRAS wild-type (WT). We 
aim to characterize the molecular profiles of KRAS WT PDAC to uncover new 
pathogenic drivers and offer targeted treatments.
EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN: Tumor tissue obtained from surgical or biopsy material was 
subjected to next-generation DNA/RNA sequencing, microsatellite instability 
(MSI) and mismatch repair status determination.
RESULTS: Of the 2,483 patients (male 53.7%, median age 66 years) studied, 266 
tumors (10.7%) were KRAS WT. The most frequently mutated gene in KRAS WT PDAC 
was TP53 (44.5%), followed by BRAF (13.0%). Multiple mutations within the 
DNA-damage repair (BRCA2, ATM, BAP1, RAD50, FANCE, PALB2), chromatin remodeling 
(ARID1A, PBRM1, ARID2, KMT2D, KMT2C, SMARCA4, SETD2), and cell-cycle control 
pathways (CDKN2A, CCND1, CCNE1) were detected frequently. There was no 
statistically significant difference in PD-L1 expression between KRAS WT (15.8%) 
and MT (17%) tumors. However, KRAS WT PDAC were more likely to be MSI-high (4.7% 
vs. 0.7%; P < 0.05), tumor mutational burden-high (4.5% vs. 1%; P < 0.05), and 
exhibit increased infiltration of CD8+ T cells, natural killer cells, and 
myeloid dendritic cells. KRAS WT PDACs exhibited gene fusions of BRAF (6.6%), 
FGFR2 (5.2%), ALK (2.6%), RET (1.3%), and NRG1 (1.3%), as well as amplification 
of FGF3 (3%), ERBB2 (2.2%), FGFR3 (1.8%), NTRK (1.8%), and MET (1.3%). 
Real-world evidence reveals a survival advantage of KRAS WT patients in overall 
cohorts as well as in patients treated with gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel or 
5-FU/oxaliplatin.
CONCLUSIONS: KRAS WT PDAC represents 10.7% of PDAC and is enriched with 
targetable alterations, including immuno-oncologic markers. Identification of 
KRAS WT patients in clinical practice may expand therapeutic options in a 
clinically meaningful manner.
