Comment in
    Science. 2004 Mar 26;303(5666):1983-4. doi: 10.1126/science.1096414.

The mammalian cerebral cortex is characterized by complex patterns of anatomical 
and functional areas that differ markedly between species, but the molecular 
basis for this functional subdivision is largely unknown. Here, we show that 
mutations in GPR56, which encodes an orphan G protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) 
with a large extracellular domain, cause a human brain cortical malformation 
called bilateral frontoparietal polymicrogyria (BFPP). BFPP is characterized by 
disorganized cortical lamination that is most severe in frontal cortex. Our data 
suggest that GPCR signaling plays an essential role in regional development of 
human cerebral cortex.
