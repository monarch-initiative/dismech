Congenital melanocytic nevi are benign skin tumours with a population-based 
prevalence of 1-6%. They run an increased life-time risk of transformation into 
malignant melanoma. Additionally, they can be associated with an involvement of 
the leptomeninges (neurocutaneous melanosis), and may cause considerable 
cosmetic and psychic problems. In contrast to the past, the risk of congenital 
melanocytic nevi developing into melanoma is now regarded as well-established. 
Thus, excision is recommended whenever possible. Large lesions which do not 
allow complete excision should be treated by dermabrasion within the first weeks 
of life.
