Glaucoma is one of the leading causes of irreversible blindness worldwide. It 
has been proposed that the intraocular pressure is a causative factor in the 
development of glaucoma, which is an optic neuropathy. This paper surveys the 
use of tonometers, gonioscopes, optical coherence tomographs, scanning laser 
polarimeters, scanning laser ophthalmoscopes (also known as scanning laser 
tomographs) and corneal pachymeters for the diagnosis and management of 
glaucoma. The working mechanisms as well as the comparative advantages and 
disadvantages of each of these instruments are presented.
