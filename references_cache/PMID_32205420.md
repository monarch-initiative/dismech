# Meta-analysis of the effect of Helicobacter pylori eradication therapy on the incidence of gastric cancer.

**Authors:** Choi IJ, Kook MC, Kim YI, Cho SJ, Lee JY, Kim CG, Park B, Nam BH
**Journal:** Gastroenterology (2020)
**DOI:** [10.1053/j.gastro.2020.03.016](https://doi.org/10.1053/j.gastro.2020.03.016)

## Content

BACKGROUND & AIMS: Helicobacter pylori infection is the strongest known risk factor for gastric cancer. We performed a systematic review and meta-analysis to evaluate the effect of H pylori eradication therapy on gastric cancer incidence and mortality.

METHODS: We searched MEDLINE, EMBASE, and Cochrane databases for randomized controlled trials comparing H pylori eradication therapy with placebo or no treatment in healthy individuals or patients with peptic ulcer disease, published through December 2019. Primary outcomes were gastric cancer incidence and gastric cancer mortality.

RESULTS: We identified 10 randomized controlled trials comprising 7156 participants (3571 in eradication group, 3585 in control group). In healthy individuals, eradication therapy reduced incidence of gastric cancer (RR=0.54; 95% CI 0.40 to 0.72, NNT=72) and reduced mortality from gastric cancer (RR=0.61; 95% CI 0.40 to 0.92, NNT=135). The protective effect was greater when therapy was administered before development of precancerous lesions.

CONCLUSIONS: H pylori eradication therapy significantly reduces gastric cancer incidence and mortality in healthy individuals, with evidence of greater benefit when administered before development of premalignant lesions.
