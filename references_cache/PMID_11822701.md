# Double vagina with sex reversal, congenital diaphragmatic hernia, pulmonary and cardiac malformations--another case of Meacham syndrome.
**Authors:** Killeen OG, Kelehan P, Reardon W
**Journal:** Clin Dysmorphol (2002)
**DOI:** [10.1097/00019605-200201000-00005](https://doi.org/10.1097/00019605-200201000-00005)

## Content

1. Clin Dysmorphol. 2002 Jan;11(1):25-8. doi: 10.1097/00019605-200201000-00005.

Double vagina with sex reversal, congenital diaphragmatic hernia, pulmonary and 
cardiac malformations--another case of Meacham syndrome.

Killeen OG(1), Kelehan P, Reardon W.

Author information:
(1)National Centre for Medical Genetics, Our Lady's Hospital for Sick Children, 
Crumlin, Dublin, Ireland.

We report a female infant of 42 weeks gestation with a left sided diaphragmatic 
hernia and a hypoplastic left heart. A true double vagina, absent uterus and 
abnormal male gonads were found in the presence of normal external female 
genitalia. Conventional G band karyotyping of skin samples revealed a normal 
male karyotype. The aetiology and inheritance are unknown. We believe this to be 
the fifth reported case of a recognizable syndrome first reported by Meacham 
[(1991). Am J Med Genet 41:478-481].
