Hajdu-Cheney Syndrome (HCS) is an exceedingly rare connective tissue disorder 
primarily manifested by skeletal abnormalities including acro-osteolysis, severe 
osseous demineralization, distinctive craniofacial/dental abnormalities and 
short stature. The syndrome is caused by gain-of-function mutations in the 
NOTCH2 gene, which disrupt bone remodeling and connective tissue integrity. 
While hearing loss is a known clinical manifestation of HCS, specific imaging 
findings have not been reported. We present the case of a 36-year-old male with 
HCS who underwent high-resolution CT of the temporal bones for evaluation of 
longstanding bilateral conductive hearing loss, which showed relatively 
symmetric, diffuse osseous demineralization of the temporal bones, with 
ossicular and otic capsule involvement.ABBREVIATIONS: HCS＝ Hajdu-Cheney 
Syndrome; CT = Computed Tomography; OI = Osteogenesis Imperfecta.
