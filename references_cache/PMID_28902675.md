This article reviews advancements in the genetics of malignant hyperthermia, new 
technologies and approaches for its diagnosis, and the existing limitations of 
genetic testing for malignant hyperthermia. It also reviews the various 
RYR1-related disorders and phenotypes, such as myopathies, exertional 
rhabdomyolysis, and bleeding disorders, and examines the connection between 
these disorders and malignant hyperthermia.
