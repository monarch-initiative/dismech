**Authors:** Orens JB, Martinez FJ, Lynch JP 3rd

Pleuropulmonary manifestations of systemic lupus erythematosus.

Orens JB(1), Martinez FJ, Lynch JP 3rd.

Author information:
(1)Division of Pulmonary and Critical Care Medicine, University of Michigan 
Medical Center, Ann Arbor.

The pleuropulmonary manifestation of systemic lupus erythematous (SLE) are 
pleuritis, acute lupus pneumonitis, chronic interstitial lung disease with 
fibrosis, alveolar hemorrhage, respiratory muscle and diaphragmatic dysfunction, 
atelectasis, bronchiolitis obliterans, pulmonary vascular disease with pulmonary 
hypertension, and pulmonary embolism. This article reviews these specific 
pleuropulmonary consequences of SLE while focusing on clinical, pathologic, and 
therapeutic considerations.
