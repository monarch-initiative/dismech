Cervical cancer remains one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths 
worldwide. Here we report the extensive molecular characterization of 228 
primary cervical cancers, one of the largest comprehensive genomic studies of 
cervical cancer to date. We observed notable APOBEC mutagenesis patterns and 
identified SHKBP1, ERBB3, CASP8, HLA-A and TGFBR2 as novel significantly mutated 
genes in cervical cancer. We also discovered amplifications in immune targets 
CD274 (also known as PD-L1) and PDCD1LG2 (also known as PD-L2), and the BCAR4 
long non-coding RNA, which has been associated with response to lapatinib. 
Integration of human papilloma virus (HPV) was observed in all HPV18-related 
samples and 76% of HPV16-related samples, and was associated with structural 
aberrations and increased target-gene expression. We identified a unique set of 
endometrial-like cervical cancers, comprised predominantly of HPV-negative 
tumours with relatively high frequencies of KRAS, ARID1A and PTEN mutations. 
Integrative clustering of 178 samples identified keratin-low squamous, 
keratin-high squamous and adenocarcinoma-rich subgroups. These molecular 
analyses reveal new potential therapeutic targets for cervical cancers.
