The current monkeypox virus global spread and lack of data regarding clinical 
specimens' infectivity call for examining virus infectivity, and whether this 
correlates with results from PCR, the available diagnostic tool. We show strong 
correlation between viral DNA amount in clinical specimens and virus infectivity 
toward BSC-1 cell line. Moreover, we define a PCR threshold value (Cq ≥ 35, 
≤ 4,300 DNA copies/mL), corresponding to negative viral cultures, which may 
assist risk-assessment and decision-making regarding protective-measures and 
guidelines for patients with monkeypox.
