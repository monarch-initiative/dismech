Marfan syndrome (MFS) is an autosomal dominant connective tissue disorder 
typically involving the ocular, skeletal and cardiovascular systems, and aortic 
aneurysms/dissection mainly contributes to its mortality. Here, we performed 
genetic testing of the FBN1 gene in 39 Chinese probands with Marfan/Marfan-like 
syndrome and their related family members by Sanger sequencing. In total, 29 
pathogenic/likely pathogenic FBN1 mutations, including 17 novel ones, were 
identified. In addition, most MFS patients with aortic disease (62%) had a 
truncating or splicing mutation. These results expand the FBN1 mutation spectrum 
and enrich our knowledge of genotype-phenotype correlations. Genetic testing for 
MFS and its related aortic diseases is increasingly important for early 
intervention and treatment.
