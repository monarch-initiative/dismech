# Leigh-like syndrome with progressive cerebellar atrophy caused by novel HIBCH variants
**Authors:** Yoshihiro Taura, Takenori Tozawa, Kenichi Isoda, Satori Hirai, Tomohiro Chiyonobu, Naoko Yano, Takahiro Hayashi, Takeshi Yoshida, Tomoko Iehara
**Journal:** Human Genome Variation (2023)
**DOI:** [10.1038/s41439-023-00251-y](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41439-023-00251-y)

## Content

AbstractPathogenic variants in theHIBCHgene cause HIBCH deficiency, leading to mitochondrial disorders associated with valine metabolism. Patients typically present with symptoms such as developmental regression/delay, encephalopathy, hypotonia and dystonia. Brain magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) shows bilateral lesions in the basal ganglia with/without brainstem involvement. Here, we report a case of a Japanese patient with Leigh-like syndrome caused by novelHIBCHvariants. Long-term follow-up MRI revealed progressive cerebellar atrophy, which expands the phenotypic spectrum of HIBCH deficiency.
