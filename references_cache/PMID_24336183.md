The ribosome is one of the main antibiotic targets in the bacterial cell. 
Crystal structures of naturally produced antibiotics and their semi-synthetic 
derivatives bound to ribosomal particles have provided unparalleled insight into 
their mechanisms of action, and they are also facilitating the design of more 
effective antibiotics for targeting multidrug-resistant bacteria. In this 
Review, I discuss the recent structural insights into the mechanism of action of 
ribosome-targeting antibiotics and the molecular mechanisms of bacterial 
resistance, in addition to the approaches that are being pursued for the 
production of improved drugs that inhibit bacterial protein synthesis.
