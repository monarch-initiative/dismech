Streptococcus pneumoniae (the pneumoccus) is the leading cause of otitis media, 
community-acquired pneumonia, and bacterial meningitis. The success of the 
pneumococcus stems from its ability to persist in the population as a commensal 
and avoid killing by immune system. This chapter first reviews the molecular 
mechanisms that allow the pneumococcus to colonize and spread from one 
anatomical site to the next. Then, it discusses the mechanisms of inflammation 
and cytotoxicity during emerging and classical pneumococcal infections.
