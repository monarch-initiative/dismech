Premature ovarian failure is a major cause of female infertility. The genetic 
causes of this disorder remain unknown in most patients. Using whole-exome 
sequence analysis of a large consanguineous family with inherited premature 
ovarian failure, we identified a homozygous 1-bp deletion inducing a frameshift 
mutation in STAG3 on chromosome 7. STAG3 encodes a meiosis-specific subunit of 
the cohesin ring, which ensures correct sister chromatid cohesion. Female mice 
devoid of Stag3 are sterile, and their fetal oocytes are arrested at early 
prophase I, leading to oocyte depletion at 1 week of age.
