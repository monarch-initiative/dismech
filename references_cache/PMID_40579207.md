Ludwig angina (LA) is a diagnosis made clinically when a patient with brawny 
oedema of the submandibular region and inflammatory oedema of the floor of mouth 
is encountered. This report describes a middle-aged man who developed 
progressive swelling of the submandibular region and neck. He had restricted 
mouth opening and pharyngolaryngeal oedema on examination. CT imaging revealed 
diffuse oedema in all neck compartments with airway narrowing. He improved after 
medical management with intravenous antibiotics and steroids. Even after 
thorough investigation, the source of infection remained unidentified. The case 
highlights the need to manage the airway in all cases clinically diagnosed with 
LA, and to keep in mind that it may occur in an otherwise healthy adult with no 
comorbidities.
