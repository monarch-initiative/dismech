# Deficiency for the ER-stress transducer OASIS causes severe recessive osteogenesis imperfecta in humans.

Osteogenesis imperfecta (OI) is a clinically and genetically heterogeneous 
brittle bone disorder. Whereas dominant OI is mostly due to heterozygous 
mutations in either COL1A1 or COL1A2, encoding type I procollagen, recessive OI 
is caused by biallelic mutations in genes encoding proteins involved in type I 
procollagen processing or chaperoning. Hitherto, some OI cases remain 
molecularly unexplained. We detected a homozygous genomic deletion of CREB3L1 in 
a family with severe OI. CREB3L1 encodes OASIS, an endoplasmic reticulum-stress 
transducer that regulates type I procollagen expression during murine bone 
formation. This is the first report linking CREB3L1 to human recessive OI, 
thereby expanding the OI gene spectrum.
