Bowel has its own elegant nervous system - the enteric nervous system (ENS) 
which is a complex network of neurons and glial clones. Derived from neural 
crest cells (NCCs), this little brain controls muscle contraction, motility, and 
bowel activities in response to stimuli. Failure of developing enteric ganglia 
at the distal bowel results in intestinal obstruction and Hirschsprung disease 
(HSCR). This Review summarises the important embryological development of the 
ENS including proliferation, migration, and differentiation of NCCs. We address 
the signalling pathways which determine NCC cell fate and discuss how they are 
altered in the context of HSCR. Finally, we outline the anatomical defects and 
the mechanisms underlying gut motility in HSCR.
