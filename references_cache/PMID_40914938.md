Adenylosuccinate lyase deficiency (ADSLd) is a rare autosomal recessive purine
metabolism disorder with several clinical manifestations. While toxic substrate
accumulation is a known hallmark, no additional molecular mechanisms have been
established. Here, we show that ADSLd is associated with mitochondrial
dysfunction, including increased fragmentation, impaired respiration, and
reduced ATP production. The severity of mitochondrial impairment correlates with
ADSLd pathology, especially in mitochondria-dependent tissues. We also identify
defects in mitochondrial dynamics and transport linked to ERK2 and AKT
suppression. Notably, overexpressing constitutively active ERK2 or supplementing
purine intermediates partially rescues the mitochondrial phenotype. These
findings suggest an alternative disease mechanism and highlight mitochondrial
metabolism as a potential therapeutic target in ADSLd.
