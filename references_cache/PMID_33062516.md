Sea blue histiocytosis is an unusual bone marrow finding in many haematological 
conditions or lipid metabolic diseases that by itself may not carry any 
prognostic value. It may occur rarely as a primary genetic clinical syndrome 
characterized by splenomegaly, hypertriglyceridemia and thrombocytopenia. More 
commonly, the presence of these lipid-laden blue-stained macrophages indicates 
an underlying condition characterized by increased bone marrow precursor cell 
turnover due to myeloproliferative conditions or ineffective erythropoiesis. 
Rarely may they be observed in cases of immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP) 
incidentally due to rapid megakaryocytic turnover. Sea blue histiocytosis should 
prompt the clinician to evaluate the patient for more sinister conditions such 
as myelodysplastic syndrome or infiltrative disorders.
