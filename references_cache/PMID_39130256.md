# Metagenome Analysis of Cholesteatoma-associated Bacteria: A Pilot Study.

## Content

Introduction: Cholesteatoma usually harbors a poly-microbial infection. As the
diversity of bacterial pathogens in the Indian chronic otitis media population is
unknown, we set out to identify the bacteria associated with cholesteatoma
disease in different patients of North India using targeted metagenomic analysis
of the 16 S rRNA gene.

Methods: We recruited 15 patients of cholesteatomatous chronic otitis media,
who underwent surgical disease clearance. Representative samples were extracted
during the surgery and sent for bacterial culture and sensitivity and 16 S rRNA
gene metagenomic analysis.

Results: Our detailed bacterial metagenomics analyses showed that while phylum
Proteobacteria was most abundant in specimens from nine patients, phylum
Firmicutes was most abundant in specimens from four patients.

Conclusion: Cholesteatoma is primarily associated with Proteobacteria and
Firmicutes phyla, even in complicated disease. Further studies with a larger
sample size are required to validate our findings.
