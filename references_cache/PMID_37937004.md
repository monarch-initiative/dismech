This report presents a case of Dieulafoy's lesion (DL), a rare and serious 
gastrointestinal condition, which occurred unusually in a duodenal diverticulum 
and highlights the diagnostic and management complexities associated with it. A 
literature review of six similar cases revealed commonalities in presentation, 
diagnosis, and management, emphasizing the complexities in identifying and 
handling this rare manifestation of DL. The findings emphasize the need for 
clinical vigilance and further research into optimizing strategies for 
diagnosing and managing this rare condition.
