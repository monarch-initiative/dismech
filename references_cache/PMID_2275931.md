Photic induced sneeze is a reflex that occurs in certain individuals after
exposure to bright light. Cystinosis is an autosomal recessive inborn error of
metabolism in which nonprotein cystine accumulates within lysosomes. The
pathognomonic ocular manifestation of cystinosis is corneal crystal deposition.
We observed photic induced sneezes during ophthalmoscopic examination in five of
19 patients with nephropathic cystinosis (26%). We report on this observation
and discuss possible pathophysiological mechanisms for photic induced sneezing
in cystinosis.
