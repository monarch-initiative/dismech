Differential diagnosis of juvenile hemochromatosis along with hemolytic anemia 
is often difficult. We report a 23-year-old woman with macrocytic hemolytic 
anemia with iron overload. The patient showed high serum ferritin and 
transferrin saturation and low serum transferrin and ceruloplasmin. We also 
noticed stomatocytes in her blood smear, which was confirmed by scanning 
electron microscopy. Target gene sequencing identified a mutation in PIEZO1 
(heterozygous c.6008C>A: p.A2003D). This mutation was reported previously in a 
family with dehydrated hereditary stomatocytosis (DHS1, [OMIM 194380]), but in 
the current case, it was identified to be a de novo mutation. We underscore DHS1 
in the differential diagnosis of iron overload associated with non-transfused 
hemolytic anemia in children and young adults.
