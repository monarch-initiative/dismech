In multiple sclerosis (MS), disease course is defined by a subclinical or 
clinical relapsing remitting phase, a progressive phase, and the overlapping 
phase in-between. Each phase can have intermittently active or inactive periods. 
Subclinical activity in radiologically isolated syndrome evolving to 
primary-progressive MS is mostly indistinguishable from relapsing-remitting MS 
evolving to secondary-progressive MS. The onset of progressive-phase MS is 
age-dependent but time and pre-progressive phase agnostic. Pathologic hallmarks 
of progressive MS onset also appear to be age-dependent but pre-progressive 
phase agnostic. Onset of progressive MS is characterized by a peak in smoldering 
plaques.
