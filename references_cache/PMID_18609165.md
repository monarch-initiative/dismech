Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth.

Rana SV(1), Bhardwaj SB.

Author information:
(1)Department of Gastroenterology, Postgraduate Institute of Medical Education
and Research, Chandigarh, India.

Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) syndrome is characterized in its
florid form by diarrhoea and weight loss. The most common underlying factors are
dysmotility, small intestinal obstruction, blind or afferent loops. Small
intestinal bacterial overgrowth can be diagnosed by: 1) culture of jejunum
aspirate for bacterial counts, 2) 14C-D-xylose breath testing, 3) non-invasive
hydrogen breath testing using glucose or lactulose or 4) 14C-glycocholic acid
breath testing. The treatment usually consists of the eradication of bacterial
overgrowth with repeated course of antimicrobials, correction of associated
nutritional deficiencies and, when possible, correction of the underlying
predisposing conditions.
