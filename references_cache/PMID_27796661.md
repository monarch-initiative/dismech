Acro-osteolysis is an osteolysis of the distal phalanges of the hands and feet 
and can affect the terminal tuft or the shaft of the distal phalanx (transverse 
or band acro-osteolysis). It is often associated with distal digital ischemia, 
digital calcinosis, or severe sensory neuropathy. Acro-osteolysis has been 
associated with a heterogeneous group of disorders, including occupational 
activities, infections, rheumatic disorders (systemic sclerosis, psoriatic 
arthritis), endocrinopathies, genetic disorders, and lysosomal storage 
disorders. Plain radiography is the gold standard for the detection of 
acro-osteolysis.
