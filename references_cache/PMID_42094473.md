Resolving human neuronal herpesvirus reactivation via petabase-scale association
studies.

Gutierrez JC, Chen Y, Babaian A, Dhindsa RS, Lareau CA.

Mounting evidence implicates herpesvirus reactivation in the etiology of
Alzheimer's disease, yet we lack a refined molecular characterization of
pathogenesis in neurodegeneration. Here, we mine over 10 petabytes of human
sequencing data for viral transcripts, identifying recurrent herpes simplex
virus 1 (HSV-1) reactivation in healthy but not pathological post-mortem human
brain tissue. Integrative single-nucleus analyses resolve direct evidence of
HSV-1 expression in RORB+ glutamatergic neurons, implicating viral reactivation
in a neuronal population progressively lost during dementia.
