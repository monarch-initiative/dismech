Wilms tumor (WT), or nephroblastoma, is the most common primary malignant renal 
tumor of childhood. It is an embryonal tumor that develops from remnants of 
immature kidney. There are approximately 500 new WT cases diagnosed in the 
United States every year. Advances in multimodal therapy including surgery, 
chemotherapy, and radiation therapy given according to risk stratification have 
allowed most patients to achieve survival rates in excess of 90%.
