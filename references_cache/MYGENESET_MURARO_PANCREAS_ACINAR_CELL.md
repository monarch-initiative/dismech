# MYGENESET:MURARO_PANCREAS_ACINAR_CELL  MURARO_PANCREAS_ACINAR_CELL

**MYGENESET:MURARO_PANCREAS_ACINAR_CELL** — MURARO_PANCREAS_ACINAR_CELL (msigdb, C8)

## Description

Single-cell marker signature of pancreatic acinar cells (exocrine, digestive- enzyme-secreting) from human pancreas (Muraro et al. single-cell atlas of the human pancreas, MSigDB C8). Pancreatic acinar cells are the predominant exocrine cell type, constituting approximately 80% of pancreatic mass. Their defining function is the synthesis of digestive enzyme precursors (zymogens), storage in apical zymogen granules, and regulated secretion into the pancreatic duct lumen in response to cholecystokinin (CCK) and neural stimuli. Secreted enzymes include serine proteases (trypsinogen, PRSS1/PRSS2; chymotrypsinogen, CTRB1/CTRB2; elastase, CELA3A/CELA2A), alpha-amylase (AMY2A), pancreatic lipase (PNLIP), and phospholipase A2 (PLA2G1B).
