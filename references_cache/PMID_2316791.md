**Journal:** Am J Trop Med Hyg (1990)

1. Am J Trop Med Hyg. 1990 Mar;42(3):215-21. doi: 10.4269/ajtmh.1990.42.215.

An epidemic of Oroya fever in the Peruvian Andes.

Gray GC(1), Johnson AA, Thornton SA, Smith WA, Knobloch J, Kelley PW, Obregon
Escudero L, Arones Huayda M, Wignall FS.

Author information:
(1)Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, Washington, DC.

Between February and October 1987, a febrile illness killed 14 persons and
seriously affected at least 14 others in Shumpillan, a remote Peruvian mountain
village of 353 people. The illness was characterized by fever, headache, chills,
and pallor. The fatality rate of untreated cases was 88%. The patients, 71% of
whom were male, were 1-75 years of age. Fatal illnesses progressed from lethargy
to coma to death in 3-60 days. Patients treated empirically with chloramphenicol
survived. Bartonella bacilliformis was isolated from the whole blood of 3
patients. A serologic study revealed a high prevalence of antibodies to B.
bacilliformis in the villagers. It is concluded that the villagers suffered from
an epidemic of Oroya fever.
