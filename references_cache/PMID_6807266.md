Vitreoretinal degeneration in spondyloepiphyseal dysplasia congenita.

Hamidi-Toosi S, Maumenee IH.

Eighteen patients had ocular changes associated with spondyloepiphyseal 
dysplasia congenita, a rare cause of congenital dwarfism with normal mental 
development. Seven patients had nonprogressive myopia of 5.00 or more diopters. 
Vitreoretinal degeneration was encountered in six patients with high myopia, and 
vitreous syneresis was present in all patients. Corrected visual acuity was 
20/50 or better in all patients. Retinal detachment was not encountered, 
although reports in the nonophthalmic literature claim up to 50% retinal 
detachment rate and poor visual prognosis in these patients.
