ALAD porphyria is an autosomal recessive disorder resulting from a homozygous
aminolevulinic acid dehydratase (ALAD) deficiency. Because of an almost complete
lack of ALAD activity, patients excrete a large amount of ALA, but not PBG, into
urine. The symptoms in this disease are similar to those seen in AIP, but ALAD
porphyria can be differentiated from AIP by its autosomal recessive, rather than
dominant, inheritance, by the lack of PBG overproduction, and by markedly
decreased ALAD activity.
