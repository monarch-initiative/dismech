# MYGENESET:HAY_BONE_MARROW_MONOCYTE  HAY_BONE_MARROW_MONOCYTE

**MYGENESET:HAY_BONE_MARROW_MONOCYTE** — HAY_BONE_MARROW_MONOCYTE (msigdb, C8)

## Description

Single-cell marker signature of monocytes from human bone marrow (Hay et al., MSigDB C8 cell-type marker collection). Monocytes are innate mononuclear phagocytes that develop in the bone marrow, circulate in blood, and patrol peripheral tissues as a first line of defence against pathogens and cellular debris. They phagocytose microorganisms and foreign material, produce pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6, IL-12), and can differentiate into macrophages or dendritic cells upon tissue entry. The bone marrow monocyte signature captures both mature monocyte identity genes and residual differentiation-stage markers reflecting the developmental context of the source tissue.
