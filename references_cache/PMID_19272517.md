Sudden falling with loss of consciousness from syncope and symptoms of 
orthostatic intolerance are common, dramatic clinical problems of diverse cause, 
but cerebral hypoperfusion is the ultimate mechanism in most. Cardiac, reflex, 
and orthostatic hypotension are important forms to consider. Syncope must be 
differentiated from seizures, psychiatric events, drop attacks, and other 
mimics. However, factors such as syncopal induced movements, ictal bradycardia, 
and insufficient clinical information can confound accurate diagnosis and hamper 
appropriate treatment. Progress in the diagnosis, treatment, and understanding 
of underlying mechanisms is continually advancing.
