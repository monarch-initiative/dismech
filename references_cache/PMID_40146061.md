# Stickler syndrome: associated musculoskeletal manifestations and first population-based incidence
**Authors:** Rehm A, Newton AC, Ashby E, et al.
**Journal:** J Pediatr Orthop B (2025)
**DOI:** [10.1097/BPB.0000000000001180](https://doi.org/10.1097/BPB.0000000000001180)

## Content

Stickler syndrome: associated musculoskeletal manifestations and first
population-based incidence.

J Pediatr Orthop B. 2025 May 1;34(3):294-304. doi:
10.1097/BPB.0000000000001180. Epub 2025 Mar 25.

The objective of this study is to identify musculoskeletal manifestations
associated with Stickler syndrome from an inclusive literature review and to
perform the first population study to establish its incidence. We reviewed all
for us retrievable publications linked to Stickler syndrome listed in PubMed
and publications quoted within these publications going back to 1954.

From our medical record data bases, we identified all patients with the
diagnosis of Stickler syndrome out of a population of consecutive live births
born in our maternity hospital between 2000 and 2016. We identified 504
publications of which we managed to obtain and review 487, listing 6182
patients with Stickler syndrome.

We identified four patients with Stickler syndrome out of 87 378 consecutive
live births, giving an incidence of 1 in 21 844 live births. Patients with
Stickler syndrome present with a large variety of musculoskeletal
manifestations.
