Ataxia-telangiectasia (AT) is a rare genetic disorder leading to neurological
defects, telangiectasias, and immunodeficiency. We aimed to study the clinical
and immunological features of Latin American patients with AT and analyze
factors associated with mortality. Referral centers from 9 Latin American
countries participated in this retrospective cohort study, and 218 patients were
included.  Median (IQR) ages at symptom onset and diagnosis were 1.0 (1.0-2.0) 
and 5.0 (3.0-8.0) years, respectively. Most patients presented recurrent airway
infections, which was significantly associated with IgA deficiency. IgA
deficiency was observed in 60.8% of patients and IgG deficiency in 28.6%. T- and
B-lymphopenias were also present in most cases. Mean survival was 24.2 years,
and Kaplan-Meier 20-year-survival rate was 52.6%, with higher mortality
associated with female gender and low IgG levels. These findings suggest that
immunologic status should be investigated in all patients with AT.
