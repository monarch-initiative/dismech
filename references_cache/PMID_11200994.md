# Schneckenbecken dysplasia, radiology, and histology.
**Authors:** Nikkels PG, Stigter RH, Knol IE, van der Harten HJ
**Journal:** Pediatr Radiol (2001)
**DOI:** [10.1007/s002470000357](https://doi.org/10.1007/s002470000357)

Schneckenbecken dysplasia, radiology, and histology.

Nikkels PG(1), Stigter RH, Knol IE, van der Harten HJ.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pathology, University Medical Centre Utrecht, The Netherlands. 
p.g.j.nikkels@azu.nl

To our knowledge this is the first report of Schneckenbecken dysplasia with the 
development of hydrops early in the second trimester. The radiological findings 
showed the typical hypoplastic iliac bones with medial extension and very 
flattened, on lateral view, oval-shaped vertebral bodies and short long bones. 
The histology showed hypercellular and hypervascular cartilage with chondrocytes 
with centrally located nucleus. The absence of the lacunar space as described 
before was also observed in some chondrocytes in our case. This male fetus was 
the product of consanguineous parents of Mediterranean origin compatible with 
autosomal recessive inheritance.
