Dengue infection classically presents with fever, thrombocytopaenia, and varying 
degrees of plasma leakage, giving rise to shock. However, a myriad of other 
manifestations, involving the cardiovascular system, the nervous system, the 
liver, the kidneys, the gut and the haematological system have been reported in 
dengue. This review summarizes these varied presentations.
