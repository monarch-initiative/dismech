Bohring-Opitz syndrome is characterized by severe intellectual disability, 
distinctive facial features and multiple congenital malformations. We sequenced 
the exomes of three individuals with Bohring-Opitz syndrome and in each 
identified heterozygous de novo nonsense mutations in ASXL1, which is required 
for maintenance of both activation and silencing of Hox genes. In total, 7 out 
of 13 subjects with a Bohring-Opitz phenotype had de novo ASXL1 mutations, 
suggesting that the syndrome is genetically heterogeneous.
