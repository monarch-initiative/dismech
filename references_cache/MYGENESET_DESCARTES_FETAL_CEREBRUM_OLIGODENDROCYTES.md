# MYGENESET:DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_OLIGODENDROCYTES  DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_OLIGODENDROCYTES

**MYGENESET:DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_OLIGODENDROCYTES** — DESCARTES_FETAL_CEREBRUM_OLIGODENDROCYTES (msigdb, C8)

## Description

Single-cell marker signature of oligodendrocytes from human fetal cerebrum (Descartes atlas, MSigDB C8). Oligodendrocytes are the myelinating glia of the central nervous system, responsible for wrapping axons in compact myelin sheaths that enable fast saltatory impulse conduction. In the fetal cerebrum, oligodendrocyte lineage cells range from proliferating progenitors to committed precursors (OPCs) and nascent myelinating oligodendrocytes. Their marker genes include the major myelin structural proteins (MBP, PLP1, MOG, MOBP), transcription factors governing lineage specification (OLIG1, OLIG2, SOX10), and ribosomal/translational components reflecting the high biosynthetic demand of myelin membrane production.
