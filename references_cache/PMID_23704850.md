Apical hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) is an atypical phenotype of 
nonobstructive HCM with an indistinguishable histology. In Japan this apical 
variant constitutes ap-proximately 25% of patients with HCM. Electrocardiogram 
typically shows repolarization changes and giant (>10 mm), inverted T waves in 
the anterolateral leads. Transthoracic echocardiography is the initial test of 
choice in making the diagnosis
