Cogan syndrome, a rare multisystemic autoimmune vasculitis, can result in 
coronaritis and precipitate acute coronary syndrome. This paper reports a case 
of a young woman affected by Cogan syndrome with acute coronary syndrome due to 
a left main lesion and the complexities in selecting the revascularization 
strategy for concomitant active vasculitis.
