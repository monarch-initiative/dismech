Tyler K(1), Sarioglu N, Kunze J.

Author information:
(1)Kinderklinik, Institut für Humangenetik, Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin, 
Germany.

We describe a family with two marriages of first cousins and a total of five 
children with opsismodysplasia. The diagnosis was based on clinical, 
radiological, and immunhistochemical findings. Helpful to the diagnosis was the 
testing with type I collagen antibodies, showing abnormally high levels in the 
hypertrophic area of growth cartilage. This observation supports the hypothesis 
of autosomal recessive transmission of opsismodysplasia.
