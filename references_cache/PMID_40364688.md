Limb-girdle muscular dystrophy (LGMD) is a genetically heterogeneous group of 
muscle disorders characterized by progressive muscle atrophy and loss of motor 
function. Over 30 subtypes have been identified and classified into two main 
inheritance patterns: autosomal dominant and autosomal recessive. Besides 
primarily affecting skeletal muscle, certain subtypes also impact the cardiac 
and respiratory muscles, significantly influencing disease progression and 
patient survival. Substantial progress has been made in understanding the 
pathogenic genes and molecular mechanisms of LGMD; however, developing 
disease-modifying therapies remains challenging due to genetic heterogeneity, 
limitations in gene delivery technologies, and secondary pathological 
complications. Current treatments are primarily supportive, focusing on symptom 
management and improving quality of life rather than addressing the underlying 
cause. This paper summarizes recent advances in LGMD pathogenesis and emerging 
therapeutic strategies, highlighting progress and remaining challenges in the 
field.
