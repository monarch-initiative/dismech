Enterovirus D68 (EV-D68) causes respiratory illness in children. It also causes
severe paralysis called acute flaccid myelitis (AFM), which has become a global
health threat. Here, we generated an mRNA vaccine expressing virus-like
particles (VLPs) of EV-D68. We found that the mRNA vaccine elicited potent
neutralizing antibodies against EV-D68 in the blood, and the neutralizing titer
was superior to that of the inactivated whole virion (IWV) vaccine. The mRNA
vaccine showed protective effects against intranasal challenge with EV-D68, and
antisera from the vaccinated mice prevented the paralysis caused by EV-D68
infection in neonatal mice. Moreover, the mRNA vaccine induced neutralizing
antibodies in the respiratory tract, which is the entry site for EV-D68.
Additionally, it attenuated infection with coxsackievirus B3 (CVB3), which
belongs to another enterovirus group, via CD8+ T cell responses. In conclusion,
our results suggest that this mRNA vaccine is a promising candidate for EV-D68
prevention.
