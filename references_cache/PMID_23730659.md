Bilateral primary fallopian tube carcinoma is a rare diagnosis in our 
gynaecological practice. Very often, the diagnosis is mistaken for an ovarian 
carcinoma or a tubo-ovarian mass. A correct, preoperative diagnosis is made only 
in 4% of the cases. This is due to the nonspecific symptoms. The Latzko's triad 
of a watery vaginal discharge, a colicky lower abdominal pain and a pelvic mass 
is typical of a fallopian tube carcinoma, but this triad is noted only in less 
than 15% of the patients. Here, we are reporting such a case where the patient 
presented with all the classical features of a primary fallopian tube carcinoma. 
This case is also being reported for it's extreme rarity.
