Two cases of Van Buchem's disease.

Dixon JM, Cull RE, Gamble P.

A brother and sister suffering from hyperostosis corticalis generalisata 
familiaris (van Buchem's disease) are described. Both presented in early adult 
life with signs and symptoms of raised intracranial pressure and underwent 
partial craniectomy. Following surgery normal intellectual function was 
maintained and both survived to old age. In later life one showed cerebellar 
deficit due to bony encroachment of the posterior cranial fossa, while the other 
had a spastic paraparesis due to spinal cord compression. Several other siblings 
were affected by this disease which appears to be transmitted as an autosomal 
recessive gene.
