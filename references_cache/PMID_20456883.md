# A long-term survival case of arginase deficiency with severe multicystic white matter and compound mutations.

A long-term survival case of arginase deficiency with severe multicystic white
matter and compound mutations.

Segawa Y(1), Matsufuji M, Itokazu N, Utsunomiya H, Watanabe Y, Yoshino M,
Takashima S.

Author information:
(1)Yanagawa Institute for Developmental Disabilities, Fukuoka, Japan.

Neuropathology and neuroimaging of long-term survival cases of arginase
deficiency are rarely reported. The magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of our case
showed severe multicystic white matter lesions with cortical atrophy, which were
more severe compared with previous reports. In this patient, low-protein diet
successfully reduced hyperammonemia, but hyperargininemia persisted. These
severe neurological and MRI findings may be explained by a compound
heterozygote, inheriting both of severe mutant alleles from her parents.
