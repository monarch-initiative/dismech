# Chondrodysplasia punctata, tibia-metacarpal (MT) type.
**Authors:** Rittler M, Menger H, Spranger J
**Journal:** Am J Med Genet (1990)
**DOI:** [10.1002/ajmg.1320370208](https://doi.org/10.1002/ajmg.1320370208)

1. Am J Med Genet. 1990 Oct;37(2):200-8. doi: 10.1002/ajmg.1320370208.

Chondrodysplasia punctata, tibia-metacarpal (MT) type.

Rittler M(1), Menger H, Spranger J.

Author information:
(1)Department of Pediatrics, University of Mainz, Federal Republic of Germany.

We describe 7 patients with a new form of chondrodysplasia punctata. Its 
principal clinical manifestations are flat midface and nose, short limbs, and 
otherwise normal development. Consistent radiologic manifestations in the 
newborn infant are discrete calcific stippling, coronal clefts of vertebral 
bodies, short tibiae, and shortness of the 2nd and 3rd metacarpal bones. 
Radiologic findings in the older child include shortness of tibiae and the 3rd 
and 4th metacarpals.
