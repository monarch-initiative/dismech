Common Variable Immunodeficiency

Complex MONDO:0015517 Primary Immunodeficiency Antibody Deficiency Disorder

Common variable immunodeficiency (CVID) is the most prevalent symptomatic primary immunodeficiency, characterized by defective B cell differentiation, hypogammaglobulinemia, and failure of antibody production. Patients present with recurrent bacterial infections, particularly of the respiratory and gastrointestinal tracts, and have increased susceptibility to autoimmune manifestations, granulomatous disease, and lymphoproliferative disorders. CVID is best understood as an umbrella diagnosis with heterogeneous genetic and immunologic etiologies spanning monogenic, digenic, and polygenic models.

Pathophysiology

4
Defective B Cell Differentiation and Class-Switch Recombination
The hallmark of CVID is a failure of B cells to differentiate into immunoglobulin-secreting plasma cells. Defective class-switch recombination leads to impaired production of IgG, IgA, and often IgM. Most patients show reduced switched memory B cells (CD27+IgD-IgM-). Defects in B cell receptor signaling, BAFF/APRIL axis, and NF-kB signaling all converge on impaired germinal center output.

PMID:16007086 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"B cells from individuals with TACI mutations expressed TACI but did not produce IgG and IgA in response to the TACI ligand APRIL, probably reflecting impaired isotype switching."
Demonstrates that TACI mutations in CVID patients lead to impaired isotype switching, a key mechanism of defective class-switch recombination.
T Cell Dysregulation
Many CVID patients exhibit T cell abnormalities including reduced naive CD4+ T cells, expanded CD21low B cells, impaired T cell proliferation, and skewed T helper cell responses. T follicular helper cell dysfunction contributes to impaired germinal center reactions and defective B cell help. ICOS deficiency specifically impairs T cell help for late B cell differentiation and memory B cell generation.

Immune Dysregulation and Autoimmunity
Approximately 25-50% of CVID patients develop autoimmune complications, most commonly autoimmune cytopenias (immune thrombocytopenia and autoimmune hemolytic anemia). This reflects a breakdown of peripheral tolerance and defective regulatory T cell function, particularly through the CTLA-4/LRBA checkpoint axis. Expansion of autoreactive CD21low B cells contributes to antibody-mediated autoimmunity.

Granulomatous Disease and GLILD
CVID-associated granulomatous and lymphocytic interstitial lung disease (GLILD) involves poorly organized granulomas with abundant T and B cells. Key features include Th1 polarization with elevated IFN-gamma and sIL-2R, B cell abnormalities with elevated BAFF and CD21low B cells, and innate immune alterations including M2 macrophage skewing and neutrophil dysregulation.

Treatments

3
Immunoglobulin Replacement Therapy MAXO:0000058
Lifelong intravenous (IVIG) or subcutaneous (SCIG) immunoglobulin replacement is the cornerstone of CVID treatment, reducing the frequency and severity of infections.
Show evidence (1 reference)
DOI:10.3390/jcm13061717 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"It assesses the efficacy of prophylactic measures for COVID-19, including vaccination and immunoglobulin replacement therapy, as well as trialled therapies."
Confirms immunoglobulin replacement therapy as a standard treatment modality for CVID patients.
Antibiotic Prophylaxis and Treatment MAXO:0000058
Prophylactic and therapeutic antibiotics are used to manage recurrent bacterial infections, particularly sinopulmonary infections.
Immunosuppressive Therapy for Autoimmune Complications MAXO:0000058
Corticosteroids, rituximab, and other immunosuppressants are used to manage autoimmune cytopenias and granulomatous disease. Treatment selection is increasingly aligned to biological mechanisms: rituximab for B cell hyperplasia, abatacept for CTLA-4/LRBA biology, and sirolimus for mTOR/Th1 programs.
