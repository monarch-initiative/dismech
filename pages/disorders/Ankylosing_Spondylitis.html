Ankylosing Spondylitis

Autoimmune MONDO:0005306 Autoimmune Disease Spondyloarthropathy

A chronic inflammatory disease primarily affecting the axial skeleton, characterized by sacroiliitis and progressive spinal fusion. Strongly associated with HLA-B27. Part of the spondyloarthritis spectrum including enthesitis and potential peripheral joint and extra-articular involvement.

Pathophysiology

3
HLA-B27-Associated Immune Activation
HLA-B27 misfolding in the endoplasmic reticulum triggers the unfolded protein response and IL-23 production. Aberrant antigen presentation may also activate autoreactive T cells against joint antigens.
Antigen Presentation link
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:33692806 SUPPORT
"The unfolded protein response (UPR) theory postulates that unconventional HLA-B27 variants homodimerize instead of heterodimerize; the misfolded proteins accumulate in the intracellular compartment triggering endoplasmic reticulum stress and increasing IL-23 production."
Evidence supporting HLA-B27 misfolding mechanism that triggers ER stress and activates IL-23 production in ankylosing spondylitis.
IL-23/IL-17 Axis Activation
IL-23-driven expansion of Th17 cells and innate lymphoid cells produces IL-17, driving inflammation at entheses and in the spine. This pathway is a key therapeutic target.

Review evidence confirming the central role of IL-23/IL-17 axis in ankylosing spondylitis pathogenesis and other autoimmune diseases.
PMID:33692806 SUPPORT
"The fundamental role played by the IL-23/IL-17 axis comes from several lines of evidence depicting a clear increase in IL-23 and IL-17 levels in the sera of AS patients (113). This observation is coupled with the increased number of Th17 cells in peripheral blood from AS patients (114)."
Direct evidence of elevated IL-23 and IL-17 levels and increased Th17 cells in ankylosing spondylitis patients.
Enthesitis and New Bone Formation
Inflammation at entheses (tendon/ligament insertions) leads to erosion followed by aberrant bone formation. Syndesmophytes and ankylosis result from dysregulated osteoblast activity driven by Wnt and BMP signaling.
