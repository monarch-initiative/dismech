Ogden syndrome

Mendelian MONDO:0010457 Pathograph 4 NAA10-related syndrome X-linked genetic disorder

Ogden syndrome is a rare X-linked recessive NAA10-related disorder caused classically by the recurrent hemizygous p.Ser37Pro missense variant in NAA10. NAA10 encodes the catalytic subunit of the NatA N-terminal acetyltransferase complex. The syndrome is characterized by severe developmental impairment, hypotonia, postnatal growth failure, prematurely aged appearance, structural cardiac defects, and cardiac arrhythmias with early lethality in the classic male phenotype.

Pathophysiology

3
Impaired NatA catalytic activity and complex assembly
The recurrent NAA10 p.Ser37Pro variant disrupts the catalytic function of the NatA complex and weakens interactions between NAA10 and its binding partners NAA15 and NAA50. Because NatA is the major N-terminal acetyltransferase in human cells, this defect reduces co-translational N-terminal acetylation of selected substrates and establishes the primary molecular lesion in Ogden syndrome.

Differential Diagnoses

2

Conditions with similar clinical presentations that must be differentiated from Ogden syndrome:

NAA10-related syndrome Not Yet Curated MONDO:0100124
Overlapping Features Ogden syndrome represents the severe classic end of the broader NAA10-related syndrome spectrum and must be distinguished from milder NAA10-associated neurodevelopmental phenotypes in females and in individuals with non-Ser37Pro variants.
Distinguishing Features
  • The recurrent hemizygous NAA10 p.Ser37Pro genotype with early lethality, progeroid appearance, and prominent cardiac disease favors classic Ogden syndrome.
  • Broader NAA10-related syndrome typically includes more variable and often milder developmental phenotypes across both sexes.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:30054457 SUPPORT Other
"NAA10-related syndrome is an X-linked condition with a broad spectrum of findings ranging from a severe phenotype in males with p.Ser37Pro in NAA10, originally described as Ogden syndrome, to the milder NAA10-related intellectual disability found with different variants in both males and females."
This review explicitly places Ogden syndrome within the broader NAA10-related syndrome spectrum and supports that distinction as a clinically relevant differential diagnosis.
Donohue syndrome Not Yet Curated MONDO:0009517
Overlapping Features Donohue syndrome can resemble Ogden syndrome in infancy through severe failure to thrive, progeroid appearance, and multisystem illness, but is distinguished by extreme insulin resistance rather than NAA10-related developmental disease.
Distinguishing Features
  • Severe insulin resistance with hyperinsulinemia favors Donohue syndrome.
  • Congenital heart disease, arrhythmia risk, and a pathogenic NAA10 variant favor Ogden syndrome.
