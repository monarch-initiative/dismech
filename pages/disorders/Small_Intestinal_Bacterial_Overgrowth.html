Pathophysiology

1
Bacterial overgrowth in small intestine
Excessive bacterial colonization of the small intestine leads to malabsorption, nutrient deficiencies, and gastrointestinal symptoms. Disruption of gastric acid, motility (including migrating motor complex), and the ileocecal barrier promotes overgrowth. Bacterial fermentation produces hydrogen, methane, and short-chain fatty acids while bile acid deconjugation impairs fat absorption.
