Diastrophic Dysplasia

Mendelian MONDO:0009107 Skeletal Dysplasia

Diastrophic dysplasia is an autosomal recessive skeletal dysplasia caused by biallelic pathogenic variants in SLC26A2. Deficient sulfate transport causes proteoglycan undersulfation in cartilage matrix, disrupting endochondral ossification and producing a characteristic pattern of limb shortening, joint contractures, spinal deformity, and hand/foot malformations.

Sulfate Transport Defect in Cartilage
Loss of SLC26A2 transporter activity reduces intracellular sulfate in chondrocytes and causes undersulfation of cartilage proteoglycans, altering extracellular matrix composition.
