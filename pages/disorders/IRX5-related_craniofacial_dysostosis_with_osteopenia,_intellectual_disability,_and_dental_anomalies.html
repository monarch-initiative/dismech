IRX5-related craniofacial dysostosis with osteopenia, intellectual disability, and dental anomalies

Mendelian MONDO:0012634 Pathograph 5

IRX5-related craniofacial dysostosis with osteopenia, intellectual disability, and dental anomalies is a very rare autosomal recessive developmental disorder in the Hamamy syndrome spectrum caused by biallelic deleterious variants in IRX5, which encodes the homeobox transcription factor IRX5. The syndrome combines distinctive craniofacial dysmorphism with dental enamel or dentin defects, osteopenia with bone fragility, severe myopia, and variable developmental delay or intellectual disability. Current mechanistic evidence supports impaired craniofacial progenitor migration via dysregulated SDF1/CXCL12 signaling together with defective osteogenic differentiation and cranial mineralization.

PMID:17230486 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"We report on two brothers, born to double first cousin Jordanian Arab parents, with a syndrome comprising severe hypertelorism with upslanted palpebral fissures, brachycephaly, abnormal ears, sloping shoulders, enamel hypoplasia, and osteopenia with repeated fractures."
The original clinical report defines the recognizable craniofacial, dental, and skeletal syndrome later molecularly linked to IRX5.
PMID:34899143 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"We report on 2 cousins, a girl and a boy, born to first-cousin Lebanese parents with Hamamy syndrome, exhibiting developmental delay, intellectual disability, severe telecanthus, abnormal ears, dentinogenesis imperfecta, and bone fragility."
This later family report confirms the same syndrome core and broadens the clinical spectrum to include explicit intellectual disability and dentinogenesis imperfecta.
