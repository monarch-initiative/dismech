Wolfram syndrome

Mendelian MONDO:0018105 Pathograph 14 Monogenic Diabetes Neurodegenerative Disease

Wolfram syndrome is a rare autosomal recessive syndromic monogenic diabetes and neurodegenerative disorder defined clinically by juvenile-onset insulin-deficient diabetes mellitus, optic atrophy, and progressive neurological involvement with frequent sensorineural hearing impairment. Classic type 1 disease is caused by biallelic WFS1 variants, whereas type 2 disease is caused by biallelic CISD2 variants. Across the syndrome, the main mechanistic theme is failure of endoplasmic-reticulum and mitochondria- associated membrane homeostasis, especially calcium handling, with downstream ER stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, selective pancreatic beta-cell vulnerability, optic pathway degeneration, and progressive brain involvement.

Wolfram syndrome type 2 Not Yet Curated MONDO:0011502
CISD2-related Wolfram syndrome with overlapping diabetes, optic atrophy, and hearing impairment, but typically without diabetes insipidus and with greater bleeding tendency and peptic ulcer disease.

PMID:35055657 SUPPORT Other
