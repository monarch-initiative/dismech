Arrhythmogenic Cardiomyopathy

Genetic MONDO:0016587 Pathograph 7 Cardiomyopathy Cardiovascular Disease Genetic Disorder

Arrhythmogenic cardiomyopathy (ACM) is a heritable myocardial disease characterized by electrical instability, ventricular arrhythmias, sudden cardiac death risk, and progressive fibrofatty replacement of ventricular myocardium. Although historically described as a predominantly right ventricular disorder, ACM is now recognized as a phenotypic spectrum that includes right-dominant, biventricular, and left-dominant forms. Pathogenic variants most commonly affect desmosomal genes, but non-desmosomal genes involved in cytoskeletal organization, nuclear architecture, and excitation-contraction coupling also contribute to disease susceptibility and phenotypic heterogeneity.
