Brachyolmia

Mendelian MONDO:0015262 Pathograph 9 Skeletal dysplasia Platyspondyly syndrome

Brachyolmia (Greek: 'short trunk') is a clinically and genetically heterogeneous group of skeletal dysplasias characterized by disproportionate short-trunk short stature, generalized platyspondyly, and relatively minor long-bone abnormalities. At least three genetic forms are recognized: autosomal dominant brachyolmia caused by gain-of-function mutations in TRPV4, autosomal recessive brachyolmia caused by loss-of-function mutations in PAPSS2, and brachyolmia with amelogenesis imperfecta caused by LTBP3 mutations. The TRPV4 form results from constitutive activation of a mechanosensitive calcium-permeable cation channel expressed in growth plate chondrocytes. The PAPSS2 form disrupts 3'-phosphoadenosine 5'-phosphosulfate biosynthesis, impairing glycosaminoglycan sulfation essential for cartilage extracellular matrix integrity. The LTBP3 form disrupts TGF-beta signaling required for both skeletal and dental development. All forms share the hallmark of platyspondyly with predominantly axial skeletal involvement, though the severity and extra-skeletal features vary by genotype.

Subtypes

3
Autosomal Dominant Brachyolmia (TRPV4)
Caused by gain-of-function mutations in TRPV4, encoding a calcium-permeable mechanosensitive cation channel. Characterized by disproportionate short stature with short trunk, scoliosis, platyspondyly, and chronic pain. Growth is typically normal in early childhood but deteriorates with increasing spinal involvement.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:18587396 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Here, we identify a locus for an autosomal dominant form of brachyolmia on chromosome 12q24.1-12q24.2."
Establishes the autosomal dominant TRPV4 subtype and its genetic locus.
Autosomal Recessive Brachyolmia (PAPSS2)
Caused by loss-of-function mutations in PAPSS2, encoding PAPS synthase 2. Features short-trunk short stature with onset in childhood, platyspondyly with rectangular vertebral bodies and irregular endplates, precocious rib cartilage calcification, short femoral neck, and low serum DHEAS. Includes the historical Hobaek and Toledo types.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:22791835 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"We identified PAPSS2 as the disease gene for an AR brachyolmia."
Identifies PAPSS2 as the causal gene for autosomal recessive brachyolmia.
Brachyolmia with Amelogenesis Imperfecta (LTBP3)
Caused by recessive mutations in LTBP3. Features brachyolmia combined with hypoplastic amelogenesis imperfecta with thin to absent dental enamel. LTBP3 encodes latent TGF-beta binding protein 3, involved in TGF-beta signaling required for both skeletal and dental development.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:25669657 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"recessive hypomorphic mutations including deletion, nonsense and splice mutations, in the LTBP3 gene, which is involved in the TGF-beta signaling pathway"
Identifies LTBP3 mutations as the cause of brachyolmia with amelogenesis imperfecta.

Pathophysiology

9
TRPV4 constitutive channel activation
Gain-of-function mutations in TRPV4 (R616Q, V620I) cause constitutive activation of a calcium-permeable mechanosensitive cation channel with cartilage-selective expression. The R616Q mutation disrupts a cholesterol-binding motif, leading to loss of lipid raft localization. Both mutations increase constitutive channel activity and elevate responses to mechanical stimulation and agonist stimulation.

Aberrant calcium signaling in growth plate chondrocytes
Constitutively active TRPV4 channels in growth plate chondrocytes lead to aberrant calcium influx. TRPV4 is selectively expressed in cartilage, and the gain-of-function mutations perturb calcium homeostasis in the cells responsible for endochondral ossification of the vertebral column.

Deficient PAPS biosynthesis
Loss-of-function mutations in PAPSS2 impair the biosynthesis of PAPS (3'-phosphoadenosine 5'-phosphosulfate), the universal sulfate donor required for all sulfation reactions. In vitro enzyme assays confirm that both frameshift and missense PAPSS2 mutations result in loss of enzymatic function.

Cartilage glycosaminoglycan undersulfation
Deficient PAPS supply impairs sulfation of chondroitin sulfate proteoglycans in cartilage extracellular matrix. Undersulfated proteoglycans compromise the structural integrity of growth plate cartilage and disrupt Indian hedgehog (Ihh) signaling. Ihh normally binds to chondroitin sulfate chains on aggrecan; in undersulfated cartilage, Ihh distribution is abnormal and hedgehog signaling is diminished, leading to significantly reduced chondrocyte proliferation.

LTBP3 deficiency
Recessive mutations in LTBP3 disrupt latent TGF-beta binding protein 3, which sequesters and presents latent TGF-beta for activation. LTBP3 is expressed in developing bone undergoing endochondral ossification and in differentiated ameloblasts during tooth formation.

Impaired vertebral endochondral ossification
All three brachyolmia pathways converge on impaired endochondral ossification in the vertebral growth plates. This results in generalized platyspondyly — the hallmark radiographic finding shared across all subtypes. The predominantly axial skeletal involvement distinguishes brachyolmia from more generalized spondyloepiphyseal or spondylometaphyseal dysplasias.
