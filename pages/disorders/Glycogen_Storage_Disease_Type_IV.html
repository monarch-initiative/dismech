Glycogen Storage Disease Type IV

Mendelian MONDO:0009292 Pathograph 45 Glycogen Storage Disease Disorder of Glycogen Metabolism

Glycogen storage disease type IV is an autosomal recessive GBE1-related glycogen storage disorder caused by deficient glycogen branching enzyme activity. Impaired glycogen branching produces poorly branched glycogen (polyglucosan) that accumulates in liver, skeletal muscle, heart, and nervous system tissues. The clinical continuum includes fatal perinatal and congenital neuromuscular presentations, classic progressive hepatic disease, non-progressive hepatic disease, childhood neuromuscular disease, and adult-onset adult polyglucosan body disease.
