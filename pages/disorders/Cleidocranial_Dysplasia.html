Cleidocranial Dysplasia

Mendelian MONDO:0007340 Skeletal Dysplasia

Cleidocranial dysplasia is a RUNX2-related skeletal dysplasia characterized by clavicular hypoplasia/aplasia, delayed cranial suture closure, and dental abnormalities. Pathogenesis is driven by RUNX2 haploinsufficiency with impaired osteoblast differentiation affecting intramembranous and endochondral ossification.

Pathophysiology

1
RUNX2 Haploinsufficiency and Osteoblast Differentiation Failure
RUNX2 (CBFA1) haploinsufficiency impairs osteoblast differentiation, causing defective ossification with prominent intramembranous skeletal involvement (clavicles/skull) and additional endochondral effects.
