Sandhoff Disease

Mendelian MONDO:0010006 Pathograph 17 GM2 Gangliosidosis Lysosomal Storage Disorder Neurodegenerative Disease

Sandhoff disease is a HEXB-related lysosomal storage disorder in the GM2 gangliosidosis branch. In MONDO/Monarch it is anchored as Sandhoff disease (MONDO:0010006), a child of GM2 gangliosidosis (MONDO:0017720) and a sibling, not a synonym, of Tay-Sachs disease (MONDO:0010100). Biallelic pathogenic variants in HEXB disrupt the beta subunit shared by hexosaminidase A and hexosaminidase B, leading to lysosomal ganglioside storage, neural lysosomal dysfunction, reactive gliosis, neurodegeneration, and progressive neurologic decline across infantile, juvenile, and adult forms.
