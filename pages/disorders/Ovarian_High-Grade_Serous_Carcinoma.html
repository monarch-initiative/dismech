Ovarian High-Grade Serous Carcinoma

MONDO:0005211 ovarian carcinoma

Ovarian high-grade serous carcinoma (HGSOC) is the most common and lethal subtype of epithelial ovarian cancer, accounting for approximately 70-80% of ovarian cancer deaths. It is characterized by near-universal TP53 mutations (96%), frequent BRCA1/2 deficiency (~50% including germline and somatic), and widespread homologous recombination deficiency. Current evidence supports origin from the fallopian tube secretory epithelium via serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma (STIC) precursor lesions. Treatment includes platinum-based chemotherapy, PARP inhibitors for BRCA-mutant/HRD-positive tumors, bevacizumab, and debulking surgery. TCGA analysis identified four transcriptional subtypes (Immunoreactive, Differentiated, Proliferative, Mesenchymal) and recurrent somatic mutations in NF1, BRCA1, BRCA2, RB1, and CDK12.

Subtypes

2
BRCA1/2-Mutant HGSOC
HGSOC harboring germline or somatic BRCA1 or BRCA2 mutations, representing approximately 50% of cases. These tumors exhibit homologous recombination deficiency and are particularly sensitive to platinum-based chemotherapy and PARP inhibitors. At 7-year follow-up in SOLO1, 67% of BRCA-mutant patients on maintenance olaparib were alive versus 46.5% on placebo.

Pathophysiology

4
TP53 Mutation and Loss of Function
TP53 mutations occur in virtually all HGSOC cases (96%). Both missense gain-of-function mutations and truncating loss-of-function mutations are observed. Loss of p53 tumor suppressor function leads to genomic instability, impaired DNA damage response, and uncontrolled cell proliferation. TP53 mutation is the earliest known molecular alteration in HGSOC pathogenesis.

Homologous Recombination Deficiency
Approximately 50% of HGSOC tumors have defective homologous recombination DNA repair, most commonly through BRCA1/2 mutations (germline or somatic), BRCA1 promoter methylation, or mutations in other HR pathway genes (RAD51C, RAD51D, PALB2). HRD leads to reliance on error-prone DNA repair pathways and sensitivity to platinum agents and PARP inhibitors.

Genomic Instability
HGSOC is characterized by extreme chromosomal instability including widespread copy number alterations, structural rearrangements, and aneuploidy. TCGA identified 113 significant focal DNA copy number aberrations including recurrent amplifications of CCNE1, MYC, and MECOM. This genomic instability results from combined TP53 loss and homologous recombination deficiency, and contributes to tumor heterogeneity and treatment resistance.

Fallopian Tube Origin
Evidence supports that most HGSOC arises from the secretory epithelial cells of the fallopian tube fimbria, specifically from serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma (STIC) precursor lesions, rather than from the ovarian surface epithelium as previously thought. Mathematical models imply that decades elapse from a TP53 mutation to STIC, followed by approximately 6 years for progression to HGSOC.

Treatments

4
Platinum-Based Chemotherapy MAXO:0000647
Carboplatin-paclitaxel combination is the standard first-line chemotherapy for HGSOC. Platinum sensitivity is a key prognostic factor, with initial response rates of 70-80%. Approximately 25% of patients develop platinum resistance within 6 months.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:26493647 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"High-grade serous ovarian cancer (HGSOC) accounts for 70-80% of ovarian cancer deaths, and overall survival has not changed significantly for several decades"
Bowtell et al. review establishing HGSOC treatment landscape and challenges with current chemotherapy approaches.
PARP Inhibitor Therapy MAXO:0000058
PARP inhibitors (olaparib, niraparib, rucaparib) exploit synthetic lethality in HRD-positive tumors. In the SOLO1 trial, maintenance olaparib after platinum-based chemotherapy showed a hazard ratio of 0.55 for overall survival at 7-year follow-up in BRCA-mutant patients, with 67% alive versus 46.5% on placebo.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:36082969 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"At 7 years, 67.0% of olaparib patients versus 46.5% of placebo patients were alive, and 45.3% versus 20.6%, respectively, were alive and had not received a first subsequent treatment"
SOLO1 trial 7-year follow-up demonstrating substantial overall survival benefit with maintenance olaparib in BRCA-mutant newly diagnosed advanced ovarian cancer.
Bevacizumab MAXO:0000058
Bevacizumab, an anti-VEGF monoclonal antibody, is used in combination with chemotherapy and as maintenance therapy. The GOG-0218 validation study showed that patients with poorly chemosensitive disease (unfavorable KELIM score) derive the most benefit from bevacizumab.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:36252167 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"An unfavorable KELIM score was associated with bevacizumab benefit compared with placebo (PFS: HR, 0.70; 95% CI, 0.59 to 0.82; OS: HR, 0.87; 95% CI, 0.73 to 1.03)"
GOG-0218 validation confirming KELIM score as a predictive biomarker for bevacizumab benefit in ovarian cancer.
Debulking Surgery MAXO:0000004
Primary or interval cytoreductive surgery aims to remove all visible tumor (complete gross resection). Optimal debulking to no residual disease is one of the strongest prognostic factors in HGSOC.
