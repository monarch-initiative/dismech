Partial androgen insensitivity syndrome

Mendelian MONDO:0010720 Pathograph 10 Androgen insensitivity syndrome 46,XY disorder of sex development Androgen receptor signaling disorder

Partial androgen insensitivity syndrome is an X-linked 46,XY difference of sex development caused most often by pathogenic variants in AR, the androgen receptor gene. Residual androgen receptor activity produces partial, variable target-tissue responsiveness to testosterone and dihydrotestosterone, leading to predominantly male or ambiguous external genitalia, hypospadias, cryptorchidism, gynecomastia, abnormal secondary sexual hair development, and impaired fertility.
