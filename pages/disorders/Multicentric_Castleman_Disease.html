Multicentric Castleman Disease

Complex MONDO:0019754 Lymphoproliferative Disease Autoimmune Disease

A rare lymphoproliferative disorder characterized by systemic lymphadenopathy with characteristic histopathological features including angiofollicular lymph node hyperplasia. Multicentric Castleman disease (MCD) can be driven by human herpesvirus 8 (HHV-8) infection or occur as an idiopathic form (iMCD). The pathogenesis centers on dysregulated interleukin-6 (IL-6) signaling leading to systemic inflammation, constitutional symptoms, cytopenias, and multi-organ dysfunction. IL-6 targeting with siltuximab or tocilizumab has transformed treatment outcomes.

Subtypes

2
HHV-8 Associated MCD
Form driven by human herpesvirus 8 (HHV-8/KSHV) infection, which produces viral IL-6 (vIL-6) that activates the JAK-STAT signaling pathway. More common in immunocompromised individuals, particularly those with HIV.

Confirms HHV-8 associated MCD as a distinct subtype predominantly affecting HIV-positive patients.
Idiopathic MCD (iMCD)
Form without HHV-8 infection, driven by endogenous IL-6 overproduction from an unknown trigger. Further subdivided into iMCD-TAFRO (thrombocytopenia, anasarca, fever, reticulin fibrosis, organomegaly) and iMCD-NOS (not otherwise specified).

Pathophysiology

2
IL-6 Hypersecretion and Cytokine Storm
Dysregulated overproduction of interleukin-6 drives the systemic inflammatory manifestations of MCD. In HHV-8-associated MCD, the virus produces viral IL-6 (vIL-6) that mimics human IL-6. In iMCD, the source of IL-6 overproduction remains unclear but may involve autoimmune mechanisms, paraneoplastic cytokine secretion, or non-HHV-8 viral triggers. IL-6 activates JAK-STAT3 signaling, driving B-cell proliferation, plasmacytosis, acute phase protein production, and vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) release.

Angiogenesis and Vascular Proliferation
IL-6-driven VEGF overproduction causes exuberant angiogenesis in affected lymph nodes, producing the characteristic angiofollicular hyperplasia. This vascular proliferation contributes to organomegaly and fluid retention.

Treatments

4
Siltuximab MAXO:0000058
Anti-IL-6 monoclonal antibody that directly binds and neutralizes IL-6. FDA-approved for iMCD. In the pivotal randomized trial, durable tumor and symptomatic responses occurred in 34% of siltuximab-treated patients versus 0% with placebo.
Show evidence (2 references)
PMID:25042199 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Durable tumour and symptomatic responses occurred in 18 (34%) of 53 patients in the siltuximab group and none of 26 in the placebo group (difference 34·0%, 95% CI 11·1-54·8, p=0·0012)."
Pivotal randomized controlled trial demonstrating siltuximab efficacy in iMCD.
PMID:25042199 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Siltuximab plus best supportive care was superior to best supportive care alone for patients with symptomatic multicentric Castleman's disease and well tolerated with prolonged exposure. Siltuximab is an important new treatment option for this disease."
Trial conclusion establishing siltuximab as a key treatment for MCD.
Tocilizumab MAXO:0000058
Anti-IL-6 receptor monoclonal antibody that blocks IL-6 signaling. Approved in Japan for MCD treatment. Recommended as an alternative to siltuximab when siltuximab is unavailable.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:30181172 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"The anti-interleukin-6 monoclonal antibody siltuximab (or tocilizumab, if siltuximab is not available) with or without corticosteroids is the preferred first-line therapy for iMCD."
International consensus guidelines recommend tocilizumab as alternative first-line therapy for iMCD.
Rituximab MAXO:0000058
Drug: rituximab ↗
Anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody used as first-line therapy for HHV-8 associated MCD. Depletes B cells harboring HHV-8 virus. Has converted HHV-8+ MCD from a rapidly fatal illness to a relapsing disease with markedly improved survival.
Show evidence (3 references)
PMID:21778341 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Clinical outcome and overall survival of HIV-MCD have markedly improved with rituximab-based therapies, considering rituximab-based therapies (with or without cytostatic agents) to be among the preferred first-line options in patients with HIV-MCD."
Multicenter retrospective study demonstrating improved outcomes with rituximab in HIV-associated MCD.
PMID:37288720 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Rituximab has revolutionized the treatment of Kaposi sarcoma-associated herpesvirus/human herpesvirus 8-associated multicentric Castleman disease (HHV8+ MCD), converting a rapidly fatal illness into a relapsing disease."
Confirms rituximab as transformative therapy for HHV-8+ MCD.
PMID:37288720 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Ninety-five patients (70 HIV+ and 25 HIV-) achieved complete remission (CR) after rituximab-based therapy."
Demonstrates high complete remission rates with rituximab-based therapy across both HIV+ and HIV- HHV8+ MCD patients.
Combination Chemotherapy MAXO:0000647
Adjuvant combination chemotherapy is recommended in the most severe iMCD cases, particularly when anti-IL-6 therapy alone is insufficient.
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:30181172 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"In the most severe cases, adjuvant combination chemotherapy is recommended."
International consensus guidelines recommend chemotherapy for severe refractory iMCD.
