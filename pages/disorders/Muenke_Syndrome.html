Muenke Syndrome

Mendelian MONDO:0011274 FGFR3-related craniosynostosis

Muenke syndrome is a craniosynostosis syndrome caused by a specific heterozygous mutation (P250R) in FGFR3. It is characterized by uni- or bilateral coronal craniosynostosis, midface hypoplasia, hearing loss, and brachydactyly. Unlike most other FGFR3 disorders (achondroplasia, thanatophoric dysplasia), Muenke syndrome primarily affects the skull rather than the long bones. Phenotypic expression is highly variable, with some mutation carriers being clinically unaffected.

Pathophysiology

1
FGFR3 Gain-of-Function in Cranial Sutures
The P250R mutation in FGFR3 causes altered ligand binding specificity, allowing aberrant activation by non-cognate FGF ligands. In cranial sutures, this leads to premature osteogenic differentiation and suture fusion. The mechanism differs from achondroplasia-type mutations that affect growth plate chondrocytes.
