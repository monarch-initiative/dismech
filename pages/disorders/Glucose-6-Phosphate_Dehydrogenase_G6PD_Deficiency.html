Oxidative Stress

response to oxidative stress
Show evidence (5 references)
PMID:17623517 SUPPORT
"Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD), the first and rate-limiting enzyme of the pentose phosphate pathway, is indispensable to maintenance of the cytosolic pool of NADPH and thus the cellular redox balance."
The literature confirms that G6PD deficiency disrupts NADPH levels, impairing the cell's ability to handle oxidative stress, which is consistent with the statement.
PMID:31961822 SUPPORT
"Fresh G6PD-deficient RBCs demonstrated defects in the oxidative phase of the pentose phosphate pathway. During refrigerated storage, G6PD-deficient RBCs demonstrated increased glycolysis, impaired glutathione homeostasis, and increased purine oxidation, as compared with G6PD-normal RBCs."
The study describes how G6PD deficiency leads to impaired management of oxidative stress in red blood cells due to defects in glutathione homeostasis, supporting the statement.
PMID:19769422 SUPPORT

This reference supports the idea that decreased NADPH levels in G6PD deficiency impair the ability of red blood cells to handle oxidative stress, leading to hemolysis.

review_notes: G6PD deficiency is an X-linked recessive disorder characterized by defective
  red blood cell metabolism. The G6PD enzyme catalyzes the rate-limiting step of
  the oxidative pentose phosphate pathway, producing NADPH essential for maintaining
  glutathione in the reduced state and protecting erythrocytes from oxidative damage.
  In deficient individuals, decreased NADPH leads to impaired glutathione homeostasis,
  making RBCs vulnerable to oxidant-induced hemoglobin denaturation (Heinz bodies),
  membrane lipid peroxidation, and hemolysis. The classic presentation is episodic
  hemolytic anemia triggered by oxidative stressors including infections, certain
  medications (primaquine, sulfonamides, dapsone), and fava beans. Severity ranges
  from asymptomatic carriers to life-threatening hemolytic crises. Neonatal hyperbilirubinemia
  is an important manifestation requiring prompt phototherapy to prevent kernicterus.
  Management focuses on trigger avoidance and supportive care during acute episodes.
