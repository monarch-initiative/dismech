Innate Immune Dysregulation
Dysregulated innate immune function, most reproducibly demonstrated by impaired natural killer cell cytotoxicity, forming part of the interconnected pathological responses in ME/CFS.

Natural Killer Cell Dysfunction
Reproducible deficits in natural killer cell cytotoxicity and phenotype abnormalities, representing one of the most consistent biological findings in ME/CFS. Meta-analysis of 28 studies confirms NK cytotoxicity is reduced to about half of healthy control levels.

Meta-analysis of 28 papers and 55 effector-to-target ratios quantifies the NK cell cytotoxicity deficit with a large effect size (Hedges' g = 0.96), confirming this as the most reproducible immune finding in ME/CFS.

CD8+ T-Cell Exhaustion
Transcriptional reprogramming of CD8+ T cells toward an exhausted state, with upregulation of exhaustion-associated transcription factors, altered chromatin landscape, and metabolic reprogramming in effector memory subsets. This represents a distinct mechanism from general immunometabolic dysfunction, focusing on the specific exhaustion transcriptional program.

Endothelial Dysfunction
Dysregulated endothelial function with impaired flow-mediated dilation, elevated endothelin-1 and VCAM-1, and reduced nitric oxide metabolites, leading to microcirculatory impairment and tissue hypoperfusion.

Neuroinflammation
Chronic microglial activation and blood-brain barrier disruption in the CNS. Systemic immune activation propagates to the brain via neurovascular pathways, establishing a self-sustaining neuroinflammatory cycle.

Cerebral Hypoperfusion

Neuroaxonal Injury
Elevated plasma neurofilament light chain (NfL) levels indicate neuroaxonal damage in ME/CFS, correlating with cognitive dysfunction and autonomic impairment. NfL levels explain up to 17.2% of variance in cognitive test performance and are associated with worsened parasympathetic function.

Intranasal mechanical stimulation targeting nerve endings in the nasal cavity, likely from the trigeminal nerve, to activate brainstem centers. Randomized placebo-controlled trial demonstrates symptom improvement with intranasal mechanical stimulation, with longitudinal immune monitoring revealing immunological correlates of improvement including reduced inflammation and changes in gut-homing immune cells.

Environmental Factors

2
Viral Infections
Post-infectious trigger in majority of cases
Many cases follow acute viral infections including Epstein-Barr virus, enteroviruses, and SARS-CoV-2.
Show evidence (2 references)
PMID:31330791 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Characterized by chronic, unexplained fatigue, a disabling payback following exertion ("post-exertional malaise"), and variably presenting multi-system symptoms, ME/CFS is a complex disease"
Review documents that ME/CFS commonly arises from viral infections as initial triggers.
PMID:38383456 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Post-infectious myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (PI-ME/CFS) is a disabling disorder, yet the clinical phenotype is poorly defined, the pathophysiology is unknown, and no disease-modifying treatments are available"
NIH deep phenotyping study focuses specifically on post-infectious ME/CFS, confirming viral infection as a major trigger.
SARS-CoV-2 Infection
Post-COVID condition shares many features with ME/CFS
COVID-19 has been associated with development of ME/CFS-like illness (Long COVID) with significant symptom overlap.
Show evidence (2 references)
PMID:33925784 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"twenty-five out of 29 known ME/CFS symptoms were reported by at least one selected long COVID study"
Systematic review demonstrates substantial symptom overlap between long COVID and ME/CFS.
DOI:10.3389/fneur.2022.877772 SUPPORT Human Clinical
"Long COVID (Post-COVID-19 condition) is a very recent ME/CFS-like illness arising from the single pandemic virus, SARS-CoV-2. We believe the ME/CFS-like ongoing effects of Long COVID are arising by very similar mechanisms involving neuroinflammation"
Mechanistic review proposes that Long COVID and ME/CFS share common neuroinflammatory pathways.
