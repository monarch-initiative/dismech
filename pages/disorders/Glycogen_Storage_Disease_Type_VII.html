Glycogen Storage Disease Type VII

Mendelian MONDO:0009295 Pathograph 21 glycogen storage disease

Glycogen storage disease type VII is an autosomal recessive glycogen storage disease caused by pathogenic variants in PFKM, encoding muscle phosphofructokinase. The enzymatic block impairs skeletal-muscle glycolysis and is classically associated with exertional fatigue, exercise intolerance, myotonia, muscle weakness, hemolytic anemia, hyperuricemia, and increased muscle glycogen content.
