Autosomal dominant striatal neurodegeneration

Mendelian MONDO:0000211 Pathograph 12 Neurodegenerative Disease Movement Disorder

Autosomal dominant striatal neurodegeneration is an ultra-rare adult-onset movement disorder caused by heterozygous PDE8B pathogenic variants. Reported families show slowly progressive parkinsonism with bradykinesia, rigidity, dysarthria, gait disturbance, dysphagia, dysdiadochokinesis, and characteristic striatal MRI abnormalities involving the putamen, caudate nucleus, or striatum.

PDE8B Cyclic Nucleotide Phosphodiesterase Loss
ADSN-associated heterozygous PDE8B frameshift or nonsense variants disrupt cyclic nucleotide phosphodiesterase function. PDE8B normally degrades cyclic AMP, a second messenger involved in dopamine signaling and movement control.
