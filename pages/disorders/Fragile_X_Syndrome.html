Pathophysiology

2
FMRP Deficiency
Expansion of CGG trinucleotide repeat in FMR1 gene 5' UTR beyond 200 repeats causes hypermethylation and transcriptional silencing. This results in absence of fragile X mental retardation protein (FMRP), an RNA-binding protein essential for synaptic plasticity.

Synaptic Plasticity
Show evidence (1 reference)
PMID:24346713 SUPPORT
"Most FXS cases occur due to the expansion of the CGG trinucleotide repeats in the 5' un-translated region of FMR1, which leads to hypermethylation and in turn silences the expression of FMRP"
Review confirms CGG repeat expansion leads to FMRP silencing in Fragile X syndrome.
Abnormal Synaptic Development
FMRP normally suppresses translation of many synaptic proteins. Its absence leads to excessive protein synthesis at synapses, abnormal dendritic spine morphology (long, thin spines), and impaired synaptic maturation and pruning.
