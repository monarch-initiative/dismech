Mitochondrial Complex IV (Cytochrome c Oxidase) Deficiency, Nuclear-Type

A group of autosomal recessive mitochondrial disorders caused by defects in the nuclear-encoded assembly factors and metallochaperones required to build cytochrome c oxidase (complex IV) of the respiratory chain. Loss of an assembly factor blocks complex IV biogenesis, impairing terminal electron transfer and oxidative ATP synthesis. The resulting bioenergetic failure produces lactic acidosis and preferentially injures high-energy tissues — brain (Leigh syndrome), heart (fatal infantile cardioencephalomyopathy), and skeletal muscle.

Why this grouping

Grouped on a shared biochemical lesion: each member conforms to the complex_iv_assembly_deficiency module (assembly-factor loss -> complex IV biogenesis failure -> impaired terminal electron transfer -> lactic acidosis and high-energy-tissue dysfunction). Members are kept as separate Disease entries because they differ in the specific assembly factor or metallochaperone affected (copper delivery via SCO1/SCO2, heme a synthesis via COX10/COX15, membrane insertion via COX18, holoenzyme assembly via SURF1, and the COA8 stabilizer) and in the dominant organ phenotype. The criteria are NECESSARY_AND_SUFFICIENT: complex IV assembly-factor conformance both characterizes every member and identifies further nuclear COX-assembly defects as candidate members.

MONDO alignment & provenance

The grouping concept corresponds to the MONDO "mitochondrial complex IV deficiency, nuclear-type" class. Downgraded from exactMatch to closeMatch: the grouping is a curated, partial union (18 further nuclear-type leaf classes remain uncurated; see research/grouping_mondo_gaps.md) and two members are classified by MONDO under clinical-syndrome terms outside the nuclear-type subtree.

MONDO consistency: consistent 5/7 listed members are is-a descendants of MONDO:0033885. The two exceptions (SCO2-Related Fatal Infantile Cardioencephalomyopathy -> MONDO:0011451; COX15-Related COX Deficiency -> MONDO:0014051) are genuine nuclear-encoded complex IV assembly/cofactor defects whose dismech entries map to clinical-presentation MONDO terms rather than to a "nuclear type N" node.

Members (7)

SURF1-Related Leigh Syndrome DISEASE SATISFIED
SURF1 is required for early complex IV holoenzyme assembly; its loss is the most common cause of complex IV-deficient Leigh syndrome (subacute necrotizing encephalomyelopathy). SURF1 hgnc:11474
SCO2-Related Fatal Infantile Cardioencephalomyopathy DISEASE SATISFIED
SCO2 is a copper metallochaperone for the CuA site; its loss produces a cardiac-dominant phenotype (fatal infantile hypertrophic cardioencephalomyopathy), distinguishing it from the brain-dominant SURF1 phenotype. SCO2 hgnc:10604
SCO1-Related COX Deficiency DISEASE SATISFIED
SCO1, the paralogous copper chaperone, causes a phenotype with hepatic and encephalopathic involvement, contrasting with the cardiac emphasis of SCO2. SCO1 hgnc:10603
COX10-Related COX Deficiency DISEASE SATISFIED
COX10 catalyzes the first step of heme a biosynthesis (farnesylation); defects impair heme a delivery to complex IV, with leukodystrophy and tubulopathy phenotypes. COX10 hgnc:2260
COX15-Related COX Deficiency DISEASE SATISFIED
COX15 catalyzes the final heme a synthesis step; its loss causes heme a-deficient complex IV with cardiomyopathy and Leigh-like encephalopathy. COX15 hgnc:2263
COX18-Related COX Deficiency DISEASE SATISFIED
COX18 mediates membrane insertion/export of the COX2 C-terminus; defects impair maturation of the mitochondrially encoded core subunit. COX18 hgnc:26801
COA8-Related COX Deficiency DISEASE SATISFIED
COA8 stabilizes the assembling complex IV against oxidative stress; its loss gives a complex IV deficiency with a notably variable, sometimes slowly progressive course. COA8 hgnc:20492
