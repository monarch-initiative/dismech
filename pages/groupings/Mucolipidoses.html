Mucolipidoses

The mucolipidoses are lysosomal storage disorders that combine mucopolysaccharide-like and lipid-storage features, but arise from lysosomal hydrolase trafficking, targeting, or endolysosomal membrane-transport defects rather than a single GAG-degrading enzyme deficiency. The current DisMech grouping spans GNPTAB/GNPTG GlcNAc-1-phosphotransferase defects and MCOLN1/TRPML1 channel dysfunction.

Why this grouping

Grouped as a mechanistic lysosomal-storage subgroup: members conform to the lysosomal_substrate_accumulation module but are distinguished from the mucopolysaccharidoses by failure of lysosomal hydrolase targeting/trafficking or endolysosomal ion-channel function rather than deficiency of one GAG-catabolic enzyme. They are kept as separate Disease entries because GNPTAB loss causes severe I-cell disease, GNPTG loss causes the attenuated mucolipidosis III gamma phenotype, and MCOLN1 loss causes mucolipidosis IV with prominent neurodevelopmental and ocular disease.
