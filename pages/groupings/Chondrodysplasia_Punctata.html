Chondrodysplasia Punctata (CDP)

Chondrodysplasia punctata (CDP) is a clinically defined, etiologically heterogeneous family of skeletal dysplasias unified by a single radiographic hallmark: punctate ("stippled") foci of abnormal calcification in the epiphyseal and other cartilaginous regions during infancy. Beyond that shared imaging signature the group is mechanistically diverse — it spans peroxisomal disorders (defective PTS2 matrix-protein import and single-enzyme ether-lipid / plasmalogen biosynthesis defects producing the rhizomelic forms), disorders of post-squalene cholesterol biosynthesis (e.g., X-linked dominant Conradi-Hunermann-Happle, CDPX2), vitamin-K-cycle / arylsulfatase and other brachytelephalangic forms, and chromosomal / acquired (maternal warfarin, autoimmune) phenocopies. Members therefore converge on the chondrodysplasia punctata radiographic phenotype while diverging in causal gene, metabolic pathway, inheritance, and extraskeletal features (rhizomelic vs non-rhizomelic limb shortening, cataracts, ichthyosis, developmental impairment). This grouping assembles the separately curated CDP Disease entries; it is not an attempt to recapitulate the full MONDO chondrodysplasia-punctata hierarchy.

Why this grouping

The members are lumped by a shared *radiographic* phenotype — punctate / stippled epiphyseal calcification (chondrodysplasia punctata) — which is the defining, historically diagnostic feature and the basis of the clinical "chondrodysplasia punctata" convention. They are kept as separate Disease entries because the underlying *metabolic mechanism* differs: the rhizomelic forms are peroxisomal (RCDP1 = a PEX7 PTS2 matrix-import defect; the plasmalogen-synthesis-defect form = single-enzyme ether-lipid/plasmalogen biosynthesis loss in GNPAT/AGPS/FAR1), whereas the tibial-metacarpal type is a non-rhizomelic CDP whose molecular cause is still unresolved. Grouping by the convergent radiographic phenotype makes the family navigable while preserving the metabolic distinctions that drive diagnosis, biochemical testing, and prognosis. The criteria are stated as NECESSARY: exhibiting chondrodysplasia punctata (punctate epiphyseal calcification) is entailed by membership, but that finding alone — being a nonspecific hallmark shared with cholesterol-, vitamin-K-, and warfarin-related phenocopies — does not by itself establish membership in this curated set.

MONDO alignment & provenance

skos:broadMatch MONDO:0019701 · chondrodysplasia punctata

MONDO:0019701 (chondrodysplasia punctata) is the clinical umbrella class for the CDP family. A broadMatch (rather than closeMatch) is used because this grouping currently lists only three curated members (the two rhizomelic peroxisomal forms and the tibial-metacarpal type) and does not yet cover the full MONDO subtree — the cholesterol-biosynthesis (Conradi-Hunermann-Happle / CDPX2), brachytelephalangic / X-linked recessive (arylsulfatase E), and chromosomal / acquired phenocopy descendants are not represented. The grouping is thus narrower in membership than, but semantically anchored to, this MONDO class.

MONDO consistency: consistent All three listed members are is-a descendants of MONDO:0019701 (chondrodysplasia punctata): chondrodysplasia punctata, tibial-metacarpal type (MONDO:0007322); rhizomelic chondrodysplasia punctata type 1 (MONDO:0008972); and rhizomelic chondrodysplasia punctata (MONDO:0015776). The grouping does not (yet) enumerate the remaining CDP descendants, so it is a consistent subset rather than a full cover.

Chondrodysplasia Punctata, Tibial-metacarpal Type DISEASE
Differentiating mechanism
A non-rhizomelic CDP subtype defined by neonatal calcific stippling with short tibiae, short central metacarpals, and coronal cleft vertebrae. Unlike the rhizomelic members, it is not a peroxisomal disorder: sterol, very-long-chain-fatty-acid, and phytanic-acid profiles are normal, and its molecular cause remains unresolved (no gene assertion). Distinguished clinically by the tibia-plus-metacarpal shortening pattern and generally preserved cognition.

Rhizomelic Chondrodysplasia Punctata, Plasmalogen-Synthesis Defect DISEASE
Differentiating mechanism
Autosomal recessive single-enzyme defects of ether-phospholipid (plasmalogen) biosynthesis — RCDP2 (GNPAT/DHAPAT) and RCDP3 (AGPS/alkyl-DHAP synthase) — lying downstream of peroxisomal import. They converge on the same lesion (cellular plasmalogen deficiency) and phenocopy RCDP1, but because they act below the PTS2 import step, phytanic-acid alpha-oxidation is preserved (normal phytanic acid) — the key biochemical discriminator from the PEX7 import-receptor form. RCDP3 arises from loss of AGPS (alkylglycerone-phosphate synthase), the enzyme that forms the ether bond in plasmalogen biosynthesis, converging on the same plasmalogen-deficiency lesion as the GNPAT (RCDP2) defect with preserved phytanic-acid oxidation. AGPS hgnc:327

Rhizomelic Chondrodysplasia Punctata Type 1 DISEASE
Differentiating mechanism
Autosomal recessive peroxisome biogenesis disorder caused by biallelic PEX7 loss of function. PEX7 is the cytosolic receptor for PTS2-targeted peroxisomal matrix enzymes, so its loss mislocalizes plasmalogen-synthesis enzymes (DHAPAT/GNPAT and alkyl-DHAP synthase/AGPS) *and* phytanoyl-CoA hydroxylase — producing plasmalogen deficiency together with phytanic-acid accumulation. Distinguished from the single-enzyme forms by the receptor-level (import) defect and by elevated phytanic acid. PEX7 hgnc:8860
