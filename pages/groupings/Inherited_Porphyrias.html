Inherited Porphyrias

A curated grouping of explicit inherited porphyria disease entries. Current members include the inherited porphyria umbrella entry plus acute intermittent porphyria and porphyria due to ALA dehydratase deficiency, representing the currently curated acute hepatic porphyria subtypes. Members share inherited heme-biosynthesis pathway disruption with porphyrin or porphyrin-precursor accumulation and variable neurovisceral, biochemical, hepatic, or cutaneous manifestations.

Why this grouping

Grouped as an explicit curated union of Disease entries for inherited porphyrias, not as all heme-pathway toxicities or all disorders with abnormal porphyrin biomarkers. Current standalone subtype coverage is concentrated in acute hepatic porphyrias, but the umbrella entry also documents additional hereditary hepatic, erythropoietic, and cutaneous porphyria subtypes. Future batches can add standalone hereditary coproporphyria, variegate porphyria, congenital erythropoietic porphyria, familial porphyria cutanea tarda, hepatoerythropoietic porphyria, and erythropoietic protoporphyria entries.

Members (3)

Inherited Porphyria DISEASE SATISFIED
Umbrella inherited porphyria entry spanning hereditary defects in heme-biosynthesis enzymes or ALAS2 regulation, with hepatic or erythropoietic accumulation of ALA, PBG, porphyrins, or protoporphyrin and a clinical spectrum from acute neurovisceral attacks to photosensitivity, anemia, liver dysfunction, and chronic kidney disease.
Unmet under criteria 0: Abnormal circulating porphyrin concentration.
Acute Intermittent Porphyria DISEASE SATISFIED
Acute hepatic porphyria caused by HMBS deficiency, where hepatic ALAS1 induction during triggers increases ALA and porphobilinogen production, causing acute neurovisceral attacks with abdominal pain, autonomic findings, hyponatremia, neuropathy, seizures, or psychiatric features. HMBS hgnc:4982
Unmet under criteria 0: Abnormal circulating porphyrin concentration.; Cutaneous photosensitivity.
Porphyria due to ALA Dehydratase Deficiency DISEASE SATISFIED
Ultra-rare autosomal recessive acute hepatic porphyria caused by ALAD deficiency at the porphobilinogen synthase step, producing marked ALA accumulation with neurovisceral attacks, peripheral neuropathy, muscle weakness, abnormal porphyrin profiles, and no primary blistering skin phenotype. ALAD hgnc:395
Unmet under criteria 0: Cutaneous photosensitivity.
