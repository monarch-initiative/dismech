Influenza <-> Alzheimer Disease

Comorbidity A_BEFORE_B CANDIDATE
Tentative directional trajectory: severe influenza (notably influenza with pneumonia) has been associated with later neurodegenerative disease, including Alzheimer's disease and other dementias, in biobank and cohort studies. IMPORTANT EVIDENCE CAVEAT: the Intercept essay cited "~4.5-5x dementia" and "~2.6-4.1x Alzheimer's" risk after severe influenza/pneumonia. Those specific magnitudes most plausibly trace to per-disease hazard ratios in the Levine 2023 (Neuron) biobank study, but they appear in that paper's figures/tables and could NOT be verified from the abstract; they are therefore NOT asserted here as quantitative signals. Only the verifiable qualitative abstract statements are curated. The adjacent Sipila 2021 cohort quantifies an infection->dementia association but is for ANY hospital-treated infection, not influenza specifically. Kept as CANDIDATE pending extraction of the influenza-specific HRs from full text.

H

Hypotheses

Severe influenza (especially with pneumonia) may contribute to later neurodegeneration through systemic and neuroinflammation, cerebrovascular injury, and accelerated amyloid/tau pathology; an alternative/competing explanation is reverse causation or shared frailty/susceptibility, and the influenza-specific effect size on Alzheimer's disease is not yet verified here from primary full text.
PMID:36669485 (SUPPORT)
Source: HUMAN_CLINICAL
"we identified 45 viral exposures significantly associated with increased risk of neurodegenerative disease and replicated 22 of these associations."
Supports a broader pattern of viral exposures associated with later neurodegenerative disease, of which influenza-with-pneumonia is one.
