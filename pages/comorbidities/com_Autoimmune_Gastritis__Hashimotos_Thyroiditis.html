Autoimmune Gastritis <-> Hashimotos Thyroiditis

Comorbidity BIDIRECTIONAL CANDIDATE
Thyrogastric syndrome: the frequent co-occurrence of autoimmune (atrophic) gastritis and autoimmune thyroid disease (Hashimoto thyroiditis) is the most characteristic polyautoimmune pairing for autoimmune gastritis, reflecting a shared organ-specific autoimmune susceptibility. Autoimmune gastritis also clusters with type 1 diabetes mellitus and vitiligo; those pairings could be captured as additional comorbidity entries.

Hypotheses

Shared organ-specific autoimmune susceptibility - HLA class II haplotypes plus non-HLA loci such as PTPN22 - predisposes to both autoimmune gastritis and autoimmune thyroid disease, manifesting as thyrogastric polyautoimmunity rather than one condition causing the other.
PMID:34145262 (SUPPORT)
Source: HUMAN_CLINICAL
"We conduct a genome-wide association study meta-analysis in 2166 cases and 659,516 European controls from population-based biobanks and identify genome-wide significant signals in or near the PTPN22"
The PTPN22/HLA susceptibility architecture of pernicious anemia / autoimmune gastritis is shared with other organ-specific autoimmune diseases including autoimmune thyroid disease.
