Atopic Dermatitis <-> Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

Comorbidity A_BEFORE_B CANDIDATE
Non-obvious comorbidity with mixed epidemiologic evidence. Recent cohort and Mendelian randomization studies suggest AD increases T2D risk, while systematic review results are sensitive to T2D definition.

Hypotheses

Hypothesis: chronic inflammatory activity in AD may increase systemic metabolic risk and contribute to insulin resistance and T2D.
PMID:31690388 (SUPPORT)
Source: HUMAN_CLINICAL
"Atopic dermatitis (AD), also known as atopic eczema, is a chronic relapsing inflammatory dermatosis characterized by pruritus, xerosis, and a close association with IgE mediated sensitization to aeroallergens and foods."
AD is described as a chronic inflammatory dermatosis, supporting an inflammation-based hypothesis.
Pathophysiology:
Systemic inflammatory burden: Chronic inflammatory signaling in AD could promote metabolic stress and insulin resistance that increase T2D risk.

inflammatory response type 2 immune response cytokine-mediated signaling pathway response to interleukin-4 response to interleukin-13
