### Disease Framing
- STAT5B deficiency is the established paralog-specific syndrome with severe postnatal growth hormone insensitivity plus combined immune dysregulation.
- The core mechanistic split from STAT5A is preserved: STAT5B is essential for GH-driven IGF-I production and for regulatory T-cell maintenance in humans.

### Key Pathophysiology Nodes
- Impaired GH-induced IGF-I production
- FOXP3-positive regulatory T-cell dysfunction
- Adaptive immune dysregulation

### Literature Signals
- Review and cohort literature consistently connect autosomal recessive STAT5B loss to GH insensitivity, severe short stature, and low IGF-I.
- Human Treg studies show reduced FOXP3 expression and impaired suppressive function.
- Longitudinal sibling data support B-cell hyperactivity, autoantibody production, and progressive CD8-positive memory T-cell skewing.
- Pulmonary pathology is interstitial, inflammatory, and T-cell predominant, with lymphoid interstitial pneumonia and pulmonary fibrosis reported in severe disease.
- Reported treatments include recombinant human IGF-I, combination immunosuppression, lung transplantation, and consideration of bone marrow transplantation.
