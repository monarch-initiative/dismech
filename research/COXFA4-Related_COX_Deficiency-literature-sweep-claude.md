# COXFA4-Related Cytochrome c Oxidase (Complex IV) Deficiency — Literature Sweep

**Disease:** COXFA4-Related COX Deficiency (mitochondrial complex IV deficiency,
nuclear type 21; MC4DN21)
**MONDO:** MONDO:0033656 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **OMIM phenotype:** 619065 &nbsp;|&nbsp;
**Gene:** COXFA4/NDUFA4 (HGNC:7687) &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Inheritance:** autosomal recessive

## Executive Summary

COXFA4-related Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase, COX) deficiency is an ultra-rare
autosomal recessive mitochondrial disorder caused by biallelic loss-of-function
variants in **COXFA4** (HGNC-approved current symbol; formerly **NDUFA4**). The gene
was historically misassigned as a Complex I subunit until 2012 work by Kadenbach et al.
established it as a stoichiometric 14th subunit of cytochrome c oxidase, prompting
Pitceathly and Taanman to propose the rename to **COX subunit FA4 (COXFA4)** in 2018.
Loss of the subunit destabilizes the holoenzyme, producing isolated Complex IV
deficiency with a relatively mild Leigh syndrome neurological phenotype.

The primary human disease literature is small and, based on this sweep, essentially
complete as of June 2026. It comprises **three papers** (two primary patient reports
plus one authoritative nomenclature/mechanism commentary):

1. **Pitceathly et al., 2013 (PMID:23746447, Cell Rep)** — founding report defining
   the human disease through homozygosity mapping and whole-exome sequencing in a
   consanguineous pedigree; homozygous splice donor site mutations in NDUFA4 confirmed
   by Blue-Native PAGE and immunocytochemistry.
2. **Pitceathly RDS & Taanman JW, 2018 (PMID:29636225, Trends Endocrinol Metab)** —
   authoritative opinion/commentary proposing renaming of NDUFA4 to COXFA4; cites
   stoichiometric subunit data and confirms that NDUFA4 deficiency causes severe loss
   of COX activity.
3. **Misceo et al., 2024 (PMID:38674434, Genes Basel)** — second patient report:
   4-year-old female with psychomotor delay, white-matter changes, and lactic acidosis
   (Leigh syndrome) carrying a homozygous 12.9 kb NDUFA4 deletion generated by
   Alu-mediated rearrangement; fibroblast Complex IV activity significantly reduced.

No curative therapy exists; management is supportive.

## 3. Known Unknowns / Gaps

- **Limited variant spectrum:** Only two distinct allele types are documented (splice
  donor site mutations in the founding family; homozygous deletion in the 2024 family).
  Additional pathogenic variant types, genotype-phenotype correlations, and modifier
  effects are unexplored.
- **No long-term natural history data:** Both reports describe early-childhood
  presentations; adult phenotype and disease trajectory remain unknown.
- **No treatment trials:** No disease-specific interventions or mitochondrial disease
  trials explicitly enrolling MC4DN21 patients have been identified. Supportive care
  (lactate management, avoidance of metabolic stressors) is by analogy to other
  nuclear Complex IV deficiencies.
- **Animal model fidelity:** The Pitceathly 2013 paper demonstrated COX deficiency
  in a mouse NDUFA4 knockout model, but the extent to which this recapitulates the
  human neurological and developmental phenotype has not been systematically assessed.
