> Include disease entries only when the disorder is an inherited defect of the heme-biosynthesis pathway, with pathogenic involvement of a heme-pathway gene/enzyme supported by characteristic porphyria biochemistry; recent reviews classify inherited porphyrias by primary site of accumulation as hepatic versus erythropoietic and by presentation as acute hepatic versus cutaneous (including blistering and nonblistering forms) (pqac-00000002, pqac-00000006, pqac-00000019).
>
> Require biochemical confirmation in symptomatic patients rather than clinical features alone: diagnosis should demonstrate characteristic patterns of porphyrin precursors/porphyrins in urine, feces, blood, or erythrocytes; for acute neurovisceral presentations, first-line markers are urinary/plasma ALA and PBG, and normal correctly collected/analyzed results can rule out the acute porphyrias (AIP, VP, HCP, ALAD deficiency porphyria) (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020).
>
> Acute hepatic subgroup: include inherited disorders with hepatic overproduction of ALA/PBG and attack-prone neurovisceral disease, namely AIP/HMBS, ALAD deficiency porphyria/ALAD, HCP/CPOX, and VP/PPOX; these are the disorders grouped as acute hepatic porphyrias in recent authoritative reviews (pqac-00000000, pqac-00000002, pqac-00000010).
>
> Cutaneous/erythropoietic subgroup: include inherited disorders whose dominant phenotype reflects accumulation of photoactive porphyrins or erythrocyte protoporphyrin, including CEP/UROS, EPP/FECH, X-linked protoporphyria/ALAS2, and inherited UROD-related forms such as hepatoerythropoietic porphyria; recent reviews also recognize cutaneous hepatic forms such as familial PCT/UROD, while noting that PCT overall is often sporadic (pqac-00000003, pqac-00000010, pqac-00000017, pqac-00000021).
>
> Use subtype-specific biochemical hallmarks to support entry-level curation: AIP typically shows elevated urinary PBG; ALAD deficiency porphyria shows increased ALA/total urinary porphyrins without significant PBG increase; HCP/VP show elevated urinary PBG with plasma/fecal porphyrins; CEP shows elevated urinary uroporphyrin and coproporphyrin I isomers; EPP/XLP show elevated erythrocyte protoporphyrin; PCT/HEP show elevated uroporphyrin/heptacarboxyl porphyrins with normal urinary ALA/PBG in PCT-like states (pqac-00000008, pqac-00000011, pqac-00000022, pqac-00000024).
>
> Exclude secondary porphyrin abnormalities and non-porphyria causes of abnormal porphyrin tests. Recent diagnostic guidance emphasizes that abnormalities of porphyrin accumulation/excretion are common in other disorders and that many porphyria-related markers cannot be interpreted in isolation; increases, especially coproporphyrin, may occur in hepatobiliary disease, alcohol use, renal impairment, anuria, and hemodialysis-related PCT-like states (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000023, pqac-00000025).
>
> Exclude acquired/sporadic PCT from an "Inherited Porphyrias" grouping boundary unless the curated disease entry is explicitly the inherited/familial UROD-related form. Multiple recent sources state that PCT is usually or predominantly sporadic/non-hereditary, even though familial UROD-associated forms exist (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020, pqac-00000021).
>
> Exclude toxic or phenocopy conditions that mimic acute porphyria biochemistry unless they are already curated as inherited porphyria disease entries; this follows from the evidence that non-porphyric conditions can generate overlapping biochemical patterns and that biochemical abnormalities alone do not establish inherited porphyria (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000020).
>
> Curation policy suggestion: operationalize inclusion as a curated union of disease entries with both (1) inherited germline etiology affecting a heme-pathway gene or accepted pathway-regulatory gene already recognized in porphyria literature/reviews and (2) a reproducible, subtype-consistent porphyria biochemical signature. On this basis, high-value additions beyond the current members would include HCP, VP, CEP, EPP, X-linked protoporphyria, hepatoerythropoietic porphyria, and familial PCT; CLPX-protoporphyria may be considered where a disease entry exists, but the retrieved evidence here was less detailed for its biochemical criteria than for the core subtypes (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000008, pqac-00000010).
