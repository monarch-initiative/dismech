> - Recommended module name: **Prenatal Viral Neural Progenitor Cytopathy**. This is slightly clearer than “Viral Neural Progenitor Cytopathy Module” because it makes the developmental timing explicit and centers the shared pathogenic event: fetal/developing-brain viral injury to progenitor systems rather than any one virus-specific syndrome. ZIKV remains the prototype evidence base, but CMV fits the same higher-level mechanism when fetal-brain progenitor-containing models and congenital malformation outcomes are considered (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000017).
> - Recommended scope: retain a **mechanism module**, not a disease entry. The module should capture the conserved causal chain from prenatal neurotropic viral exposure and infection of neural stem/progenitor/radial-glial lineages through innate immune/stress responses to impaired neurogenesis and congenital cortical malformation; virus-specific placental receptors, systemic manifestations, and diagnostic details should stay in conforming disease entries (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017).
> - **Core required node 1:** fetal brain exposure with **direct infection of neural progenitor systems**. This is strongly supported for ZIKV by human fetal explants and mixed neural cultures showing that “NSCs and oligodendrocytes had the highest rate of ZIKV-positive cells,” and by review evidence explicitly including radial glial cells and neuronal progenitor cells among infected cell types (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000014).
> - **Core required node 2:** **antiviral innate immune activation** in the fetal neural environment, but with cell-type asymmetry. The module should require evidence that viral sensing/IFN pathways are engaged somewhere in the relevant fetal neural system, while allowing the infected progenitor population itself to show weak or impaired responses. This distinction is now well supported by astrocyte sentinel behavior versus NSC innate-immunity failure in recent human-cell data (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000023, pqac-00000027).
> - **Core required node 3:** **viral cytopathic stress affecting progenitor fitness**, which may be instantiated by apoptosis, ER stress, mitochondrial stress, DNA-damage/cell-cycle stress, or mitotic disruption. The literature supports a shared injury node even when the exact effector differs by virus/model, so conformance should not require centrosome-specific evidence in every disease entry (pqac-00000014, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000017).
> - **Core required node 4:** **progenitor pool depletion and/or lineage diversion**. The evidence supports treating death-driven depletion and differentiation-state distortion as alternative but equivalent ways to reduce effective neurogenic output. Recent human ZIKV data particularly support a switch “from neurogenesis to astrogliogenesis,” while CMV work supports dysregulated pluripotency/axon-guidance and developmental signaling in progenitor-containing organoids (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017).
> - **Core required node 5:** **impaired neurogenesis leading to congenital cortical malformation**. Required downstream outcomes should include reduced cortical growth or organization such as microcephaly/cortical thinning/simplified gyration-spectrum malformation, while broader extracortical or systemic congenital findings should remain outside the core mechanism module (pqac-00000015, pqac-00000017).
> - **Optional branch:** **specific PRR and IFN-axis details** such as TLR3, TLR7/8, RIG-I, IRF3/7, IFN-β, or viral IFN antagonism. These are valuable specialization branches for disease entries, but current evidence does not justify making any single receptor or IFN-evasion strategy mandatory across all conforming viruses (pqac-00000018, pqac-00000019, pqac-00000023).
> - **Optional branch:** **mitotic/centrosome/cytoskeletal dysfunction** should be modeled as an important convergence route, especially for ZIKV, but not as a universal requirement. Strong evidence supports NS5-linked microtubule reorganization, centrosome/MTOC associations, and mitotic catastrophe in hNPC-related systems; however, CMV support is currently stronger for developmental signaling dysregulation than for a universally established centrosome mechanism (pqac-00000019, pqac-00000021, pqac-00000017).
> - **Optional branch:** **microglial inflammatory amplification** belongs in the module as a non-required branch. Reviews support infected/activated microglia producing IFN-α/β, TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, MCP-1, NO, and iNOS, with possible bystander neurotoxicity, but this appears to be an amplifier rather than the indispensable shared skeleton (pqac-00000015, pqac-00000013).
> - **Optional branch:** **virus-specific developmental signaling dysregulation** such as CMV-associated DYRK/SHH pathway disruption, or ZIKV-associated ciliary dysfunction/premature neurogenesis. These are useful specialization nodes for particular disease entries and may explain phenotype variation, but they should not define module conformance (pqac-00000017, pqac-00000019).
> - Boundary conditions: keep the module limited to **prenatal/neurodevelopmental cortical-malformation biology**. Exclude postnatal encephalitis-only mechanisms, adult neuropathology, purely maternal immune activation without demonstrated fetal neural infection, and congenital findings that do not pass through progenitor injury/impaired neurogenesis. Also flag preprint- and thesis-derived claims for curator verification before hard-coding them as canonical evidence, especially where abstracts/full text are the main source (pqac-00000016, pqac-00000017).
